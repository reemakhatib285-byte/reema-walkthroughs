📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Cangrelor IV bag
Cangrelor is the only INTRAVENOUS P2Y12 inhibitor — a direct-acting, reversible ATP analog. IV route gives near-immediate onset within seconds, used during PCI when oral agents are too slow or cannot be given.
⚠️ Cangrelor is IV-only and directly active (not a prodrug) — its niche is immediate intra-procedural inhibition, unlike all oral P2Y12 agents.
2. Half-life 3–5 minutes
Cangrelor has an ultra-short plasma half-life of 3–5 minutes due to rapid dephosphorylation by plasma esterases. Platelet function normalizes within ~60 minutes of stopping the infusion.
⚠️ 3–5 min half-life is cangrelor's signature — its effect is fully controllable and gone ~1 hour after stop, unlike oral agents lasting days.
3. Offset within 1 hour
Because binding is reversible and clearance is esterase-driven (not hepatic/renal), full reversal of platelet inhibition occurs within ~1 hour of discontinuation — ideal bridge to surgery or oral agents.
⚠️ Cangrelor offset (~1 h) is independent of renal/hepatic function — a key advantage in unstable or pre-surgical patients.
4. Bolus 30 µg/kg
Dosing: 30 µg/kg IV bolus immediately before PCI, then a continuous infusion. The bolus achieves rapid, near-complete P2Y12 blockade for the procedure.
⚠️ Remember 30 µg/kg bolus THEN 4 µg/kg/min infusion — bolus precedes PCI, infusion runs through it.
5. Infusion 4 µg/kg/min
After the bolus, cangrelor runs at 4 µg/kg/min for at least 2 hours or the duration of PCI, whichever is longer, maintaining universal platelet inhibition during the intervention.
⚠️ Infusion is 4 µg/kg/min for at least 2 h or the duration of PCI — both the bolus and infusion are weight-based.
6. Universal platelet inhibition
Cangrelor produces immediate, profound, and consistent P2Y12 blockade across all patients — no inter-individual variability from CYP genotypes because it is directly active and not metabolized to its active form.
⚠️ No CYP2C19 'poor metabolizer' problem with cangrelor — it sidesteps the clopidogrel variability entirely.
7. Uniquely controllable
Titratable on/off control is cangrelor's defining feature: start it for instant effect, stop it for full reversal in an hour — making it the only true 'on/off switch' antiplatelet.
⚠️ Controllability, not potency, is the cangrelor exam point — it bridges patients who cannot take or absorb oral P2Y12 agents.
8. Green light — ticagrelor anytime
Transition rule: ticagrelor (also reversible) can be started ANYTIME during or after the cangrelor infusion — no receptor competition problem because neither irreversibly occupies the site permanently.
⚠️ Ticagrelor = green light = give anytime with cangrelor; this contrasts sharply with the thienopyridine red-light rule below.
9. Red light — clopidogrel/prasugrel after stop
Critical interaction: the active metabolites of clopidogrel and prasugrel cannot bind P2Y12 while cangrelor occupies it, so they are WASTED if given during the infusion. Give them only after cangrelor is stopped (or at end of infusion).
⚠️ Irreversible thienopyridines must wait until cangrelor stops — dosing them during infusion leaves the patient unprotected once cangrelor clears.
10. P2Y12 gates snap shut
Cangrelor reversibly occupies essentially all P2Y12 receptors during infusion, blocking ADP-mediated platelet activation and aggregation immediately and completely.
⚠️ Because it physically occupies the receptor, thienopyridine active metabolites have nowhere to bind — the mechanistic basis of the red-light rule.
11. Cath lab urgency / ambulance
Indication: PCI in P2Y12-naive patients not pre-treated with an oral agent. Logic — patient rushed in with MI hasn't taken pills, so cangrelor gives immediate intra-procedural protection.
⚠️ Cangrelor's indication is the NOT-pretreated PCI patient — if oral P2Y12 was already loaded and absorbed, cangrelor adds little.
12. Stent at work
During stent deployment, immediate platelet inhibition prevents acute stent thrombosis. Cangrelor (CHAMPION PHOENIX trial) reduced peri-procedural ischemic events vs clopidogrel in PCI.
⚠️ Cangrelor bridges the gap until oral agents take effect — its benefit is peri-procedural, then you transition to an oral P2Y12 inhibitor.
13. Bleeding — main risk
As with all potent antiplatelets, bleeding is the principal adverse effect, but cangrelor's ultra-short half-life makes it uniquely manageable: simply stopping the infusion restores hemostasis within ~1 hour.
⚠️ There is no specific reversal agent needed for cangrelor — stopping the drug IS the reversal, thanks to the 3–5 min half-life.
14. Dyspnea (class cousin)
Like ticagrelor, cangrelor can cause transient dyspnea (adenosine-related class effect of direct-acting reversible P2Y12 agents). Usually mild and resolves as infusion stops.
⚠️ Dyspnea links cangrelor and ticagrelor — both directly acting reversible agents — and is absent with the prodrug thienopyridines.
