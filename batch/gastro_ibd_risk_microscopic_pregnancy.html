📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. UC house with umbrella shield
Smoking is PROTECTIVE in ulcerative colitis (the shield) — UC classically presents in EX-smokers, and quitting can precipitate or worsen UC. The umbrella = protection over the UC house.
2. Out-infection bandits raise IBD risk
Prior gastrointestinal INFECTION (infectious gastroenteritis) raises subsequent IBD risk via altered microbiome/immune priming. Antibiotic exposure and NSAIDs are other environmental risk modifiers.
3. Appendectomy surgeon (UC protective)
Appendectomy (especially before age 20 for true appendicitis) is PROTECTIVE against developing ulcerative colitis. Conversely it may slightly increase Crohn's risk. Reinforces the smoking/appendectomy = UC-protective pairing.
4. CD barn on fire + OCP packet
Smoking is HARMFUL in Crohn's (the barn burns), worsening course and recurrence. Oral CONTRACEPTIVE pills increase Crohn's disease risk (feeding the fire). Smoking cessation improves Crohn's outcomes.
5. Traveler umbrella over to CD (red X)
FALSE: smoking is NOT protective in Crohn's — the protective umbrella belongs only to UC. Carrying the smoking-shield over to the CD side is the trap; smoking harms Crohn's.
⚠️ "Smoking is protective in Crohn's disease" → wrong; smoking is harmful in Crohn's; protection applies to UC only.
6. 'Quitting smoking worsens CD' traveler (red X)
FALSE: quitting smoking does NOT worsen Crohn's — quitting HELPS Crohn's (slows progression, reduces flares/surgery). It is quitting that worsens UC, not Crohn's. Watch the direction of the trap.
⚠️ "Quitting smoking worsens Crohn's disease" → wrong; quitting helps Crohn's; quitting worsens UC.
7. Watery Diarrhea Inn (microscopic colitis)
Microscopic colitis = chronic watery NON-bloody diarrhea with a NORMAL-appearing endoscopy; diagnosis is histologic. Two subtypes (collagenous, lymphocytic); both respond to budesonide.
8. Collagenous wing 9:1 female / collagen band
Collagenous colitis: female predominance ~9:1, older women, with a THICK subepithelial collagen band on biopsy. Triggers include NSAIDs, PPIs, and beta-blockers. Treat with budesonide.
9. PPIs are a risk — innkeeper (red X)
FALSE: PPIs ARE a recognized trigger of collagenous colitis (along with NSAIDs and beta-blockers). Denying PPIs as a risk is the explicit trap.
⚠️ "PPIs are not a risk factor for collagenous colitis" → wrong; PPIs (with NSAIDs and beta-blockers) trigger it.
10. Lymphocytic wing — equal sexes / celiac / sertraline
Lymphocytic colitis: EQUAL sex distribution; associated with celiac disease and the drug sertraline. Biopsy shows increased intraepithelial lymphocytes without thick collagen band. Treat with budesonide; screen for celiac.
11. Both wings get budesonide nightcap
BOTH collagenous and lymphocytic colitis are treated with budesonide (first-line, induces and maintains remission). Remove offending drugs (NSAIDs/PPIs) and treat associated celiac as needed.
12. Pregnancy ward — sulfasalazine + folate (safe)
In pregnancy CONTINUE sulfasalazine but ADD folate (sulfasalazine impairs folate absorption → neural-tube risk). Maintaining remission protects the pregnancy; active disease is more dangerous than the drug.
13. Anti-TNF IgG1 stork crosses placenta (safe)
Anti-TNF (IgG1) is SAFE in pregnancy and usually continued; it crosses the placenta mainly in the 3rd trimester, so time live vaccines in the infant accordingly. Active disease poses greater fetal risk than the biologic.
14. Teratogen shelf MTX & JAK (red X)
Methotrexate (abortifacient/teratogen) and JAK inhibitors are CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy — stop MTX 3–6 months before conception and supplement folate. These are the agents to discontinue preconception.
⚠️ "Methotrexate and JAK inhibitors are safe to continue in pregnancy" → wrong; both are teratogenic and must be stopped before conception.
