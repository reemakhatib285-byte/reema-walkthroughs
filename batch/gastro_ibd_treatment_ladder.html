📖 Read-through — all 11 symbols
1. Staircase ladder UC vs CD
Step-up therapy: mild → 5-ASA/budesonide; flares → steroids (induction only); maintenance → AZA/6-MP, MTX; refractory/severe → biologics, cyclosporine rescue, surgery. The two paths emphasize UC-specific vs Crohn-specific agents.
2. 5-ASA pharmacist (gold star)
5-ASA (mesalamine/sulfasalazine) is the gold-standard MAINSTAY for UC — both induction and maintenance of mild–moderate disease — acting topically on colonic mucosa. Gold star = first-line for UC.
3. 5-ASA crossed on CD path (red X)
5-ASA is NOT effective for Crohn's maintenance — it does not reliably reach transmural small-bowel disease and lacks maintenance benefit in CD. A classic trap: do not use 5-ASA to maintain Crohn's remission.
⚠️ "5-ASA maintains Crohn's remission" → wrong; 5-ASA is ineffective in Crohn's, especially small-bowel disease.
4. Budesonide hiker
Ileal-release budesonide is used for mild–moderate ILEAL/right-colon Crohn's. High first-pass hepatic metabolism limits systemic steroid side effects. Induction agent, not long-term maintenance.
5. Steroids firefighter
Systemic corticosteroids INDUCE remission in flares of UC or Crohn's but are NEVER for maintenance (no mucosal healing, cumulative toxicity: osteoporosis, hyperglycemia, infection). Always taper and transition to a steroid-sparing agent.
6. AZA & 6-MP turtles
Azathioprine / 6-mercaptopurine are slow-onset (weeks–months, the turtles) STEROID-SPARING maintenance agents. Their pancreatitis is reversible on stopping; monitor for myelosuppression and hepatotoxicity.
7. TPMT star
Check TPMT (thiopurine methyltransferase) activity BEFORE starting AZA/6-MP — low/absent TPMT → toxic metabolite accumulation → severe myelosuppression. Low TPMT requires dose reduction or avoidance.
8. Cyclosporine rescue climber / colectomy tent
Severe steroid-refractory UC: IV cyclosporine (or infliximab) is RESCUE therapy to avoid emergency colectomy. Severe UC flare → IV steroids first; if no response in ~3–5 days, rescue with cyclosporine/infliximab.
9. Pouchitis antibiotics (gold star)
Antibiotics (ciprofloxacin/metronidazole) are first-line for POUCHITIS after ileal pouch–anal anastomosis in UC. Gold star = correct antibiotic indication in IBD.
10. Antibiotics crossed for active UC (red X)
Antibiotics do NOT treat active UC colitis — they are reserved for pouchitis or perianal Crohn's/abscess. Using antibiotics to treat an active UC flare is a trap.
⚠️ "Antibiotics treat active ulcerative colitis" → wrong; antibiotics are for pouchitis, not active UC.
11. Etanercept bouncer (red X)
Etanercept (soluble TNF-receptor decoy) does NOT work in IBD — it is effective only for joint disease (RA, AS, psoriatic arthritis). Effective IBD anti-TNFs are the monoclonals infliximab and adalimumab.
⚠️ "Etanercept is an effective IBD biologic" → wrong; it works for joints only, not IBD; use infliximab/adalimumab.
