📖 Read-through — all 15 symbols
1. HOCM + MVP LOUDER (top-left banner)
HOCM and mitral valve prolapse are the EXCEPTION pair: maneuvers that shrink the LV make them LOUDER, the opposite of every other murmur. A smaller left ventricular cavity worsens dynamic LVOT obstruction in HOCM and lets the mitral leaflets prolapse sooner in MVP. Memorize them together as the two that behave backwards.
⚠️ If a murmur gets LOUDER on standing or Valsalva, it is HOCM or MVP — do not call it aortic stenosis or MR, which both soften.
2. Standing, Valsalva = less preload (figures)
Standing and the strain phase of Valsalva DECREASE venous return and preload, producing a smaller LV. This is the maneuver pair that drives the whole left side of the seesaw. Reduced filling is the unifying mechanism for why most murmurs soften here.
⚠️ Valsalva RELEASE/recovery transiently increases venous return — the classic teaching effect is during the STRAIN phase, not release.
3. Hypertrophied small-cavity heart (red, angry)
With less preload the LV cavity collapses smaller. In HOCM the hypertrophied septum and reduced cavity worsen dynamic outflow obstruction, so the systolic murmur grows LOUDER. Smaller chamber size is the through-line for the HOCM/MVP exception.
⚠️ The HOCM murmur intensity tracks degree of obstruction/cavity size, NOT contractility alone — don't assume louder always means worse pump function.
4. Parachute = mitral valve prolapse
In MVP, a smaller LV from reduced preload lets the leaflets prolapse EARLIER — the systolic click and murmur move earlier and the murmur lengthens. Standing therefore makes the click-murmur complex come sooner and last longer. MVP joins HOCM as louder-on-standing.
⚠️ Standing moves the MVP click EARLIER (toward S1) and lengthens the murmur — squatting delays the click later; don't reverse the timing.
5. ALL OTHERS SOFTER
Every murmur except HOCM and MVP gets SOFTER with standing and Valsalva because reduced preload means less flow across the valve. MR, AR, AS, VSD all diminish. This is the default rule against which HOCM/MVP are the exception.
⚠️ Aortic stenosis softens with Valsalva — a maneuver-induced increase in a systolic murmur points to HOCM, not AS.
6. Thought bubble: STAND LOUDER = HOCM/MVP
The mnemonic anchor: standing (less preload) = HOCM/MVP louder; squatting (more preload) = HOCM/MVP softer. The two maneuvers are mirror images across the preload axis. Use this single bubble to derive the whole scene.
⚠️ Passive leg raise mimics squatting (raises preload), not standing — both increase venous return and soften HOCM/MVP.
7. STAND LOUDER = HOCM/MVP (center figure)
Restates that the standing position selectively augments the two exception murmurs. The seesaw fulcrum is preload: tip one way and HOCM/MVP win, tip the other and the regurgitant lesions win. Position on the seesaw equals preload state.
⚠️ Don't confuse standing with inspiration — standing changes preload globally; inspiration selectively boosts RIGHT-sided murmurs (Carvallo).
8. PRELOAD seesaw beam
Preload is the single hinge variable of the whole figure: less preload (standing/Valsalva) on the left, more preload (squat/handgrip) on the right. The direction the beam tips tells you which murmurs get louder. Frame every maneuver as 'what does it do to LV filling?'
⚠️ Handgrip's dominant effect is raising AFTERLOAD (and preload), which is why it boosts MR/AR/VSD — don't attribute handgrip purely to preload.
9. MR, AR, VSD LOUDER (top-right banner)
Squatting and handgrip INCREASE preload and afterload, enlarging the LV and driving more regurgitant/shunt flow, so MR, AR, and VSD murmurs grow LOUDER. Handgrip raises systemic resistance, increasing the regurgitant gradient across the aortic/mitral valve and the VSD shunt. These are the lesions that behave 'normally.'
⚠️ These three get louder with squatting/handgrip — if they got louder on STANDING instead, you'd be wrong: standing softens them.
10. Large dilated LV (red, enlarged)
More preload and afterload dilate the LV. The larger cavity reduces dynamic LVOT obstruction (softening HOCM) and seats the mitral leaflets better (softening MVP), while augmenting regurgitant volume in MR/AR. Cavity size is again the deciding factor.
⚠️ A bigger LV makes HOCM SOFTER, not louder — a louder murmur with squatting argues against HOCM.
11. HOCM + MVP SOFTER (upper right)
Squatting raises preload and afterload, enlarging the LV and SOFTENING both HOCM and MVP — the mirror image of their standing behavior. In MVP the click also moves LATER (toward S2) and the murmur shortens. This confirms the exception pair tracks LV cavity size in both directions.
⚠️ Squatting delays the MVP click LATER and shortens the murmur — opposite of standing; reversing this is a classic error.
12. Squat, handgrip = more preload (figures)
Squatting increases venous return (preload) AND arterial resistance (afterload); sustained handgrip mainly raises afterload. Both enlarge the LV and increase the gradient for regurgitant lesions and VSD. They are the right-side drivers of the seesaw.
⚠️ Handgrip is primarily an AFTERLOAD maneuver — it loudens MR/AR/VSD and softens HOCM/AS; don't equate it with simple volume loading only.
13. HOCM + MVP SOFTER (lower right)
Reinforces that with squat/handgrip the two exception murmurs diminish. Both maneuvers enlarge the ventricle and reduce obstruction/prolapse. Keep HOCM and MVP mentally tied so you apply the exception consistently.
⚠️ Don't isolate HOCM from MVP — they always move together; if you make one louder you must make the other louder too.
14. Inspiration → right-sided heart/lung
INSPIRATION increases venous return to the RIGHT heart, making right-sided murmurs LOUDER — this is Carvallo's sign. Tricuspid regurgitation and pulmonic murmurs intensify on inspiration; left-sided murmurs are better heard in EXPIRATION. Use the breath as the right-vs-left discriminator.
⚠️ Right-sided murmurs increase with INSPIRATION; left-sided with EXPIRATION — assigning a louder-on-inspiration murmur to the left heart is the trap.
15. Tricuspid + pulmonic valves = Carvallo
The right-sided valves — tricuspid (regurgitation) and pulmonic — are the ones augmented by inspiration. Increased inspiratory venous return raises right-heart flow, loudening these murmurs (Carvallo's sign for tricuspid regurgitation). This is independent of the preload seesaw governing left-sided lesions.
⚠️ Carvallo's sign is classically described for TRICUSPID REGURGITATION — don't attribute inspiratory augmentation to a left-sided lesion like MR.
