📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. del(13q) best
del(13q14) (loss of miR-15a/16-1 → unchecked BCL2 → cell survival) is the MOST COMMON CLL abnormality (~50%) and, when isolated, carries the BEST/most indolent prognosis with the longest survival.
⚠️ Best prognosis only when del(13q) is the SOLE abnormality — its favorable weight is overridden if del(17p)/TP53 or a complex karyotype coexists.
2. Trisomy 12
Trisomy 12 (~15%) confers an INTERMEDIATE prognosis and is associated with atypical morphology/immunophenotype and NOTCH1 mutations.
3. del(11q) adverse
del(11q22-23) deletes the ATM gene (impaired DNA-damage repair), producing BULKY lymphadenopathy and aggressive disease, often in younger patients — an ADVERSE marker. BTK inhibitors notably improve outcomes in del(11q) disease.
⚠️ del(11q) = bulky nodes + aggressive course; remember ATM is the lost gene, not TP53.
4. del(17p) worst
del(17p) deletes TP53 — the WORST prognosis, with resistance to DNA-damaging chemoimmunotherapy. Always test by FISH (and add TP53 sequencing, since TP53 mutation without 17p deletion is equally adverse).
⚠️ del(17p) AND/OR TP53 mutation both confer the worst prognosis — a normal FISH for 17p does not exclude a high-risk TP53 mutation; sequence TP53 too.
5. TP53 → no apoptosis
Loss of p53 means cells cannot trigger apoptosis after DNA damage, so they survive cytotoxic chemotherapy — the mechanistic reason del(17p)/TP53 CLL is chemoresistant and MUST be treated with targeted agents.
6. FISH before therapy
Repeat FISH (and TP53 sequencing) BEFORE EVERY line of therapy because clonal evolution can acquire del(17p)/TP53 over time, which changes drug selection (mandates BTKi/venetoclax over chemoimmunotherapy).
⚠️ A favorable FISH at diagnosis can evolve — never reuse old cytogenetics to plan a new line of treatment.
7. BTK / BCL2 for 17p
del(17p)/TP53 disease: use BTK inhibitors (ibrutinib/acalabrutinib/zanubrutinib) or the BCL2 inhibitor venetoclax — NOT chemoimmunotherapy (FCR/BR), which fails p53-deficient cells.
⚠️ Never give chemoimmunotherapy to del(17p)/TP53 CLL — it is ineffective and these patients require targeted therapy regardless of fitness.
8. IGHV mutated good
IGHV-MUTATED CLL (≥2% deviation from germline) is INDOLENT with favorable prognosis and durable responses; this is the subset that can achieve long remissions with fixed-duration chemoimmunotherapy (e.g., FCR) in TP53-intact, young, fit patients.
⚠️ "Mutated" sounds bad but is GOOD in CLL — mutated IGHV = favorable. (Counterintuitive: the opposite of the usual oncology connotation.)
9. IGHV unmutated poor
IGHV-UNMUTATED CLL (~40%) is AGGRESSIVE with shorter time-to-treatment and poorer survival, and responds poorly/short-lived to chemoimmunotherapy — favor continuous BTKi or venetoclax-based therapy.
⚠️ Unmutated IGHV = worse outcome; unlike most cancers, fewer mutations here means worse prognosis. IGHV status does not change over the disease course (test once).
10. AIHA / ITP
CLL causes AUTOIMMUNE cytopenias (warm AIHA, ITP) from immune dysregulation, not marrow replacement — treat with corticosteroids ± rituximab/IVIG, separate from CLL-directed therapy.
⚠️ Autoimmune anemia/thrombocytopenia does NOT upstage Rai — these are immune-mediated, distinct from the marrow-failure cytopenias that define Rai III/IV.
11. Rai staging ladder
Rai staging ladder: 0 = lymphocytosis only (low risk); I = + lymphadenopathy; II = + splenomegaly/hepatomegaly (intermediate); III = + anemia (Hb <11); IV = + thrombocytopenia (plt <100) (high risk). Binet (A/B/C) is the European equivalent based on number of involved areas + cytopenias.
⚠️ Stage III/IV require MARROW-FAILURE cytopenias — anemia/thrombocytopenia from AUTOIMMUNITY does not count toward advanced stage.
12. Watch and wait
Early/asymptomatic CLL (Rai 0–II without triggers) is OBSERVED ("watch and wait") — multiple trials show treating early-stage asymptomatic disease provides NO survival benefit and only adds toxicity.
⚠️ Do not treat asymptomatic early-stage CLL just because the diagnosis is made — observation is the standard of care.
13. Treatment triggers
Treat only when iwCLL indications are met: progressive marrow failure (worsening anemia/thrombocytopenia), massive/progressive/symptomatic splenomegaly or lymphadenopathy, constitutional B symptoms, symptomatic disease, autoimmune cytopenia refractory to steroids, or lymphocyte doubling time <6 months.
⚠️ A high WBC/lymphocyte count ALONE is NOT an indication to treat — even very high counts without symptoms or end-organ effects don't warrant therapy (CLL rarely causes leukostasis).
14. Richter / clonal evolution
CLL can transform (Richter) into aggressive DLBCL — suspect with rapid asymmetric nodal growth, B symptoms, and markedly elevated LDH; confirm with PET-guided excisional biopsy. Clonal evolution (esp. acquiring TP53) also drives resistance, so recheck genetics over time.
⚠️ Sudden clinical acceleration in known CLL = look for Richter (biopsy the PET-hot node), not merely CLL progression.
