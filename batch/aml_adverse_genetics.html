📖 Read-through — all 15 symbols
1. ADVERSE banner
Per ELN 2022 risk stratification, these genetic lesions define adverse-risk AML, which carries the worst overall survival (~10-20% at 5 years with chemo alone). Adverse risk is the prime indication for allogeneic HCT in first complete remission (CR1) in eligible patients, because chemotherapy alone rarely cures this group. Risk drives the entire post-remission strategy: favorable → chemo consolidation, intermediate/adverse → transplant.
⚠️ Risk group is assigned by genetics, NOT by morphology or blast count; an adverse-risk patient with 25% blasts still needs allo-HCT planning even if they achieve CR.
2. FLT3-ITD
FLT3 internal tandem duplication (ITD) is a constitutively active receptor tyrosine kinase driving proliferation; it occurs in ~25-30% of AML and confers a high relapse rate. Add midostaurin to 7+3 induction and consolidation for newly diagnosed FLT3-mutated AML (RATIFY trial); quizartinib is also approved with chemo for FLT3-ITD. High allelic ratio (≥0.5) is more strongly adverse than low ratio.
⚠️ In ELN 2022 the FLT3-ITD allelic ratio no longer changes the risk category by itself — FLT3-ITD now places the patient in the INTERMEDIATE group (high ratio is not automatically adverse); the classic teaching that high-ratio FLT3-ITD is adverse is the older ELN 2017 rule. Also, FLT3 is NOT favorable even when NPM1 is mutated.
3. R/R relapsed-refractory
In relapsed/refractory FLT3-mutated AML (ITD or TKD), gilteritinib monotherapy is the standard (ADMIRAL trial: superior survival vs salvage chemo). Distinguish from the upfront setting where midostaurin/quizartinib are given WITH chemo.
⚠️ Gilteritinib covers both FLT3-ITD and FLT3-TKD point mutations; midostaurin also covers TKD, but quizartinib is ITD-selective and does not target TKD.
4. TP53 mutation
TP53 mutation is the single worst prognostic lesion in AML — median survival ~5-9 months, high chemoresistance, and frequent association with complex/monosomal karyotype and therapy-related/secondary AML. Even allo-HCT rarely overcomes it. Often treated with hypomethylating agent (azacitidine) ± venetoclax rather than intensive 7+3 because intensive chemo offers little benefit.
⚠️ TP53-mutated AML responds poorly to standard 7+3 and relapses early even after transplant; do not assume achieving CR equals cure here.
5. High VAF
For TP53, a variant allele frequency (VAF) ≥10% and/or biallelic (multi-hit) involvement defines the adverse, chemoresistant phenotype. Multi-hit TP53 is now recognized as its own AML/MDS entity in WHO/ICC classifications.
⚠️ A low-VAF single TP53 mutation may represent clonal hematopoiesis rather than driving disease; the prognostic weight is greatest with multi-hit/high-VAF TP53.
6. -5 / -7 del
Loss of chromosome 7 (-7), del(5q)/-5, abnl(17p), and complex karyotype (≥3 unrelated abnormalities) or monosomal karyotype are all ELN adverse. These are hallmarks of myelodysplasia-related and therapy-related AML.
⚠️ -7 (monosomy 7) is adverse, but isolated trisomy 8 alone is intermediate, not adverse; do not lump all numerical abnormalities together.
7. KMT2A (MLL) 9;11
KMT2A (MLL, 11q23) rearrangements are adverse, classically t(9;11) MLLT3::KMT2A and other partner fusions; strongly linked to therapy-related AML after topoisomerase-II inhibitors (etoposide, anthracyclines).
⚠️ t(9;11) is the LEAST aggressive KMT2A partner and is sometimes labeled intermediate, but ELN groups KMT2A rearrangements as adverse overall — and do not confuse the leukemogenic t(9;11) with benign findings.
8. ASXL1
ASXL1 is a secondary-type / myelodysplasia-related mutation; its presence defines adverse risk and suggests AML arising from antecedent MDS/MPN. Part of the secondary-type mutation signature (ASXL1, BCOR, EZH2, RUNX1, SF3B1, SRSF2, STAG2, U2AF1, ZRSR2).
⚠️ Any single secondary-type mutation (e.g. ASXL1) flags the AML as myelodysplasia-related and adverse, regardless of an otherwise normal karyotype.
9. BCOR
BCOR is a secondary-type/myelodysplasia-related mutation conferring adverse risk, again pointing to a secondary or MDS-derived AML biology.
10. EZH2
EZH2 is a secondary-type adverse mutation (polycomb repressive complex 2 member), part of the myelodysplasia-related signature.
11. RUNX1
A somatic RUNX1 mutation (point mutation/indel) is adverse and is a secondary-type/myelodysplasia-related lesion. This is mechanistically and prognostically opposite to the favorable t(8;21) RUNX1::RUNX1T1 FUSION.
⚠️ Mutated RUNX1 = adverse; the t(8;21) RUNX1::RUNX1T1 translocation = favorable core-binding-factor AML. Same gene name, opposite prognosis — a classic board trap.
12. Splice (SF3B1/SRSF2/U2AF1/ZRSR2)
Spliceosome mutations (SRSF2, U2AF1, ZRSR2; SF3B1 in this context) are myelodysplasia-related secondary-type mutations conferring adverse risk and indicating MDS-derived biology.
⚠️ SF3B1 is classically the GOOD-prognosis mutation in MDS with ring sideroblasts, but in AML it falls into the adverse secondary-type group — context flips its meaning.
13. IDH
IDH1 and IDH2 mutations produce the oncometabolite 2-hydroxyglutarate, causing a hypermethylation/differentiation block. They are TARGETABLE: ivosidenib for IDH1, enasidenib (or ivosidenib) for IDH2; olutasidenib for IDH1. IDH mutation alone is intermediate risk, not adverse.
⚠️ IDH inhibitors can cause differentiation syndrome (like ATRA in APL), treated with steroids. IDH mutation is NOT adverse by itself — it is placed here as a targetable lesion, intermediate risk.
14. TKD ? panel
FLT3-TKD (tyrosine kinase domain, D835) point mutations are distinct from FLT3-ITD; their prognostic impact is less clearly adverse and they do not by themselves confer the adverse risk that ITD does. They are still targetable by midostaurin and gilteritinib (but NOT quizartinib).
⚠️ FLT3-TKD is NOT prognostically equivalent to FLT3-ITD; quizartinib does not cover TKD mutations whereas gilteritinib and midostaurin do.
15. Adverse outcome clock
Adverse-risk genetics drive short time-to-relapse and poor overall survival, which is why these patients are referred urgently for donor search and allo-HCT in CR1 rather than relying on chemotherapy consolidation alone.
⚠️ Delaying donor identification in adverse-risk AML loses the narrow CR1 window for transplant — the chance of cure falls sharply once the disease relapses.
