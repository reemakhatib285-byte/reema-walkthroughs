📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Mnemonic: 21 salt / 11 pressure / 17 no sex
Master rule for CAH: 21-hydroxylase deficiency = salt wasting; 11-beta-hydroxylase = hypertension; 17-alpha-hydroxylase = no sex steroids. All share cortisol block with high ACTH and adrenal hyperplasia.
⚠️ Mixing up which enzyme causes HTN vs salt-wasting — 21 wastes salt, 11 raises pressure.
2. 17-OHP is the master marker
Elevated 17-hydroxyprogesterone is the screening/confirmatory marker for classic 21-hydroxylase deficiency, the most common CAH (~90%). It accumulates just above the enzyme block.
⚠️ 17-OHP is diagnostic for 21-OH deficiency, NOT for 11 or 17 hydroxylase — don't apply it to the cousins.
3. 21-OH baby: salt wasting
Classic 21-hydroxylase deficiency presents in neonates with salt-wasting crisis (hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, hypotension) and virilized/ambiguous female genitalia. Low aldosterone + low cortisol, high ACTH, high androgens.
⚠️ Salt-wasting newborn with hyperkalemia + hyponatremia = 21-OH; do not confuse with pyloric stenosis (which gives hypochloremic alkalosis).
4. 21-OH: low aldosterone, high K
In 21-OH deficiency, aldosterone is low so renin rises and potassium climbs (hyperkalemia). Volume depletion and hypotension follow. Treat with glucocorticoid + mineralocorticoid (fludrocortisone) + salt.
⚠️ High renin + hyperkalemia distinguishes salt-wasting 21-OH from the hypertensive 11/17 forms (which have low renin).
5. Nonclassic 21-OH toddler/teen
Nonclassic 21-OH deficiency presents later (childhood–young adult) with hirsutism, acne, precocious puberty, or PCOS-like infertility — not salt wasting. Cortisol is adequate; only androgens are mildly high.
⚠️ Nonclassic 21-OH mimics PCOS — screen with 17-OHP; it does NOT cause salt-wasting crises.
6. 11-beta-OH: hypertension
11-beta-hydroxylase deficiency blocks cortisol/aldosterone but accumulates 11-deoxycorticosterone (DOC), a mineralocorticoid → hypertension and hypokalemia. Androgens still high → virilization.
⚠️ 11-OH causes HYPERtension with low K (from DOC), opposite of 21-OH's salt wasting — and still virilizes.
7. 11-OH: DOC drives HTN
The hypertension in 11-beta-OH deficiency comes from DOC (11-deoxycorticosterone), which has aldosterone-like activity. This suppresses renin and aldosterone yet still retains salt.
⚠️ HTN here is DOC-mediated, not aldosterone — aldosterone is actually low, distinguishing it from primary aldosteronism.
8. 17-alpha-OH teen: no sex steroids
17-alpha-hydroxylase deficiency blocks both cortisol and all sex steroids → no puberty/ambiguous-to-absent secondary sex characteristics, plus HTN and hypokalemia from DOC/corticosterone excess.
⚠️ 17-OH = hypertension + sexual infantilism (no sex steroids); 46,XY appear phenotypically female — opposite androgen picture from 21/11.
9. 17-OH: HTN + low K
Like 11-OH, 17-OH deficiency causes mineralocorticoid-excess hypertension with hypokalemia and suppressed renin, but uniquely also has absent androgens AND estrogens.
⚠️ Both 11 and 17 cause HTN/low K; 17 is set apart by ABSENT sex steroids (delayed puberty, primary amenorrhea).
10. 3-beta-HSD cousin
3-beta-HSD deficiency blocks early steroidogenesis — low cortisol, aldosterone, and potent androgens. Males are undervirilized; females are mildly virilized by weak DHEA. Salt wasting can occur.
⚠️ 3βHSD gives UNDERvirilized males (unlike 21/11 which virilize females) due to weak DHEA accumulation.
11. POR (cytochrome P450 oxidoreductase) cousin
P450 oxidoreductase deficiency impairs multiple CYP enzymes (21, 17, aromatase) → mixed CAH picture with skeletal malformations (Antley-Bixler), maternal virilization in pregnancy, and ambiguous genitalia in both sexes.
⚠️ POR deficiency is the CAH cousin associated with SKELETAL/craniofacial malformations — a unique distinguishing feature.
12. Cortisol block → high ACTH
Every CAH variant shares a cortisol synthesis block, so ACTH rises and drives adrenal hyperplasia. What differs is the downstream pattern of mineralocorticoids and sex steroids above/below the block.
⚠️ All CAH = low cortisol + high ACTH + hyperplasia; the variants are told apart by salt, BP, and sex-steroid effects.
13. Forensic chemist (enzyme assay)
Diagnosis is by measuring the specific steroid that piles up above each block: 17-OHP for 21-OH, 11-deoxycortisol/DOC for 11-OH, and low sex steroids with high DOC for 17-OH.
⚠️ Each enzyme block has its own signature precursor — match the elevated steroid to the deficient enzyme.
14. Mineralocorticoid/androgen key table
Quick grid: 21-OH (low MC, low aldo, high K, high androgen); 11-OH (high MC via DOC, HTN, high androgen); 17-OH (high MC, HTN, low androgen). Use BP + K + androgen to localize.
⚠️ Read three axes together — BP, potassium, and androgen — to nail the enzyme; any single value can mislead.
