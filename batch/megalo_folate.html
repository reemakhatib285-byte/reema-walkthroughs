📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. Green leafy sources
Folate (folium = leaf) comes from green leafy vegetables, legumes, liver, and nuts (>100 µg/100 g in greens); daily requirement ~400 µg (600 µg in pregnancy). In the US, grains are folate-fortified, lowering dietary deficiency. Mnemonic: folate lives in foliage.
⚠️ WRONG: assuming any well-fed adult cannot be folate deficient because of fortification. Discriminator: fortification reduces but doesn't eliminate it — alcoholics, the 'tea-and-toast' elderly, pregnant women, and malabsorbers still become deficient.
2. Heat destroys folate
Folate is HEAT-LABILE — prolonged cooking/boiling destroys it, so overcooked or canned food loses much of its folate. This is a distinguishing feature from the more stable B12. Mnemonic: boil the folate away.
⚠️ WRONG: thinking diet rich in greens guarantees folate sufficiency regardless of preparation. Discriminator: heavily cooked diets can still be folate-poor; B12 is comparatively heat-stable, so cooking selectively depletes folate.
3. Small stores, fast depletion
Folate body stores are SMALL (~5-20 mg, lasting only 3-4 MONTHS), so deficiency develops within weeks-months of poor intake or malabsorption — far faster than B12 (years). Mnemonic: folate = small fridge (months); B12 = big vault (years).
⚠️ WRONG (swap trap): 'folate stores last years.' That's B12. Discriminator: a megaloblastic anemia appearing within months of a new insult (alcoholism, pregnancy, MTX) is far more likely FOLATE than B12 because folate stores deplete fast.
4. Absorbed in jejunum
Dietary folate (polyglutamate) is deconjugated to MONOGLUTAMATE and absorbed in the proximal JEJUNUM via PCFT (proton-coupled folate transporter, optimal at acidic pH ~5.5); no intrinsic factor needed. Mnemonic: folate = jejunum + PCFT; B12 = ileum + IF.
⚠️ WRONG (favorite swap): 'folate is absorbed in the terminal ileum.' That's B12. Discriminator: jejunal disease (celiac, tropical sprue) → folate deficiency; terminal-ileal disease/resection (Crohn) → B12 deficiency.
5. Poor intake / alcoholism
The classic folate causes are poor intake states: alcoholism (poor diet + impaired hepatic folate metabolism + enterohepatic loss), the elderly, and chronic illness. Alcohol is the single most common cause of folate deficiency in developed countries. Mnemonic: alcohol attacks folate on every front — intake, absorption, storage.
⚠️ WRONG: attributing an alcoholic's macrocytic anemia to B12. Discriminator: alcohol classically causes FOLATE deficiency (and direct non-megaloblastic macrocytosis); think B12 only with neuro signs, pernicious-anemia antibodies, ileal disease, or metformin.
6. Malabsorption (celiac/sprue)
Folate malabsorption occurs with proximal small-bowel disease: celiac disease, tropical sprue, and jejunal resection — all hitting the jejunal PCFT absorptive site. Mnemonic: jejunal disease starves folate (ileal disease starves B12).
⚠️ WRONG: expecting terminal-ileal Crohn to primarily cause folate deficiency. Discriminator: proximal/jejunal disease (celiac, tropical sprue) → folate; distal/ileal disease (Crohn ileitis, ileal resection) → B12. Severe diffuse small-bowel disease can deplete both.
7. Increased use — pregnancy
High-demand states deplete folate: pregnancy/lactation (need ~600 µg/day), chronic hemolysis (sickle cell — rapid RBC turnover), malignancy, and dialysis (folate is dialyzed off). Mnemonic: anything that makes cells fast (fetus, hemolysis, tumor) burns through folate.
⚠️ WRONG: not anticipating folate deficiency in a chronically hemolyzing patient (e.g., sickle cell) with a falling hemoglobin. Discriminator: high RBC turnover increases folate demand — these patients are often given prophylactic folate, and a sudden megaloblastic crisis suggests folate depletion.
8. Methotrexate (DHFR inhibitor)
Antifolate drugs cause functional folate deficiency: METHOTREXATE and TRIMETHOPRIM (and pyrimethamine) inhibit dihydrofolate reductase (DHFR), blocking THF regeneration; phenytoin impairs folate absorption/metabolism. Rescue with FOLINIC ACID (leucovorin), which is already reduced and bypasses DHFR. Mnemonic: MTX/trimethoprim/pyrimethamine block DHFR → leucovorin rescues.
⚠️ WRONG: trying to reverse methotrexate toxicity with plain FOLIC acid. Discriminator: folic acid still needs DHFR (which MTX has blocked) to become active — only FOLINIC acid (leucovorin), already reduced, bypasses the block and rescues the patient.
9. Pregnancy supplement / NTD
Treat folate deficiency with oral folic acid (~1-5 mg/day; the megaloblastic anemia corrects over weeks). PERICONCEPTIONAL folate (≥400 µg/day, 4 mg in high-risk) prevents neural-tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly) — start before conception since the tube closes by ~week 4. Mnemonic: folate before you're pregnant prevents NTDs.
⚠️ WRONG: starting folate only after a positive pregnancy test to prevent NTDs. Discriminator: the neural tube closes very early (~28 days), often before pregnancy is recognized — folate must be PERICONCEPTIONAL/started before conception to be protective.
10. Rule out B12 first
Before giving folate for any macrocytic/megaloblastic anemia, EXCLUDE B12 deficiency. Folate alone corrects the anemia but does not treat — and can worsen/unmask — B12 neurologic disease (subacute combined degeneration), which then progresses silently. Mnemonic: folate fixes the blood but hides the danger to the nerves.
⚠️ WRONG (high-yield trap): giving folate empirically for a macrocytic anemia. Discriminator: always check B12 first; folate monotherapy in occult B12 deficiency masks the CBC while subacute combined degeneration advances. Replace B12 (or co-treat) when both are uncertain.
11. DHF/THF & 1-carbon
Folate's job is to carry ONE-CARBON units for nucleotide synthesis (thymidylate/purines) and methylation. THF is the active workhorse; tissue folate is stored as POLYglutamate (trapped inside cells), absorbed as monoglutamate. Impaired DNA synthesis is why deficiency causes megaloblastic change. Mnemonic: folate = the one-carbon delivery van for DNA.
⚠️ WRONG: assuming folate and B12 act on the same biochemical step. Discriminator: folate provides the 1-carbon units for DNA; B12 additionally runs methylmalonyl-CoA mutase (myelin) — which is why only B12 deficiency causes neurologic disease.
12. Folinic acid rescue
Folinic acid (leucovorin) is a reduced folate that enters the active pool WITHOUT needing DHFR, so it rescues cells from DHFR-inhibitor toxicity (methotrexate, trimethoprim, pyrimethamine). 'Leucovorin rescue' is timed after high-dose MTX to spare normal cells. Mnemonic: leucovorin = the antidote that skips the blocked enzyme.
⚠️ WRONG: substituting folic acid for folinic acid in MTX rescue, or giving leucovorin too early (which would also rescue the tumor). Discriminator: only FOLINIC acid bypasses the DHFR block; in chemotherapy it is timed to protect normal tissue while still letting MTX act on the cancer.
