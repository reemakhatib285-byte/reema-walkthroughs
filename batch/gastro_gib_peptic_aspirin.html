📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Crater ulcer
Peptic ulcer = the most common cause of upper GI bleeding. The endoscopic appearance (Forrest classification) of the ulcer base predicts rebleeding and dictates whether endoscopic therapy is needed.
2. Visible vessel ~33% rebleed
High-risk Forrest stigmata: active spurting (Ia) ~ up to 90% rebleed, non-bleeding visible vessel (IIa) ~33-50%, adherent clot (IIb) ~20-30%. These require endoscopic hemostasis. Clean-based ulcers (III) and flat spots (IIc) are low risk -> no therapy, can feed early.
3. Combine TWO modalities
Endoscopic hemostasis = TWO modalities combined: injection (dilute epinephrine 1:10,000) PLUS a second method (thermal coaptive coagulation OR mechanical clips). Dual therapy lowers rebleeding versus any single method. Epinephrine is only the first half.
4. Epinephrine alone (RED X concept)
Epinephrine injection ALONE is insufficient; it gives transient vasoconstriction/tamponade but high rebleed rates. It must be paired with a second modality (thermal or clip). Choosing epinephrine monotherapy is a classic wrong answer.
⚠️ 'epinephrine injection alone' -> wrong; monotherapy rebleeds; must add a second modality (thermal or clip).
5. High-dose IV PPI 72h
After endoscopic therapy of a high-risk ulcer, give high-dose IV PPI for 72h (e.g., pantoprazole 80 mg bolus then 8 mg/h infusion, or intermittent high-dose). Raising intragastric pH >6 stabilizes the clot and reduces rebleeding, surgery and mortality. Intermittent high-dose dosing is non-inferior to continuous infusion.
6. Hospitalize >=3 days (gold star)
High-risk ulcers (active bleeding, visible vessel, clot) rebleed mostly within 72h, so admit and observe for at least 3 days. Low-risk clean-based ulcers can be fed and discharged early. Risk-stratify with Forrest + clinical scores (Glasgow-Blatchford 0-1 = outpatient).
7. Restart aspirin ASAP (gold star)
If aspirin is for SECONDARY cardiovascular prevention, restart it as soon as hemostasis is secured, usually within 1-3 days (continue PPI). The cardiovascular/thrombotic risk of withholding aspirin outweighs the small rebleed risk. Do not stop it long-term.
8. Secondary prevention indication
The restart rule applies to true secondary prevention (prior MI, stroke, stent, established CAD). For pure primary prevention with bleeding, aspirin can often be stopped permanently. Distinguish the indication before deciding.
9. Hemostasis confirmed
Aspirin resumption is gated on confirmed endoscopic hemostasis. Once the vessel is treated and not actively bleeding, the protective benefit of restarting outweighs rebleed risk; pair with continued PPI.
10. Go home / discharge now (RED X)
Discharging a high-risk ulcer immediately is wrong; they need >=72h observation for rebleed. Only low-risk (clean base, Glasgow-Blatchford <=1) patients may go home early.
⚠️ 'discharge home immediately' for a high-risk ulcer -> wrong; rebleeding peaks in 72h, admit >=3 days.
11. Solids now (RED X)
Allowing solids immediately after high-risk ulcer therapy is wrong; keep NPO/clear-liquids initially in case of rebleed needing re-scope or surgery. Early feeding is appropriate only for low-risk clean-based ulcers.
⚠️ 'resume solid food immediately' in a high-risk ulcer -> wrong; advance diet slowly given rebleed/re-scope risk.
12. Delay aspirin 8 weeks (RED X)
Waiting 8 weeks to restart aspirin is wrong for secondary prevention: prolonged withdrawal sharply raises MI/stroke/stent-thrombosis risk. Restart within days once hemostasis is secured, under PPI cover.
⚠️ 'delay aspirin 8 weeks' -> wrong; for secondary prevention restart within days of secured hemostasis.
13. Never restart (RED X)
Permanently stopping aspirin after a GI bleed in a secondary-prevention patient roughly doubles cardiovascular mortality. The correct answer is restart, not abandon.
⚠️ 'never restart aspirin' -> wrong; permanent withdrawal in secondary prevention increases cardiovascular death.
14. Switch to clopidogrel (RED X)
Switching aspirin to clopidogrel after a GI bleed is wrong; clopidogrel monotherapy causes as much or more GI bleeding and is not protective. Correct strategy is aspirin + PPI, not a switch.
⚠️ 'switch aspirin to clopidogrel' -> wrong; clopidogrel bleeds as much; keep aspirin + PPI.
