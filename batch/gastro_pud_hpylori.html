📖 Read-through — all 13 symbols
1. H. pylori villain
H. pylori is a gram-negative, urease-producing spiral organism that colonises gastric mucosa. Urease splits urea → ammonia, buffering acid (the basis of the urea breath test) and damaging mucosa. It is the dominant cause of peptic ulcers worldwide.
⚠️ 'Most peptic ulcers are caused by stress/spicy food' → wrong; H. pylori and NSAIDs cause the vast majority.
2. WANTED poster — its crimes
H. pylori is found in ~90% of duodenal ulcers and ~70% of gastric ulcers, and is a WHO class I carcinogen — it causes gastric adenocarcinoma AND gastric MALT lymphoma. Eradication reduces ulcer recurrence and cancer risk.
⚠️ 'H. pylori causes GERD / Barrett's / oesophageal adenocarcinoma' → wrong; if anything it's inversely associated. Its cancers are gastric (adenocarcinoma, MALT).
3. Urea breath test (gold star)
Urea breath test and stool antigen are the best NON-invasive active-infection tests and are the tests of CURE. Endoscopic biopsy/rapid urease is used when scope is already indicated.
⚠️ 'Serology is the best test to confirm active infection' → wrong; antibodies persist after cure — use breath/stool for active disease and cure.
4. Stool antigen (gold star)
Stool antigen detects active infection and confirms eradication. Like the breath test, it must be done off PPI and well after antibiotics to avoid false negatives.
⚠️ 'A negative stool antigen taken on a PPI rules out H. pylori' → wrong; PPIs cause false negatives. Stop the PPI first.
5. Clock — wait ≥4 weeks after antibiotics
Test of cure must be done at least 4 weeks after finishing antibiotics (and after bismuth), or you get false negatives from suppressed-but-not-eradicated bacteria.
⚠️ 'Confirm cure immediately after the antibiotic course' → wrong; wait ≥4 weeks or you'll falsely call it cured.
6. Clock — stop PPI 2 weeks
Hold the PPI for ~1-2 weeks before any active-infection test (breath, stool, biopsy urease) — acid suppression drives the organism into the body of the stomach and lowers its density, causing false negatives.
⚠️ 'PPIs don't affect H. pylori test accuracy' → wrong; they cause false-negative active tests. Stop them before testing.
7. Red X on serology (IgG)
IgG serology only shows past exposure; it stays positive for years after eradication, so it CANNOT confirm cure or reliably prove active infection.
⚠️ 'Repeat serology to confirm eradication' → wrong; IgG remains positive. Use breath or stool antigen for cure.
8. Quadruple-therapy strike team
Bismuth quadruple therapy (PPI + bismuth + tetracycline + metronidazole, ~14 days) is preferred first-line where clarithromycin resistance is high or after macrolide exposure. Clarithromycin triple therapy only where local resistance is low. Always confirm eradication afterwards.
⚠️ 'A single antibiotic plus a PPI eradicates H. pylori' → wrong; monotherapy fails — combination (triple/quadruple) therapy is required.
9. Gastric MALT shrinking
Gastric MALT lymphoma is H. pylori-driven. First-line treatment for early-stage, H. pylori-positive MALT is ERADICATION ALONE → regression in roughly 60-80%. (Label reads 'gyastric' — a Gemini typo for gastric.)
⚠️ 'Gastric MALT lymphoma needs chemo/radiation up front' → wrong; eradicate H. pylori first in early HP-positive disease.
10. ERADICATE HP FIRST banner (gold star)
The single rule that ties the MALT story together: in H. pylori-positive, early/localised gastric MALT, eradication is the entire first-line treatment and often curative.
⚠️ 'Always biopsy then jump to R-CHOP for any gastric lymphoma' → wrong for early HP+ MALT; eradicate first and reassess.
11. Regression meter 60-80%
Most early HP-positive MALT lymphomas regress with eradication. Non-responders, HP-negative cases, or those with t(11;18) translocation are less likely to respond and need radiation/immunochemotherapy.
⚠️ 't(11;18)-positive MALT will still regress with antibiotics' → wrong; that translocation predicts antibiotic non-response.
12. Red X on R-CHOP & imatinib
R-CHOP is for diffuse large B-cell lymphoma, not early MALT. Imatinib (a tyrosine-kinase inhibitor for CML/GIST) is a pure distractor here.
⚠️ 'Imatinib treats gastric MALT lymphoma' → wrong; it's a distractor. Early MALT = eradicate H. pylori.
13. Antrum vs body split
Pattern matters: antral-predominant gastritis raises gastrin → increased acid → DUODENAL ulcers; body/corpus-predominant gastritis causes atrophy → reduced acid → higher risk of gastric cancer and MALT.
⚠️ 'H. pylori always raises cancer risk by raising acid' → wrong; it's the body-predominant ATROPHIC (low-acid) pattern that drives gastric cancer/MALT.
