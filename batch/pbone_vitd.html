⚠️ Board traps 12
  1. 1,25-D acts via VDR — WRONG: ordering a 1,25-D (calcitriol) level to diagnose vitamin D DEFICIENCY. 1,25-D can be NORMAL or even HIGH in deficiency because secondary hyperPTH upregulates 1-alpha-hydroxylase. Deficiency is defined by a low 25(OH)D — that's the storage marker. Reserve 1,25-D for specific cases (CKD, hereditary rickets, granulomatous hypercalcemia).
  2. Gut: Ca + PO4 absorption — WRONG: assuming vitamin D, like PTH, lowers phosphate. Vitamin D raises BOTH Ca AND phosphate (gut absorption), whereas PTH raises Ca but LOWERS phosphate (renal phosphaturia). This is why vitamin D TOXICITY produces high Ca AND high phosphate, while primary hyperPTH gives high Ca with LOW phosphate.
  3. Bone: RANKL + FGF23 — WRONG: thinking vitamin D only BUILDS bone. Adequate vitamin D is needed for mineralization, but 1,25-D's direct bone action is actually pro-RESORPTIVE (via RANKL) to defend serum Ca; the bone-building benefit comes indirectly from supplying Ca/PO4 substrate for mineralization. Deficiency, not excess, causes the under-mineralized soft bone (rickets/osteomalacia).
  4. Parathyroid feedback — WRONG: forgetting that vitamin D deficiency is a major DRIVER of secondary hyperPTH. Low 1,25-D removes this brake AND lowers serum Ca, both of which raise PTH. So vitamin D deficiency classically shows HIGH PTH — a 'normal' PTH in a deficient patient may itself be inappropriate.
  5. Rickets (open plates) — WRONG: using 'rickets' and 'osteomalacia' interchangeably. RICKETS = the CHILD with OPEN growth plates (growth-plate cartilage doesn't mineralize). OSTEOMALACIA = the same biochemistry in the ADULT after growth plates have fused (defective mineralization of mature osteoid). Age/growth-plate status is the discriminator.
  6. Osteomalacia (Looser zones) — WRONG: calling diffuse bone pain with low Ca/low PO4/HIGH ALP 'osteoporosis.' Osteoporosis has NORMAL Ca, PO4, and ALP and is a quantity (low bone mass) problem; osteomalacia is a QUALITY (mineralization) problem with abnormal labs and Looser zones. The chemistry separates them.
  7. Antiproliferative role — WRONG: extrapolating these effects into claims that vitamin D supplements prevent cancer. The antiproliferative biology is real at the cellular/VDR level, but board answers should not equate it with proven clinical cancer prevention — the classic exam role of vitamin D remains mineral/bone homeostasis (and topical analogs for psoriasis).
  8. Type I rickets (1-alpha def) — WRONG: treating VDDR-I with ordinary (inactive) vitamin D2/D3. The enzyme to activate it is missing, so plain cholecalciferol won't work — give the ACTIVE form, CALCITRIOL. The intact receptor (no alopecia) is the clue that activated vitamin D will be effective.
  9. Type II rickets (VDR) + alopecia — WRONG: giving calcitriol to a VDDR-II child and expecting a response. The VDR itself is defective, so even sky-high 1,25-D can't act — therapy is high-dose/IV CALCIUM to bypass the gut absorption defect. ALOPECIA is the giveaway pointing to a VDR mutation (type II) rather than the enzyme defect (type I).
  10. Diagnose with 25(OH)D <20 — WRONG: using 1,25-D (calcitriol) to screen for or confirm deficiency. 1,25-D is tightly regulated and frequently normal/high even when stores are depleted (secondary hyperPTH drives 1-alpha-hydroxylase). Order 25(OH)D for diagnosis; reserve 1,25-D for CKD, granulomatous hypercalcemia, or suspected hereditary rickets.
  11. CKD: low 1,25-D, high FGF23 — WRONG: treating CKD bone-mineral disease with plain ergocalciferol/cholecalciferol alone. The diseased kidney can't activate it — give ACTIVE vitamin D (calcitriol or paricalcitol) PLUS phosphate binders (and cinacalcet for high PTH). Plain vitamin D only repletes 25-D stores; it won't fix the activation block.
  12. Vit D negative feedback (gut) — WRONG: concluding 'a normal 1,25-D rules out vitamin D deficiency.' Because PTH-driven compensation keeps 1,25-D normal (or high) even as 25-D falls, a normal calcitriol does NOT exclude deficiency. The storage form 25(OH)D is the only reliable diagnostic measure.
📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. 1,25-D acts via VDR
Vitamin D activation pathway: skin (7-dehydrocholesterol + UVB) and diet provide vitamin D3/D2 -> LIVER 25-hydroxylase makes 25(OH)D (calcidiol, the STORAGE form and the one you measure) -> KIDNEY 1-alpha-hydroxylase (CYP27B1, stimulated by PTH/low PO4, inhibited by FGF23/high Ca) makes 1,25(OH)2D (CALCITRIOL, the ACTIVE form). Calcitriol acts through the nuclear VDR (a transcription factor) to drive intestinal Ca/PO4 absorption.
⚠️ WRONG: ordering a 1,25-D (calcitriol) level to diagnose vitamin D DEFICIENCY. 1,25-D can be NORMAL or even HIGH in deficiency because secondary hyperPTH upregulates 1-alpha-hydroxylase. Deficiency is defined by a low 25(OH)D — that's the storage marker. Reserve 1,25-D for specific cases (CKD, hereditary rickets, granulomatous hypercalcemia).
2. Gut: Ca + PO4 absorption
The PRIMARY action of 1,25-D is to increase INTESTINAL absorption of both calcium (via TRPV6 channel and calbindin-9K) and PHOSPHATE. This is the dominant route by which active vitamin D raises serum Ca and PO4. Without it, gut Ca absorption falls to a fraction of normal.
⚠️ WRONG: assuming vitamin D, like PTH, lowers phosphate. Vitamin D raises BOTH Ca AND phosphate (gut absorption), whereas PTH raises Ca but LOWERS phosphate (renal phosphaturia). This is why vitamin D TOXICITY produces high Ca AND high phosphate, while primary hyperPTH gives high Ca with LOW phosphate.
3. Bone: RANKL + FGF23
In bone, 1,25-D promotes osteoblast expression of RANKL, which activates osteoclasts -> bone resorption that mobilizes Ca/PO4 (important at high concentrations / when gut supply is short). 1,25-D also stimulates osteocyte FGF23, providing negative feedback (FGF23 then suppresses 1-alpha-hydroxylase and promotes phosphaturia).
⚠️ WRONG: thinking vitamin D only BUILDS bone. Adequate vitamin D is needed for mineralization, but 1,25-D's direct bone action is actually pro-RESORPTIVE (via RANKL) to defend serum Ca; the bone-building benefit comes indirectly from supplying Ca/PO4 substrate for mineralization. Deficiency, not excess, causes the under-mineralized soft bone (rickets/osteomalacia).
4. Parathyroid feedback
1,25-D directly SUPPRESSES PTH gene transcription in the parathyroid (negative feedback), complementing the CaSR-mediated suppression by calcium. This is the basis for using active vitamin D analogs (calcitriol, paricalcitol) to control secondary hyperparathyroidism in CKD.
⚠️ WRONG: forgetting that vitamin D deficiency is a major DRIVER of secondary hyperPTH. Low 1,25-D removes this brake AND lowers serum Ca, both of which raise PTH. So vitamin D deficiency classically shows HIGH PTH — a 'normal' PTH in a deficient patient may itself be inappropriate.
5. Rickets (open plates)
RICKETS is defective mineralization at the OPEN growth plates of CHILDREN: widened/frayed metaphyses, bowing of long legs (genu varum/valgum), rachitic rosary (costochondral beading), frontal bossing, delayed fontanelle closure. Labs: low/normal Ca, low PO4, high ALP, high PTH, low 25(OH)D.
⚠️ WRONG: using 'rickets' and 'osteomalacia' interchangeably. RICKETS = the CHILD with OPEN growth plates (growth-plate cartilage doesn't mineralize). OSTEOMALACIA = the same biochemistry in the ADULT after growth plates have fused (defective mineralization of mature osteoid). Age/growth-plate status is the discriminator.
6. Osteomalacia (Looser zones)
OSTEOMALACIA is impaired mineralization of mature adult bone -> diffuse BONE PAIN, PROXIMAL MUSCLE WEAKNESS/myopathy (waddling gait), and LOOSER'S ZONES (pseudofractures — radiolucent bands perpendicular to the cortex, classically at the femoral neck, pubic rami, scapula). Labs: low/normal Ca, low PO4, HIGH ALP, high PTH, low 25(OH)D.
⚠️ WRONG: calling diffuse bone pain with low Ca/low PO4/HIGH ALP 'osteoporosis.' Osteoporosis has NORMAL Ca, PO4, and ALP and is a quantity (low bone mass) problem; osteomalacia is a QUALITY (mineralization) problem with abnormal labs and Looser zones. The chemistry separates them.
7. Antiproliferative role
Beyond mineral metabolism, the VDR mediates ANTIPROLIFERATIVE and pro-differentiation effects on keratinocytes, breast, prostate, and immune cells. This underlies the use of vitamin D analogs (e.g., calcipotriol) topically for PSORIASIS.
⚠️ WRONG: extrapolating these effects into claims that vitamin D supplements prevent cancer. The antiproliferative biology is real at the cellular/VDR level, but board answers should not equate it with proven clinical cancer prevention — the classic exam role of vitamin D remains mineral/bone homeostasis (and topical analogs for psoriasis).
8. Type I rickets (1-alpha def)
Vitamin-D-DEPENDENT rickets type I (VDDR-I): loss-of-function of 1-alpha-hydroxylase (CYP27B1) -> the kidney can make 25(OH)D but CANNOT convert it to active 1,25-D. Labs: NORMAL/high 25(OH)D but LOW 1,25-D, low Ca, low PO4, high PTH. Treatment is CALCITRIOL (active vitamin D), which bypasses the missing enzyme — patients respond well because the VDR is intact.
⚠️ WRONG: treating VDDR-I with ordinary (inactive) vitamin D2/D3. The enzyme to activate it is missing, so plain cholecalciferol won't work — give the ACTIVE form, CALCITRIOL. The intact receptor (no alopecia) is the clue that activated vitamin D will be effective.
9. Type II rickets (VDR) + alopecia
Vitamin-D-dependent rickets type II (VDDR-II / hereditary vitamin-D-resistant rickets): a VDR mutation causes END-ORGAN RESISTANCE to 1,25-D. Labs: VERY HIGH 1,25-D (compensatory), low Ca, low PO4, high PTH. Hallmark physical clue: ALOPECIA (often total). Calcitriol does NOT work (receptor is broken) — treat with high-dose calcium (often IV calcium infusions) and high-dose calcitriol attempts.
⚠️ WRONG: giving calcitriol to a VDDR-II child and expecting a response. The VDR itself is defective, so even sky-high 1,25-D can't act — therapy is high-dose/IV CALCIUM to bypass the gut absorption defect. ALOPECIA is the giveaway pointing to a VDR mutation (type II) rather than the enzyme defect (type I).
10. Diagnose with 25(OH)D <20
Vitamin D status is defined by serum 25(OH)D (the storage form): DEFICIENT <20 ng/mL, INSUFFICIENT 20-30 ng/mL, sufficient >30 ng/mL. Treat deficiency with ergocalciferol/cholecalciferol ~50,000 IU weekly for 6-8 weeks, then maintenance 800-2000 IU/day, plus calcium.
⚠️ WRONG: using 1,25-D (calcitriol) to screen for or confirm deficiency. 1,25-D is tightly regulated and frequently normal/high even when stores are depleted (secondary hyperPTH drives 1-alpha-hydroxylase). Order 25(OH)D for diagnosis; reserve 1,25-D for CKD, granulomatous hypercalcemia, or suspected hereditary rickets.
11. CKD: low 1,25-D, high FGF23
In CKD the kidney loses functional 1-alpha-hydroxylase AND phosphate retention raises FGF23, both of which suppress 1,25-D production -> low ACTIVE vitamin D -> reduced gut Ca absorption -> hypocalcemia -> secondary (then tertiary) hyperPTH. The picture is high phosphate, low/low-normal Ca, low 1,25-D, high PTH (CKD-MBD).
⚠️ WRONG: treating CKD bone-mineral disease with plain ergocalciferol/cholecalciferol alone. The diseased kidney can't activate it — give ACTIVE vitamin D (calcitriol or paricalcitol) PLUS phosphate binders (and cinacalcet for high PTH). Plain vitamin D only repletes 25-D stores; it won't fix the activation block.
12. Vit D negative feedback (gut)
Tie-together: vitamin D acts on GUT (main: absorb Ca/PO4), BONE (RANKL resorption + supply substrate for mineralization), and PARATHYROID (suppress PTH transcription). In deficiency, secondary hyperPTH upregulates 1-alpha-hydroxylase so 1,25-D may stay 'normal' despite depleted 25-D stores — order 25(OH)D, not 1,25-D, to diagnose.
⚠️ WRONG: concluding 'a normal 1,25-D rules out vitamin D deficiency.' Because PTH-driven compensation keeps 1,25-D normal (or high) even as 25-D falls, a normal calcitriol does NOT exclude deficiency. The storage form 25(OH)D is the only reliable diagnostic measure.
