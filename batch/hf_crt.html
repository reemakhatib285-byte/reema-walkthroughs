⚠️ Board traps 13

  2. Class I — strongest evidence — WRONG: CRT is indicated for any wide QRS regardless of morphology. Discriminator: the Class I indication specifically requires LBBB AND QRS ≥150 ms; non-LBBB or narrower QRS drops you into weaker Class II categories with far less benefit.
  3. LVEF ≤35% — WRONG: implant CRT on the first echo before optimizing meds. Discriminator: you must re-measure EF after ≥3 months of GDMT, because medical therapy alone often recovers EF above 35% and removes the indication.
  4. LBBB + QRS ≥150 — WRONG: RBBB responds as well as LBBB to CRT. Discriminator: RBBB does not produce the LV lateral-wall delay that CRT corrects, so RBBB patients derive minimal benefit — RBBB is a poor-responder pattern, not an indication.
  5. Sinus rhythm + optimal GDMT ≥3 mo — WRONG: start CRT immediately at diagnosis to 'get ahead' of the disease. Discriminator: GDMT must be optimized for ≥3 months first — many patients improve EF and symptoms enough on meds alone that they no longer meet criteria.
  6. Class II — weaker / selective — WRONG: an AF patient with low EF and wide QRS gets the same CRT benefit as a sinus-rhythm patient. Discriminator: in AF, intrinsic conduction competes with the device, so without AV node ablation BiV pacing falls below the ~95% threshold needed for benefit.
  7. QRS duration = strongest predictor — WRONG: degree of EF reduction is the best predictor of who responds to CRT. Discriminator: across trials QRS duration/morphology predicts response better than EF — a patient with EF 34% and QRS 100 ms responds worse than one with EF 35% and LBBB 160 ms.
  8. Reverse remodeling — WRONG: CRT only relieves symptoms and has no effect on survival. Discriminator: CARE-HF and COMPANION showed CRT reduces all-cause mortality, not just symptoms — reverse remodeling is a structural, prognosis-changing effect.
  9. Functional MR improves — WRONG: significant functional MR in a CRT candidate must be surgically repaired before considering CRT. Discriminator: functional MR is secondary to LV remodeling — resynchronization often improves it medically, so optimize GDMT + CRT before escalating to mitral intervention.
  10. CRT-D vs CRT-P — WRONG: every CRT patient should automatically receive a defibrillator (CRT-D). 📖 Read-through — all 13 symbols
1. CRT fixes dyssynchrony, not sudden death
CRT (cardiac resynchronization therapy) is biventricular pacing — a third lead in the coronary sinus paces the LV lateral wall simultaneously with the RV so the dyssynchronous, LBBB-delayed ventricle contracts in coordination. This improves stroke volume, reduces functional MR and drives reverse remodeling. CRT by itself does NOT prevent sudden cardiac death — that role belongs to the ICD, which is why high-SCD-risk patients get a combined CRT-D device.
⚠️ WRONG: CRT is implanted to prevent sudden cardiac death. Discriminator: CRT corrects electrical/mechanical dyssynchrony and pump function; only the defibrillator component (ICD/CRT-D) terminates VT/VF. A pacing-only CRT-P does not protect against SCD.
2. Class I — strongest evidence
CRT Class I (all must be present): LVEF ≤35% + NYHA class II–IV symptoms + LBBB morphology + QRS ≥150 ms + sinus rhythm, on optimal GDMT for ≥3 months. This is the ideal-responder bundle (think LBBB + wide QRS + low EF). Landmark trials: COMPANION, CARE-HF (mortality benefit), MADIT-CRT and RAFT (benefit even in mild NYHA I–II disease).
⚠️ WRONG: CRT is indicated for any wide QRS regardless of morphology. Discriminator: the Class I indication specifically requires LBBB AND QRS ≥150 ms; non-LBBB or narrower QRS drops you into weaker Class II categories with far less benefit.
3. LVEF ≤35%
CRT requires a depressed LVEF ≤35%, measured after ≥3 months of optimal guideline-directed medical therapy (the same EF threshold used for primary-prevention ICD). The combination of low EF + wide LBBB defines the substrate that benefits from resynchronization.
⚠️ WRONG: implant CRT on the first echo before optimizing meds. Discriminator: you must re-measure EF after ≥3 months of GDMT, because medical therapy alone often recovers EF above 35% and removes the indication.
4. LBBB + QRS ≥150
The best CRT responders are LBBB morphology with QRS ≥150 ms — the wide LBBB represents the electrical delay CRT actually corrects. Response is graded: LBBB + QRS ≥150 ms gives the largest benefit; LBBB + 120–149 ms is intermediate; non-LBBB benefits little. Women and non-ischemic cardiomyopathy tend to respond best.
⚠️ WRONG: RBBB responds as well as LBBB to CRT. Discriminator: RBBB does not produce the LV lateral-wall delay that CRT corrects, so RBBB patients derive minimal benefit — RBBB is a poor-responder pattern, not an indication.
5. Sinus rhythm + optimal GDMT ≥3 mo
Class I CRT criteria require sinus rhythm and ≥3 months of optimal GDMT (ARNI/ACEi, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i, titrated). Sinus rhythm matters because consistent biventricular capture is needed; in AF you can't reliably achieve it without rate control or AV node ablation.
⚠️ WRONG: start CRT immediately at diagnosis to 'get ahead' of the disease. Discriminator: GDMT must be optimized for ≥3 months first — many patients improve EF and symptoms enough on meds alone that they no longer meet criteria.
6. Class II — weaker / selective
Weaker (Class IIa/IIb) indications: LBBB with QRS 120–149 ms; non-LBBB morphology only if QRS ≥150 ms; and AF patients only if near-100% biventricular pacing can be guaranteed (BiV ≥95%, often requiring AV node ablation). Benefit shrinks as you move down this list.
⚠️ WRONG: an AF patient with low EF and wide QRS gets the same CRT benefit as a sinus-rhythm patient. Discriminator: in AF, intrinsic conduction competes with the device, so without AV node ablation BiV pacing falls below the ~95% threshold needed for benefit.
7. QRS duration = strongest predictor
QRS duration is the single strongest predictor of CRT benefit — the wider the QRS (especially ≥150 ms with LBBB), the greater the electrical delay CRT corrects and the better the response. This is why QRS width, not just EF, drives the indication tiers.
⚠️ WRONG: degree of EF reduction is the best predictor of who responds to CRT. Discriminator: across trials QRS duration/morphology predicts response better than EF — a patient with EF 34% and QRS 100 ms responds worse than one with EF 35% and LBBB 160 ms.
8. Reverse remodeling
CRT drives reverse remodeling: coordinated contraction reduces wall stress and LV volumes, so over months EF rises, exercise capacity and NYHA class improve, functional MR falls, and mortality + HF hospitalizations decrease (COMPANION, CARE-HF). 'Super-responders' can normalize EF.
⚠️ WRONG: CRT only relieves symptoms and has no effect on survival. Discriminator: CARE-HF and COMPANION showed CRT reduces all-cause mortality, not just symptoms — reverse remodeling is a structural, prognosis-changing effect.
9. Functional MR improves
CRT reduces functional (secondary) mitral regurgitation by resynchronizing the LV walls and papillary muscles, improving mitral leaflet coaptation and reducing the dyssynchrony-driven regurgitant volume. Less MR means lower LA pressures and less pulmonary congestion.
⚠️ WRONG: significant functional MR in a CRT candidate must be surgically repaired before considering CRT. Discriminator: functional MR is secondary to LV remodeling — resynchronization often improves it medically, so optimize GDMT + CRT before escalating to mitral intervention.
10. CRT-D vs CRT-P
CRT-D adds an ICD/defibrillator and is chosen for younger, ischemic, or higher-SCD-risk patients with good life expectancy. CRT-P is pacing only — preferred in older/frail patients, non-ischemic etiology, or limited life expectancy where defibrillator shocks add burden without benefit. The decision is a shared SCD-risk vs goals-of-care judgment.
⚠️ WRONG: every CRT patient should automatically receive a defibrillator (CRT-D). Discriminator: in elderly/frail or limited-prognosis patients, CRT-P avoids inappropriate shocks and lead complications; DANISH-type data show ICD benefit is attenuated in older non-ischemic patients.
11. Prevent pacing-induced CM
In a patient who needs frequent/chronic RV pacing AND has EF ≤35% (or is expected to pace >40% of the time), use CRT (biventricular) rather than RV-only pacing to prevent pacing-induced cardiomyopathy. Chronic RV apical pacing creates an iatrogenic LBBB-like dyssynchrony (BLOCK-HF supports CRT here). Conduction-system pacing (His/left bundle branch area pacing) is an emerging alternative.
⚠️ WRONG: chronic RV apical pacing is harmless to the failing heart. Discriminator: high-burden RV pacing induces dyssynchrony and can drop EF (pacing-induced cardiomyopathy) — anticipate it and choose CRT or conduction-system pacing in low-EF patients needing a pacemaker.
12. 3-month GDMT wait
Wait ≥3 months on optimized GDMT before implanting CRT (and before primary-prevention ICD), because medical therapy frequently recovers EF and resolves the indication. The exception: a patient who already needs pacing (complete heart block, AV block) is paced/resynchronized without waiting.
⚠️ WRONG: implant CRT at the index admission in a newly diagnosed low-EF patient with LBBB. Discriminator: unless there's a bradycardia/heart-block pacing need, you give ≥3 months of GDMT first and re-image — premature device implant is a common board distractor.
13. Avoid CRT — narrow QRS / RBBB
CRT will NOT work — and may harm — with a narrow QRS (<130, certainly <120 ms) or with RBBB lacking LBBB-criteria delay, because there is no LV dyssynchrony to correct. Echo-CRT randomized narrow-QRS (<130 ms) HF patients and found CRT INCREASED mortality. Mechanical dyssynchrony on echo is NOT an indication when the QRS is narrow.
⚠️ WRONG: narrow-QRS HF with echo-demonstrated mechanical dyssynchrony benefits from CRT. Discriminator: Echo-CRT showed harm (increased mortality) when QRS <130 ms — CRT is an ELECTRICAL-delay therapy; never implant it for narrow QRS regardless of echo dyssynchrony.
