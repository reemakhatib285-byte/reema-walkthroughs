📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. RIPE = R/I/P/E first-line
First-line active TB regimen: Rifampin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol — 2 months of all four, then 4 months of rifampin + isoniazid.
⚠️ Four drugs up front prevent resistance; never start fewer than four for active, drug-susceptible TB before DST.
2. Rifampin — RNA polymerase
Rifampin inhibits bacterial DNA-dependent RNA polymerase (rpoB). Bactericidal; key sterilizing drug. rpoB mutation = rifampin resistance, a marker for MDR-TB on Xpert.
⚠️ Rifampin is a potent CYP450 INDUCER (lowers warfarin, OCP, PI levels) and turns secretions orange — both expected, not toxic-stop signals.
3. Rifampin orange secretions
Harmless orange-red discoloration of urine, sweat, tears; permanently stains soft contacts. Rifampin can also cause thrombocytopenia and flu-like syndrome with intermittent dosing.
⚠️ Orange fluids are benign — reassure; do not stop the drug.
4. Isoniazid — mycolic acid synthesis
INH is activated by catalase-peroxidase (katG) and inhibits InhA, blocking mycolic acid synthesis in the cell wall. Bactericidal against replicating bacilli. katG mutation = INH resistance.
⚠️ INH is the CYP450 INHIBITOR (opposite of rifampin's induction) — raises phenytoin/warfarin levels.
5. Isoniazid → B6 / neuropathy
INH depletes pyridoxine (vitamin B6) → peripheral neuropathy; co-administer B6 in pregnant, diabetic, alcoholic, malnourished, uremic, and HIV patients. Also causes hepatitis and drug-induced lupus.
⚠️ Treat/prevent the neuropathy with B6 — do not discontinue INH; INH overdose seizures are also reversed with pyridoxine.
6. Pyrazinamide — acidic milieu
PZA is converted by pncA to pyrazinoic acid and is active in the acidic intracellular/caseous environment, killing dormant bacilli — this is why it shortens therapy to 6 months.
⚠️ PZA works specifically in acidic conditions; pncA mutation = PZA resistance.
7. Pyrazinamide hepatotox + uric acid
PZA is the most hepatotoxic first-line drug and raises uric acid (inhibits renal urate excretion) → arthralgias/hyperuricemia. Continue the drug for asymptomatic hyperuricemia.
⚠️ PZA hyperuricemia is usually not true gout and does not require stopping; stop only for hepatotoxicity.
8. Ethambutol — arabinosyl transferase
Ethambutol inhibits arabinosyl transferase (embB), blocking arabinogalactan synthesis in the cell wall. Bacteriostatic; added mainly to cover possible resistance until DST returns.
⚠️ embB mutation = ethambutol resistance; EMB is the weakest of the four and is dropped once susceptibility is confirmed.
9. Ethambutol → optic neuritis
Dose-dependent optic neuritis with decreased visual acuity and red-green color blindness. Check baseline and monthly visual acuity/color vision; stop permanently if it develops.
⚠️ Visual changes = ethambutol — discontinue and do not rechallenge; usually reversible if caught early.
10. Hepatotoxicity trio (I, R, P)
INH, rifampin, and pyrazinamide are all hepatotoxic. Stop all if ALT/AST >3× ULN with symptoms or >5× ULN without; reintroduce one drug at a time, rifampin first.
⚠️ Ethambutol is the non-hepatotoxic drug — its toxicity is the eye, not the liver.
11. Streptomycin — aminoglycoside
Streptomycin (alternative/second-line) is an aminoglycoside binding the 30S ribosome, inhibiting protein synthesis. Causes ototoxicity and nephrotoxicity.
⚠️ Streptomycin is absolutely CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy (fetal ototoxicity) — unlike INH/RIF/EMB which are pregnancy-safe.
12. Bactericidal vs bacteriostatic
Bactericidal first-line drugs: isoniazid, rifampin, pyrazinamide, streptomycin. Ethambutol is bacteriostatic.
⚠️ Knowing ethambutol is the lone bacteriostatic agent (and least active) is a frequent test discriminator.
13. Resistance genes (rpoB/katG/embB/pncA)
Resistance loci: rpoB (RIF), katG/inhA (INH), embB (EMB), pncA (PZA). Xpert detects rpoB to flag MDR-TB rapidly.
⚠️ MDR-TB = resistance to at least INH + RIF; isolated rifampin resistance is treated as MDR until proven otherwise.
14. Monitoring: LFTs, vision, uric acid
Baseline and serial monitoring: LFTs (all hepatotoxic drugs), monthly visual acuity/color (EMB), and uric acid/symptoms (PZA). Counsel on orange fluids (RIF).
⚠️ Match the monitoring to the drug: eyes→EMB, liver→INH/RIF/PZA, neuropathy/B6→INH, ototoxicity→streptomycin.
