📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. Cytogenetics mandatory
Bone-marrow karyotype (conventional cytogenetics on ≥20 metaphases) is mandatory at diagnosis: it confirms the Ph chromosome AND surveys the whole genome for additional clonal abnormalities (ACAs) that FISH and PCR cannot see. It also establishes the baseline against which cytogenetic response is later measured.
⚠️ Karyotype — not FISH or PCR — is the only test that detects additional cytogenetic abnormalities (+8, i(17q), double Ph, 3q26.2). These ACAs upstage to accelerated phase and change risk, so you cannot skip the marrow karyotype at diagnosis.
2. Ph t(9;22)
Demonstrating the Philadelphia chromosome — the derivative chr22 from t(9;22)(q34;q11) — establishes the diagnosis of CML in the right clinical/lab context. Found in nearly all metaphases at diagnosis; molecular confirmation of BCR-ABL1 is still required.
⚠️ Diagnosis needs BCR-ABL1 confirmation: a 'leukemoid reaction' (infection/sepsis) can mimic the CBC but is Ph-negative with a HIGH LAP score.
3. ACAs (additional abnormalities)
Additional cytogenetic abnormalities are clonal changes beyond the Ph chromosome (trisomy 8, i(17q), a second Ph, 3q26.2/EVI1). They reflect clonal evolution, predict inferior outcomes, and their presence is an accelerated-phase criterion. Only full karyotype detects them.
⚠️ ACAs are prognostically important but invisible to FISH/PCR — this is the board's favorite reason that karyotype remains mandatory despite molecular testing.
4. FISH
FISH uses fluorescent BCR and ABL1 probes to detect the fusion signal rapidly (can be done on blood, not just marrow) and confirms cryptic or variant translocations missed by karyotype. Useful for quick diagnostic confirmation.
⚠️ FISH confirms the BCR-ABL1 fusion but CANNOT detect additional cytogenetic abnormalities or measure transcript level on the International Scale — it does not replace karyotype (for ACAs) or PCR (for monitoring).
5. Quantitative PCR
Quantitative RT-PCR measures BCR-ABL1 transcript level on the International Scale (IS) and is the most sensitive test (detects ~1 leukemic cell in 100,000). It is the gold standard for monitoring response and minimal residual disease — the baseline is set at diagnosis (~100% IS) and tracked at 3/6/12 months.
⚠️ PCR is the most sensitive test and the one used for serial monitoring/MRD and milestone assessment — not karyotype. Standard primers can miss atypical transcripts (false-negative).
6. Leukocytosis
The classic CBC shows marked leukocytosis (often >100,000) with a left-shifted myeloid series displaying the FULL maturation spectrum — myelocytes, metamyelocytes, bands, neutrophils — with a characteristic 'myelocyte bulge.' Often with thrombocytosis and mild anemia. Many patients are asymptomatic at diagnosis (incidental high WBC) or present with fatigue, splenomegaly, or early satiety.
⚠️ Unlike acute leukemia, CML shows the complete maturation spectrum with relatively FEW blasts in chronic phase — a 'leukoerythroblastic' full-range left shift, not a blast-dominant picture.
7. Low LAP score
Leukocyte alkaline phosphatase (LAP, also called neutrophil ALP / NAP score) is LOW or absent in CML because the malignant neutrophils are functionally abnormal. This is a cheap discriminator from a reactive leukemoid response.
⚠️ LAP is LOW in CML but HIGH in a leukemoid reaction, infection, and other myeloproliferative neoplasms (e.g. polycythemia vera). High WBC + LOW LAP → think CML; high WBC + HIGH LAP → reactive.
8. Basophilia
Peripheral basophilia (often with eosinophilia) is a near-universal CML hallmark and a useful diagnostic clue. The degree of basophilia rises with progression — basophils ≥20% is an accelerated-phase criterion.
⚠️ Persistent unexplained basophilia should prompt a workup for CML. Basophils ≥20% specifically upstages to accelerated phase.
9. Chronic <5% blasts
At diagnosis the vast majority (~90%) of patients are in chronic phase with <10% blasts in blood and marrow (this drawing shows <5%). Chronic phase is well controlled by TKIs with near-normal life expectancy.
10. Blast % defines phase
Blast percentage in blood/marrow is THE key parameter staging the disease: chronic <10%, accelerated 10-19%, blast phase ≥20% (WHO). Always quantify blasts on the diagnostic marrow.
⚠️ Memorize the cutoffs: chronic <10%, accelerated 10-19%, blast crisis ≥20% blasts. Some older criteria use ≥30% for blast phase — know both.
11. Baseline BCR-ABL 100%
The baseline BCR-ABL1 transcript level (~100% on the International Scale) is established at diagnosis so that every subsequent log reduction can be measured against it. All response milestones (≤10% at 3mo, ≤1%/CCyR at 12mo, MMR ≤0.1%) reference this baseline.
12. MR2/CCyR ≤1%
Complete cytogenetic response (CCyR) corresponds to BCR-ABL1 ≤1% IS (a 2-log reduction, MR2) — an important early treatment goal, ideally by 12 months. The diagnostic baseline and these milestone bands frame the whole monitoring strategy.
