📖 Read-through — all 11 symbols
1. Very low risk — no testing
In patients with very low pre-test probability of CAD, no further testing is needed — testing only raises false positives. Board point: do not order stress tests reflexively in low-risk chest pain; the post-test probability barely changes and a positive result is more likely false.
⚠️ Ordering CTA or stress in low-PTP patients — the discriminator is pre-test probability, not symptom presence.
2. Coronary CTA
Coronary CT angiography is the preferred test in low-to-intermediate PTP because of its high negative predictive value — it rules OUT obstructive CAD. Excellent anatomic test; high sensitivity. Limited by coronary calcium and need for low/regular heart rate.
⚠️ Choosing CTA to assess ischemia/viability — CTA shows anatomy, not function; it cannot tell you if a stenosis is flow-limiting.
3. Exercise ECG false negative — circumflex
The 12-lead ECG poorly captures the posterolateral wall, so circumflex-territory ischemia is a classic false-negative on exercise ECG. Standard ECG has a posterolateral blind spot. Board point: a normal exercise ECG does not exclude LCx disease.
⚠️ Calling exercise ECG definitively negative — the circumflex blind spot is the discriminator behind a missed posterolateral MI.
4. Exercise ECG ≥1mm flat/downsloping ST
A positive exercise ECG is ≥1mm horizontal or downsloping ST depression. Upsloping ST depression does NOT count as positive. Used in patients who can exercise and have an interpretable baseline ECG.
⚠️ Upsloping ST depression labeled positive — only flat/downsloping ≥1mm qualifies.
5. SPECT / PET perfusion
Nuclear perfusion (SPECT thallium-201 or technetium-99m sestamibi, PET) distinguishes ischemia (reversible defect) from infarction (fixed defect). Reversible = viable ischemic tissue; fixed = scar. Adds functional/perfusion data the ECG cannot.
⚠️ Reading a fixed defect as active ischemia — fixed = scar/non-viable, reversible defect = inducible ischemia.
6. Cardiac MRI — viability & transmural extent
CMR with late gadolinium enhancement quantifies infarct size by transmural extent of scar. <50% transmural late enhancement predicts functional recovery after revascularization (viable); >50% is non-viable. Best test for viability assessment.
⚠️ Revascularizing a wall with >50% transmural LGE expecting recovery — that segment is scar and will not improve.
7. Can patient exercise? — modality choice
First decision in stress testing: can the patient exercise? If yes and ECG interpretable → exercise ECG. If ECG uninterpretable (LBBB, paced, baseline ST) → add imaging. If cannot exercise → pharmacologic stress.
⚠️ Doing exercise ECG with an uninterpretable baseline (LBBB/paced) — you must add imaging or use a perfusion/pharmacologic protocol.
8. Pharmacologic stress agents
If a patient cannot exercise, use pharmacologic stress: vasodilators (adenosine/regadenoson/dipyridamole) for perfusion imaging, or dobutamine for stress echo. Vasodilators induce coronary steal; dobutamine increases myocardial demand.
⚠️ Giving adenosine to a severe-asthma/AV-block patient — bronchospasm/heart block; dobutamine is the alternative.
9. Stress echocardiography
Dobutamine stress echo detects inducible wall-motion abnormalities and assesses contractile reserve/viability. Used when patients cannot exercise. A new regional wall-motion abnormality with stress indicates inducible ischemia.
⚠️ Expecting perfusion data from stress echo — echo shows wall motion, not perfusion; choose nuclear if perfusion is the question.
10. ISCHEMIA trial — invasive vs medical
The ISCHEMIA trial showed that in stable CAD, an initial invasive strategy gave NO reduction in death or MI versus optimal medical therapy. It tied for hard endpoints. This reframed revascularization in stable disease.
⚠️ Claiming early angiography reduces mortality in stable IHD — ISCHEMIA showed no death/MI benefit over OMT.
11. Revascularize for symptoms, not survival
Per ISCHEMIA, revascularization in stable IHD is superior for ANGINA RELIEF and quality of life but does NOT extend life. Board point: in stable IHD revascularize for refractory symptoms, not to prolong survival.
⚠️ PCI 'to extend life' in stable IHD — the indication is symptom control; left main / high-risk anatomy are the survival-benefit exceptions.
