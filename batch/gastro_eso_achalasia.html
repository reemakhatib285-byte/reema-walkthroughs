📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. Manometry = gold standard
High-resolution esophageal MANOMETRY is the diagnostic gold standard for achalasia: shows elevated LES resting pressure, failure of the LES to relax with swallowing, and absent peristalsis (aperistalsis) in the body. It defines the diagnosis when imaging is suggestive.
2. High LES pressure
Manometric hallmark: elevated LES resting pressure with incomplete relaxation (integrated relaxation pressure raised). The LES stays tonically tight, obstructing outflow at the GE junction.
3. No peristalsis
Loss of ganglion cells in the myenteric (Auerbach) plexus causes aperistalsis of the esophageal body plus the non-relaxing LES. This degeneration of inhibitory (nitric-oxide) neurons is the core mechanism.
4. Dysphagia to solids AND liquids
Achalasia causes dysphagia to BOTH solids and liquids from the start = a MOTILITY disorder. Contrast with mechanical obstruction (stricture/cancer), which gives progressive solids-then-liquids dysphagia. Also regurgitation of undigested food, weight loss.
5. LES won't relax
The non-relaxing LES creates the functional obstruction; food piles up above it and the esophagus dilates over time, giving the dilated body tapering to a tight GE junction.
6. Barium bird's-beak (suggestive only)
Barium esophagram shows the classic 'bird's-beak' tapering with a dilated proximal esophagus; it is SUGGESTIVE but not diagnostic by itself. Manometry is still required to confirm. Barium also estimates emptying for follow-up.
⚠️ 'barium bird's-beak alone confirms achalasia' -> wrong; it is suggestive only; manometry is the confirmatory gold standard.
7. Scope to exclude pseudoachalasia
Always perform upper ENDOSCOPY to exclude pseudoachalasia: a GE-junction/cardia malignancy or infiltrating tumor mimicking achalasia (also Chagas disease in endemic areas). Suspect it with rapid symptoms, older age, marked weight loss.
8. Pseudoachalasia (malignancy/Chagas)
Pseudoachalasia: secondary causes mimicking achalasia, chiefly GE-junction adenocarcinoma, and Chagas (Trypanosoma cruzi destroys myenteric plexus). Red flags: age >55, short symptom duration <6-12 months, profound weight loss. Endoscopy +/- imaging/EUS to exclude.
9. CCB / nitrates weak (RED X as best)
Calcium-channel blockers (nifedipine) and nitrates only transiently and weakly lower LES pressure; they are last-resort for patients who cannot have any procedure, NOT definitive therapy. Choosing them as best treatment is wrong.
⚠️ 'CCB/nitrates as definitive achalasia treatment' -> wrong; they are weak/temporary, reserved for non-procedure candidates.
10. Botox ~6 months
Endoscopic botulinum toxin injection into the LES relaxes it but the effect lasts only ~6-12 months and wanes with repeats. Reserved for elderly or poor surgical/dilation candidates as a temporizing measure.
11. Definitive: dilation / Heller / POEM (gold star)
Definitive therapies disrupt the LES: pneumatic balloon dilation, laparoscopic Heller myotomy (usually + partial fundoplication), or per-oral endoscopic myotomy (POEM). All carry esophageal perforation risk. POEM and Heller give the most durable relief.
12. Perforation risk
The main complication of pneumatic dilation, Heller myotomy and POEM is esophageal perforation; POEM and post-myotomy patients also get post-procedure GERD. Watch for chest pain, fever, subcutaneous emphysema after the procedure.
