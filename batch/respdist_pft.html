📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. PFT algorithm board
PFT interpretation algorithm. Step 1: is FEV1/FVC < 0.7 (or below the lower limit of normal)? YES → OBSTRUCTIVE. If NO, step 2: is FVC low? YES → possible RESTRICTION (confirm with TLC); NO → normal. Step 3 for obstruction: assess severity by FEV1 % predicted and check bronchodilator reversibility.
⚠️ ALWAYS start with the FEV1/FVC RATIO, not the absolute FEV1 — a low FEV1 alone does not define obstruction (FEV1 falls in restriction too). The ratio is the first branch point.
2. Pseudo-restriction node
When FVC is low but the ratio is not obstructive, check TLC. NORMAL/high TLC with low FVC = PSEUDO-restriction (air trapping, submaximal effort, or coexisting obstruction), NOT true restriction. True restriction requires a LOW TLC.
⚠️ Spirometry alone CANNOT diagnose restriction — a low FVC only suggests it; you need lung volumes (TLC) to confirm. Low FVC + normal TLC = pseudo-restriction.
3. Restrictive causes shelf
Causes of restriction split into PARENCHYMAL (fibrosis/ILD) and EXTRApulmonary (chest-wall disease like kyphoscoliosis/ankylosing spondylitis, neuromuscular weakness, obesity, pleural disease).
⚠️ DLCO splits them: LOW DLCO in parenchymal fibrosis vs NORMAL DLCO in chest wall / neuromuscular / obesity. This is the single most useful next step after confirming low TLC.
4. STIFF (restrictive) man
Restrictive (fibrotic) lungs are STIFF: low compliance and INCREASED elastic recoil make them hard to expand, so all volumes shrink. The increased recoil is why the FEV1/FVC ratio is normal or high — airways are pulled open.
⚠️ Stiff/low-compliance lungs = restriction; do not confuse with the floppy, high-compliance lungs of emphysema (opposite end of the compliance spectrum).
5. RV–FRC–TLC safe
Lung volume definitions: TLC = total air at maximal inhalation; FRC = resting volume at end of a normal exhalation (ERV + RV); RV = air remaining after maximal exhalation. RV, FRC, and TLC all include RV.
⚠️ RV, FRC, and TLC CANNOT be measured by spirometry (because RV never leaves the lung) — they require body plethysmography or gas dilution (helium dilution / nitrogen washout).
6. FRC weights
FRC is the equilibrium point where the inward elastic recoil of the lung exactly balances the outward recoil of the chest wall (net zero pressure, no muscle effort). Emphysema (↓recoil) raises FRC; fibrosis (↑recoil) lowers it.
⚠️ Obesity lowers FRC and ERV FIRST (the outward chest-wall force is overwhelmed by mass) before other volumes change — the earliest obesity PFT abnormality.
7. RESTRICTIVE values
Restrictive pattern: ↓TLC, ↓FVC, ↓RV, with a NORMAL or HIGH FEV1/FVC ratio and increased elastic recoil. FEV1 is reduced but FVC falls proportionally more, keeping the ratio up.
⚠️ The FEV1/FVC ratio is NORMAL or HIGH in restriction — a LOW ratio rules restriction out (it indicates obstruction or a mixed pattern).
8. FLOPPY (obstructive) clown
Obstructive (emphysematous) lungs are FLOPPY: HIGH compliance and LOW elastic recoil. Loss of recoil lets airways collapse on expiration → air trapping → hyperinflation (↑RV, ↑TLC).
⚠️ Floppy/high-compliance = obstruction (emphysema), the exact opposite of stiff/low-compliance restriction. Compliance and recoil move in opposite directions in these two patterns.
9. Obstructive causes shelf
Causes of obstruction (FEV1/FVC <0.7): asthma, COPD/emphysema, chronic bronchitis, and bronchiectasis. DLCO subdivides them: low in emphysema, normal/high in asthma and chronic bronchitis.
⚠️ Asthma obstruction REVERSES with bronchodilator (FEV1 rise ≥12% AND ≥200 mL); fixed COPD does not — reversibility testing is the discriminator.
10. OBSTRUCTIVE values
Obstructive pattern: ↓FEV1/FVC (<0.7), ↑RV, ↑TLC (hyperinflation), ↑FRC, and reduced elastic recoil. A scooped/concave expiratory flow-volume limb accompanies it.
⚠️ An elevated RV/TLC in obstruction reflects air TRAPPING/hyperinflation — don't misread the high volumes as restriction (restriction LOWERS volumes).
11. Air trapping / fast restriction
Severe obstruction with marked air trapping raises RV so much that FVC falls, mimicking restriction (pseudo-restriction). The distinguishing feature: TLC is HIGH in obstruction vs LOW in true restriction.
⚠️ A low FVC in an obstructive patient is pseudo-restriction from air trapping, NOT coexisting true restriction — confirm by measuring TLC (high = trapping, low = true restriction).
12. Lungs-in vs chest-out arrows
Elastic recoil determines resting volumes: LOSS of recoil (emphysema) shifts the balance outward → ↑FRC and ↑RV (hyperinflation); INCREASED recoil (fibrosis) pulls everything inward → ↓FRC, ↓RV, ↓TLC.
⚠️ Recoil direction predicts the volume change — but increased recoil ≠ obstruction. Increased recoil is the RESTRICTIVE (fibrotic) direction; decreased recoil is the obstructive (emphysematous) direction.
