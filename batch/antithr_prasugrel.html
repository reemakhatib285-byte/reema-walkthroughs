📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Prasugrel — rocket-powered potency
Prasugrel is an irreversible thienopyridine P2Y12 inhibitor that is ~10x more potent than clopidogrel, giving faster, stronger, more consistent platelet inhibition. Same target (P2Y12) as clopidogrel.
⚠️ Greater potency = more bleeding, not a free win. Prasugrel saves more ischemic events but causes more major/fatal bleeding than clopidogrel.
2. Efficient CYP activation (one-step)
Prasugrel is also a prodrug but is activated efficiently in essentially one CYP step and is NOT dependent on CYP2C19, so it bypasses the poor-metabolizer problem and PPI interaction.
⚠️ Unlike clopidogrel, prasugrel response is NOT reduced by CYP2C19 loss-of-function or omeprazole. Choose prasugrel/ticagrelor over clopidogrel in CYP2C19 poor metabolizers.
3. Irreversible P2Y12 blockade
Like clopidogrel, prasugrel irreversibly inhibits P2Y12 for the platelet lifespan, so its effect persists after the drug clears and requires new platelets to recover.
⚠️ Irreversible -> hold 5-7 days before surgery. Don't expect rapid offset just because the plasma drug clears.
4. Loading 60 mg, onset ~30 min
Prasugrel loads 60 mg with rapid ~30 min onset; maintenance is 10 mg daily, reduced to 5 mg in the elderly (>=75) or low body weight (<60 kg) to limit bleeding.
⚠️ The 5 mg reduced maintenance dose is specifically for age >=75 OR weight <60 kg. Giving full 10 mg to a frail elderly/low-weight patient is the dosing trap.
5. TRITON-TIMI 38 scoreboard
TRITON-TIMI 38 compared prasugrel vs clopidogrel in ACS patients going to PCI. Prasugrel reduced ischemic events but increased major bleeding — the trial that defined its niche and contraindications.
⚠️ Prasugrel's benefit was in ACS patients with PLANNED PCI/known coronary anatomy. It is not for medically managed ACS (TRILOGY-ACS showed no benefit there).
6. WINS — fewer MI / stent thrombosis
Prasugrel cut the composite of CV death/MI/stroke (9.9% vs 12.1%) and roughly halved stent thrombosis (1.1% vs 2.4%) compared with clopidogrel. 'Saves more hearts.'
⚠️ The ischemic benefit is real but must be weighed against bleeding — net clinical benefit was lost or harmful in the contraindicated subgroups (prior stroke/TIA, elderly, low weight).
7. LOSSES — more fatal bleeding
Prasugrel increased life-threatening bleeding (1.4% vs 0.9%) and fatal bleeding (0.4% vs 0.1%, ~4x). 'Causes more fatal bleeds' — the cost of greater potency.
⚠️ The ~4x fatal-bleed increase drives the contraindications. A board stem with prior stroke/TIA on prasugrel is testing net harm from bleeding.
8. Absolute contraindication — prior stroke/TIA
Prasugrel is ABSOLUTELY contraindicated with any history of stroke or TIA because of net harm from intracranial bleeding observed in TRITON-TIMI 38. This is its hardest rule.
⚠️ Prior stroke/TIA = do NOT use prasugrel (absolute). This is the single most tested prasugrel contraindication; choose clopidogrel or ticagrelor instead.
9. Relative caution — age >=75, weight <60 kg
Age >=75 and weight <60 kg are RELATIVE cautions (higher bleed risk); if used, reduce maintenance to 5 mg daily. Renal impairment warrants careful monitoring.
⚠️ These are RELATIVE (dose-reduce to 5 mg), versus prior stroke/TIA which is ABSOLUTE (avoid entirely). Mixing relative vs absolute contraindications is the classic trap.
10. PCI checklist — when prasugrel fits
Ideal prasugrel candidate: ACS with PLANNED PCI, no stroke/TIA history, age <75, weight >=60 kg, adequate renal function. Outside this profile, prefer an alternative.
⚠️ All checklist boxes must be satisfied. Failing any (stroke/TIA, elderly, low weight) flips prasugrel from first-line to contraindicated/dose-reduced.
11. Medical management — no benefit (TRILOGY-ACS)
In medically managed ACS without PCI (TRILOGY-ACS), prasugrel showed NO benefit over clopidogrel. Its role is specifically ACS undergoing PCI with known anatomy.
⚠️ Don't start prasugrel in conservatively (non-PCI) managed ACS — no benefit there. Ticagrelor, by contrast, is approved across ACS including medical management.
12. Stop 5-7 days before surgery
Prasugrel must be stopped ~7 days before elective surgery (longer than clopidogrel's 5) because of strong irreversible inhibition; recovery requires regenerating fresh platelets.
⚠️ Prasugrel needs ~7 days off (vs ~5 for clopidogrel/ticagrelor). Using the clopidogrel hold time for prasugrel under-doses the washout.
13. Side effects — bleeding, TTP, cytopenias
Primary adverse effect is bleeding; rare thienopyridine-class effects include neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, and TTP. Bleeding dominates prasugrel's risk profile.
⚠️ Bleeding (not dyspnea) is prasugrel's signature toxicity. Dyspnea and ventricular pauses point to TICAGRELOR instead — don't attribute those to prasugrel.
14. Always combine with low-dose aspirin
Prasugrel is used as DAPT with low-dose aspirin (<=100 mg). Low-dose aspirin is mandated in combination to keep bleeding manageable while covering both TXA2 and P2Y12 pathways.
⚠️ Keep aspirin <=100 mg in any DAPT/triple combo. For ticagrelor specifically, higher aspirin doses (>100 mg) reduce its efficacy — a paired board point.
