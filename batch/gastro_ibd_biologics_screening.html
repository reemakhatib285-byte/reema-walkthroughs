📖 Read-through — all 11 symbols
1. Screening gate (TB + HBV)
Before starting biologics (especially anti-TNF) screen for latent TB (IGRA/PPD + CXR) and hepatitis B (HBsAg, anti-HBc) — both can reactivate. Treat latent TB before biologics; manage HBV with antiviral prophylaxis.
2. HCV crossed out (red X)
Routine HCV screening is NOT a required pre-biologic step in IBD (TB and HBV are the mandated screens). HCV does not reactivate with anti-TNF the way HBV does — a board trap.
⚠️ "Screen for HCV before biologics" → wrong; required pre-biologic screens are latent TB and HBV, not HCV.
3. Zoster/Shingrix before JAK (nurse)
Give the recombinant zoster (Shingrix) vaccine BEFORE starting a JAK inhibitor — JAK inhibitors markedly raise herpes zoster risk. JAKs also carry a black-box warning (thrombosis/VTE, MI, malignancy, serious infection).
4. Vedolizumab vest — NO PML (gold star)
Vedolizumab is gut-selective: anti-α4β7 integrin blocking MAdCAM-1 binding, so it acts only on gut lymphocyte trafficking — NO PML risk (the shield). Slow onset; excellent safety profile; good for UC and Crohn's.
5. Natalizumab smoke man (red X)
Natalizumab is anti-α4 integrin (non-selective) and crosses into the CNS → JC-virus reactivation causing PML (progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy). Rarely used in IBD because of this; check anti-JCV antibody status.
⚠️ "Natalizumab is gut-selective and PML-free" → wrong; it is anti-α4 (non-selective) and causes PML.
6. Infliximab fireman on fistula (gold star)
Infliximab = chimeric (mouse-human) IV anti-TNF, BEST agent for FISTULIZING Crohn's. The chimeric portion is more immunogenic. Gold star = first-line for fistulizing CD.
7. Adalimumab mouse (anti-drug Ab)
Adalimumab = fully human subcutaneous anti-TNF; can still form anti-drug antibodies, so combine with an immunomodulator (e.g., AZA/MTX) to reduce immunogenicity and loss of response. Self-injected, with a weekly dosing option.
8. Ustekinumab dart (IL-12/23)
Ustekinumab targets the shared p40 subunit of IL-12 and IL-23. Used in Crohn's and UC; favorable safety, good for anti-TNF failures.
9. Risankizumab dart (IL-23)
Risankizumab selectively targets the p19 subunit of IL-23 — newer, effective for Crohn's. Selective IL-23 blockade is the trend beyond combined IL-12/23 inhibition.
10. Pregnant traveler passes safely
Anti-TNF agents are SAFE in pregnancy and generally continued to maintain remission (active disease is the bigger fetal risk). IgG1 anti-TNF crosses the placenta mainly in the 3rd trimester; coordinate live-vaccine timing for the infant.
⚠️ "Anti-TNF is contraindicated in pregnancy" → wrong; anti-TNFs are considered safe and usually continued.
11. Etanercept blocked at door (red X)
Etanercept (TNF-receptor decoy) is ineffective in IBD — joint disease only. The effective IBD anti-TNFs are the monoclonals infliximab and adalimumab.
⚠️ "Etanercept works for IBD" → wrong; it is ineffective in IBD; use infliximab or adalimumab.
