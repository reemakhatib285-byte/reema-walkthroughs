📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. VEXAS — UBA1
VEXAS syndrome is caused by an ACQUIRED SOMATIC mutation in UBA1 (the E1 ubiquitin-activating enzyme) in myeloid progenitor cells. Because UBA1 is on the X chromosome, it almost exclusively affects older MEN (>50). The acronym: Vacuoles (in myeloid/erythroid precursors on marrow biopsy), E1 enzyme (UBA1), X-linked, Autoinflammatory, Somatic. Described in 2020, it bridges rheumatology and hematology.
⚠️ VEXAS is an ACQUIRED somatic mutation of adulthood — NOT an inherited childhood autoinflammatory syndrome. The trap is treating an older man's refractory inflammation as a benign autoimmune condition and missing the bone marrow / UBA1 workup.
2. VEXAS features
VEXAS presents with treatment-refractory systemic inflammation — recurrent fevers, chondritis (ear/nose, relapsing-polychondritis-like), skin vasculitis/neutrophilic dermatosis, periorbital/orbital inflammation, lung infiltrates — PLUS hematologic disease: macrocytic anemia, progressive cytopenias, and a strong association with myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) and VTE. It is often steroid-dependent and tends to be poorly responsive to standard immunosuppression; azacitidine or allogeneic transplant may be needed.
⚠️ Suspect VEXAS in an older man with refractory inflammation AND unexplained macrocytic anemia / cytopenias / MDS — the wrong path is to keep escalating empiric immunosuppression for presumed seronegative rheumatic disease without marrow biopsy and UBA1 sequencing.
3. PAPA syndrome
PAPA = Pyogenic (sterile) Arthritis, Pyoderma gangrenosum, and Acne. It is autosomal DOMINANT due to PSTPIP1 mutations that dysregulate pyrin/inflammasome signaling, presenting in childhood with recurrent destructive sterile arthritis that later gives way to severe cystic acne and pyoderma gangrenosum. IL-1 and TNF inhibitors are used.
⚠️ PAPA arthritis is STERILE despite looking pyogenic — synovial fluid is purulent-appearing but cultures are NEGATIVE. Treating it as septic arthritis with antibiotics alone (and missing the autoinflammatory diagnosis) is the trap.
4. DADA2
DADA2 = Deficiency of ADA2, autosomal recessive loss-of-function in ADA2/CECR1. It causes a PAN-like medium-vessel vasculitis with early-onset (childhood) recurrent ischemic/hemorrhagic LACUNAR STROKES, livedo racemosa, fevers, and variable immunodeficiency/cytopenias (hypogammaglobulinemia, pure red cell aplasia).
⚠️ DADA2 causes EARLY childhood strokes + livedo — the correct therapy is ANTI-TNF (which prevents strokes), NOT antiplatelets/anticoagulation alone, and anticoagulation can be dangerous given hemorrhagic risk. Treating it as ordinary pediatric stroke or isolated PAN is the trap.
5. Blau syndrome
Blau syndrome is autosomal DOMINANT NOD2 (CARD15) gain-of-function, presenting in early childhood (<4 yrs) with the triad of granulomatous polyarthritis (boggy synovitis/tenosynovitis), granulomatous uveitis (which can threaten vision), and granulomatous dermatitis. Histology shows noncaseating granulomas.
⚠️ Blau is early-onset GRANULOMATOUS arthritis/uveitis/rash from NOD2 — it looks like sarcoidosis but is a monogenic childhood disease (early-onset sarcoidosis is the same entity). The trap is diagnosing childhood sarcoid without recognizing the NOD2 genetics. Note NOD2 loss-of-function is linked to Crohn's, while Blau is gain-of-function.
6. Majeed / SAVI / interferonopathies
Type I interferonopathies (e.g., SAVI from STING gain-of-function; CANDLE; Aicardi-Goutières) are driven by excess type I interferon (IFN-α/β) rather than IL-1. SAVI causes early-onset systemic inflammation, severe acral/facial vasculopathy with skin ulceration and digital gangrene, and interstitial lung disease.
⚠️ Interferonopathies are IFN-driven — the rational therapy is a JAK inhibitor (baricitinib/ruxolitinib) to block IFN signaling, NOT IL-1 blockade. Reaching for anakinra in an IFN-signature disease is the wrong-mechanism trap.
7. Rosai-Dorfman / histiocytosis
Histiocytic disorders sit in the systemic-inflammation differential. Rosai-Dorfman disease (sinus histiocytosis with massive lymphadenopathy) features painless massive cervical lymphadenopathy and infiltrative skin/extranodal lesions; biopsy shows emperipolesis (intact lymphocytes within histiocytes) and S100-positive, CD1a-negative histiocytes.
⚠️ Massive painless lymphadenopathy with EMPERIPOLESIS on biopsy points to Rosai-Dorfman, not FMF or another periodic fever. The trap is anchoring on 'recurrent fever + nodes' and missing the histiocytic diagnosis that biopsy reveals.
8. CRMO / chronic osteomyelitis
Chronic recurrent multifocal osteomyelitis (CRMO) is a sterile autoinflammatory bone disease of children/adolescents with multifocal, often metaphyseal, lytic/sclerotic lesions and bone pain; it falls within the SAPHO spectrum (Synovitis, Acne, Pustulosis, Hyperostosis, Osteitis). NSAIDs are first-line; bisphosphonates/TNF inhibitors for refractory disease.
⚠️ CRMO bone lesions are STERILE — cultures are negative and ANTIBIOTICS do not help. The trap is repeatedly treating it as bacterial osteomyelitis; it is autoinflammatory, and biopsy/cultures distinguish it from infection.
9. Schnitzler syndrome
Schnitzler syndrome is an adult-onset acquired autoinflammatory disorder defined by chronic urticarial rash PLUS a monoclonal IgM gammopathy (rarely IgG), with recurrent fever, bone pain, arthralgia, lymphadenopathy, and elevated inflammatory markers. It is IL-1β driven and responds dramatically to anakinra; there is a long-term risk of progression to Waldenström macroglobulinemia/lymphoproliferative disease.
⚠️ Schnitzler = chronic urticaria + monoclonal IgM in an ADULT — order SPEP/immunofixation to find the IgM paraprotein. The trap is treating the rash as ordinary chronic idiopathic urticaria with antihistamines and missing the monoclonal gammopathy; it responds to IL-1 blockade, not antihistamines.
10. MEN / inheritance tags
Inheritance pattern is a high-yield sorting tool across the gallery: VEXAS is X-linked AND acquired/somatic; PAPA and Blau are autosomal DOMINANT; DADA2 is autosomal RECESSIVE; FMF and HIDS are recessive while TRAPS is dominant.
⚠️ Boards test inheritance directly — don't mix up the dominant syndromes (PAPA, Blau, TRAPS) with the recessive ones (DADA2, FMF, HIDS). Calling DADA2 dominant or VEXAS inherited-germline is the classic trap.
11. Sterile inflammation theme
The unifying theme: autoinflammatory disease is driven by the INNATE immune system (inflammasome/cytokine dysregulation, especially IL-1 and IFN) WITHOUT high-titer autoantibodies or antigen-specific T cells. Inflammation is sterile, recurrent/stereotyped, and culture-negative.
⚠️ Autoinflammatory is NOT the same as autoimmune — there are no characteristic autoantibodies (no ANA/RF/anti-dsDNA driving it) and complement/serologies are typically unremarkable. Ordering an autoantibody panel and expecting it to clinch the diagnosis is the conceptual trap.
12. Treatment cheat sheet
Targeted therapy follows the cytokine driver: IL-1 blockers (anakinra/canakinumab) for cryopyrinopathies, Schnitzler, and refractory FMF; anti-TNF for DADA2 (stroke prevention) and Blau; JAK inhibitors for type I interferonopathies (SAVI/CANDLE); colchicine for FMF. VEXAS is often refractory and may need azacitidine or transplant.
⚠️ Match the biologic to the cytokine driver — a wrong target equals treatment failure. The trap is reflexively choosing IL-1 blockade for everything: it fails in interferonopathies (need JAK) and is not the answer for DADA2 (anti-TNF) or VEXAS.
