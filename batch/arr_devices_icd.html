📖 Read-through — all 8 symbols
1. DDD for complete AV block
A dual-chamber (DDD) pacemaker paces and senses both atrium and ventricle, preserving AV synchrony — preferred for AV block when the atrium is in sinus rhythm. The NBG code = chamber paced / sensed / response.
⚠️ WRONG: 'a single-chamber ventricular pacemaker is ideal for AV block in sinus rhythm.' DDD preserves AV synchrony and avoids pacemaker syndrome.
2. VVI for chronic AF
In chronic (permanent) AF there is no organized atrial activity to track, so a single-chamber ventricular (VVI) pacemaker is appropriate — an atrial lead would be useless.
⚠️ WRONG: 'use DDD in permanent AF.' With no atrial rhythm to sense, dual-chamber pacing adds no benefit — VVI is used.
3. Pacemaker syndrome → upgrade to DDD
Pacemaker syndrome occurs with VVI pacing when retrograde VA conduction causes AV dyssynchrony — fatigue, dyspnea, hypotension, and neck pulsations. Treatment is upgrading to dual-chamber (DDD) pacing.
⚠️ WRONG: 'pacemaker syndrome means the device has failed and needs replacement.' It's an AV-synchrony problem — upgrade VVI to DDD.
4. Cannon A waves
Cannon A waves = the right atrium contracts against a closed tricuspid valve (AV dissociation) — seen in complete heart block, VT, and pacemaker syndrome.
⚠️ WRONG: 'cannon A waves indicate tricuspid regurgitation.' They reflect atrial contraction against a closed valve from AV dissociation.
5. ICD primary prevention: EF ≤35% + NYHA II–III
Primary-prevention ICD is indicated for EF ≤35% with NYHA class II–III on optimal medical therapy (ischemic or non-ischemic cardiomyopathy) to prevent sudden cardiac death.
⚠️ WRONG: 'any low EF qualifies immediately for an ICD.' You must be on optimal medical therapy and past the required waiting period first.
6. Wait 40 d post-MI / 90 d post-revasc
Re-assess EF and implant a primary-prevention ICD only after ≥40 days post-MI (DINAMIT/IRIS showed no benefit earlier) and ≥90 days post-revascularization — EF often recovers in that window.
⚠️ WRONG: 'implant the ICD during the index MI admission if EF is low.' Early post-MI ICD shows no mortality benefit — wait and reassess EF.
7. ICD secondary prevention: VT/VF/aborted SCD
Secondary-prevention ICD is for survivors of cardiac arrest (VF/VT) or sustained VT with structural heart disease not from a reversible cause — regardless of EF.
⚠️ WRONG: 'a normal EF after an aborted cardiac arrest means no ICD.' Survived VF/sustained VT (no reversible cause) warrants an ICD irrespective of EF.
8. CRT: EF ≤35% + LBBB + QRS >150
Cardiac resynchronization (biventricular pacing) helps HFrEF with EF ≤35%, LBBB, and wide QRS (greatest benefit ≥150 ms) on optimal therapy — improving symptoms and survival.
⚠️ WRONG: 'CRT helps any wide QRS.' Benefit is greatest with LBBB and QRS ≥150 ms; non-LBBB/narrower QRS benefit little.
