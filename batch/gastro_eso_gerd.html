📖 Read-through — all 13 symbols
1. Transient LES relaxation
The dominant pathophysiology of GERD is transient lower-esophageal-sphincter relaxations (tLESRs), not a chronically weak sphincter alone. Hiatal hernia, obesity and delayed gastric emptying contribute. Acid (and sometimes bile) refluxes into the esophagus.
2. Empiric PPI = diagnosis (gold star)
GERD is a CLINICAL diagnosis; typical heartburn/regurgitation without alarm features warrants an empiric PPI trial (once daily x8 weeks). Symptom response confirms it. No scope or pH study needed for the classic uncomplicated case.
3. Scope only if alarm
Endoscopy is reserved for ALARM features: dysphagia, odynophagia, weight loss, GI bleeding/anemia, recurrent vomiting, or new symptoms at older age (and to screen for Barrett's in chronic GERD with risk factors). Routine scoping of typical reflux is not indicated.
4. Scope everyone (RED X concept)
Endoscoping every reflux patient is wrong; classic GERD is diagnosed and treated empirically. Scope only for alarm features or Barrett's surveillance. Over-scoping is low yield and costly.
⚠️ 'endoscopy for everyone with reflux' -> wrong; scope only with alarm features; otherwise empiric PPI.
5. PPI more effective than H2
PPIs heal erosive esophagitis and control symptoms better than H2-blockers (which also lose potency via tachyphylaxis). PPI is first-line maintenance for erosive/severe disease. H2-blockers are for mild/intermittent symptoms or nocturnal breakthrough.
6. H2-blocker / PPI fails
If a PPI 'fails', first verify adherence and timing (30-60 min before meals) and twice-daily dosing before escalating. H2-blocker is weaker than PPI and not the upgrade. Refractory true GERD -> pH-impedance testing, consider fundoplication.
7. Take PPI 30 min before food
PPIs are prodrugs that must covalently bind ACTIVATED proton pumps; take ~30-60 min BEFORE a meal so peak drug level coincides with meal-stimulated pump activity. Taking it with food or at bedtime fasting blunts efficacy.
8. With food / take it (RED X)
Taking a PPI WITH food is wrong: fewer pumps are in the activatable state when the drug peaks, reducing acid suppression. Correct timing is 30-60 min before the meal.
⚠️ 'take PPI with food' -> wrong; take 30-60 min before meals so it inhibits meal-activated proton pumps.
9. With food and lying down (RED X)
Lying down after eating worsens reflux (gravity no longer clears acid) and mistimes the PPI. Lifestyle advice: don't recline within 3h of meals, elevate the head of bed, weight loss, avoid late meals.
⚠️ 'take PPI with food and lie down' -> wrong; recumbency worsens reflux and timing is off; dose before meals, stay upright.
10. Low magnesium (PPI side effect)
Long-term PPI side effects: hypomagnesemia, plus impaired absorption of B12, iron and calcium (-> fracture risk), increased C. difficile and community pneumonia, possible CKD/AIN. These reflect reduced gastric acidity.
11. High calcium (RED X)
Hypercalcemia is NOT a PPI effect; PPIs actually impair calcium absorption (favoring low Ca and fractures). The classic electrolyte association is hypoMAGNESemia. High calcium on a stem points elsewhere.
⚠️ 'hypercalcemia as a PPI side effect' -> wrong; PPIs cause hypomagnesemia and reduce Ca absorption, not raise it.
12. Heartburn worse after meals/lying
Reflux heartburn classically worsens AFTER meals and when lying down or bending (postprandial gastric distension + recumbency). 'Before meals / relieved by food' pattern suggests duodenal ulcer instead, a useful discriminator.
13. NERD = normal endoscopy
Non-erosive reflux disease (NERD) = typical reflux symptoms with a NORMAL endoscopy (no erosions/Barrett's). Still real GERD; confirm with pH-impedance if needed. Manage with PPI/lifestyle as in erosive disease.
