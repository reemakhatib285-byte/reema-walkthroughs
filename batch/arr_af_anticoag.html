📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. CHA₂DS₂-VASc scoreboard
CHA₂DS₂-VASc estimates annual ischemic-stroke risk in non-valvular AF and drives the anticoagulation decision. Anticoagulate when score ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women); score 0 (men)/1 (women) can be left off. C/H/D/Vascular/Age65-74/female = 1 each; Age≥75 and prior Stroke/TIA/thromboembolism = 2 each.
⚠️ WRONG: 'aspirin is adequate when the score is 1-2.' Antiplatelets are NOT a substitute — a true indication needs an oral anticoagulant (DOAC or warfarin).
2. Age ≥75 = 2 points
Age ≥75 carries TWO points (the heaviest age weight) because stroke risk rises steeply with age. A single elderly patient with no other factor already reaches a treatment-level score.
⚠️ WRONG: 'every risk factor is worth 1 point.' Age≥75 and prior stroke/TIA are each worth 2 — miscounting these changes the treatment decision.
3. Stroke/TIA = 2 points
Prior stroke, TIA, or systemic embolism is the strongest predictor and scores 2 points — secondary prevention. Any such history alone mandates lifelong anticoagulation regardless of other factors.
⚠️ WRONG: 'a remote TIA doesn't count.' Prior cerebral ischemia is a 2-point, treatment-defining factor.
4. Female sex alone ≠ mandate
Female sex is a risk MODIFIER, not an independent trigger: a woman with sex as her only point (score 1) does not require anticoagulation. The threshold is ≥2 in men and ≥3 in women so that sex alone never tips the decision.
⚠️ WRONG: 'any woman with AF needs anticoagulation.' Female sex counts only when other risk factors are present.
5. Valvular AF → warfarin only
'Valvular AF' = moderate-to-severe mitral stenosis or a mechanical heart valve. These REQUIRE a vitamin-K antagonist (warfarin) — DOACs are contraindicated (RE-ALIGN showed harm with dabigatran in mechanical valves). CHA₂DS₂-VASc is not used here; the valve itself mandates warfarin.
⚠️ WRONG: 'a DOAC is fine for AF with a mechanical valve.' DOACs are contraindicated in mechanical valves and significant mitral stenosis — use warfarin.
6. Non-valvular AF → DOAC
For non-valvular AF, DOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban) are first-line over warfarin — comparable/better stroke prevention with less intracranial bleeding and no INR monitoring. Apixaban is often favored in CKD and bleeding-prone patients.
⚠️ WRONG: 'warfarin is preferred for all AF.' Warfarin is reserved for valvular AF; otherwise DOACs are preferred.
7. DOAC bottles (apixaban)
DOACs need no routine level monitoring and have rapid on/off kinetics. Apixaban dose-reduces to 2.5 mg BID with ≥2 of: age≥80, weight≤60 kg, creatinine≥1.5; dabigatran/edoxaban need adequate renal function.
⚠️ WRONG: 'DOACs always need INR checks.' DOACs are not monitored by INR — that's warfarin.
8. Aspirin = INADEQUATE
Antiplatelet therapy (aspirin ± clopidogrel) is inadequate for AF stroke prevention and carries bleeding risk without the benefit — it is no longer recommended in place of anticoagulation.
⚠️ WRONG: 'aspirin is a safer alternative to anticoagulation in the elderly.' It is both less effective and not meaningfully safer — use a DOAC.
9. Warfarin INR 2–3
When warfarin is required (valvular AF), the target INR is 2.0–3.0 (2.5–3.5 for some mechanical valves). It needs bridging consideration and regular monitoring.
⚠️ WRONG: 'INR target is 1.5–2 for AF.' Sub-therapeutic INR <2 does not protect against stroke.
10. HAS-BLED seesaw
HAS-BLED estimates bleeding risk; a high score flags modifiable factors (BP, alcohol, labile INR, concomitant antiplatelets) to correct — it does NOT by itself contraindicate anticoagulation.
⚠️ WRONG: 'a high HAS-BLED means withhold anticoagulation.' It prompts risk-factor correction and closer follow-up, not withholding.
11. AF >48 h → image or 3 wks
Before cardioversion of AF lasting >48 h (or unknown duration), either give ≥3 weeks of therapeutic anticoagulation OR exclude left-atrial-appendage thrombus first; anticoagulate ≥4 weeks after cardioversion regardless.
⚠️ WRONG: 'cardiovert immediately if the patient feels well.' Unanticoagulated cardioversion of AF >48 h risks thromboembolic stroke.
12. Pre-cardioversion: TEE/CT vs TTE
To exclude a left-atrial-appendage thrombus before cardioversion, use TEE (or cardiac CT) — a transthoracic echo (TTE) cannot reliably visualize the appendage.
⚠️ WRONG: 'a normal TTE rules out atrial thrombus.' TTE cannot see the LAA — you need TEE or CT.
