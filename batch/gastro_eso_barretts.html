📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. Intestinal metaplasia
Barrett's esophagus = the normal squamous epithelium of the distal esophagus is replaced by intestinal-type columnar epithelium (metaplasia), an adaptive response to chronic acid. It extends upward from the GE junction (salmon-colored on endoscopy).
2. Goblet cells
Histologic hallmark required for diagnosis: specialized intestinal metaplasia with GOBLET cells (mucin-containing, stain with Alcian blue). Columnar lining without goblet cells does not qualify as Barrett's in most definitions.
3. Risk: chronic GERD, obese white male
Risk factors: long-standing GERD, age >50, male sex, white race, central obesity, smoking, hiatal hernia. The classic stem patient is an older obese white man with years of reflux. These same factors raise esophageal adenocarcinoma risk.
4. Progression metaplasia->dysplasia->cancer
Stepwise progression: intestinal metaplasia -> low-grade dysplasia -> high-grade dysplasia -> invasive adenocarcinoma. Dysplasia grade drives both surveillance interval and treatment. Each step raises cancer risk.
5. Adenocarcinoma ~0.1-0.3%/yr
Non-dysplastic Barrett's progresses to esophageal ADENOcarcinoma at ~0.1-0.3% per year. Barrett's is the main precursor lesion for distal esophageal adenocarcinoma. Cancer risk rises sharply with high-grade dysplasia.
6. Squamous cell cancer (RED X)
Barrett's leads to ADENOcarcinoma, NOT squamous cell carcinoma. Squamous cell esophageal cancer is linked to smoking + alcohol and is mid/upper esophagus; it is not the Barrett's pathway.
⚠️ 'Barrett's progresses to squamous cell carcinoma' -> wrong; it leads to adenocarcinoma; SCC is the smoking/alcohol upper-esophagus cancer.
7. Surveillance every 3-5 yr
Non-dysplastic Barrett's: surveillance endoscopy with 4-quadrant biopsies every 3-5 years. Low-grade dysplasia -> ablation or scope every 6-12 months; high-grade dysplasia -> endoscopic eradication. Continue PPI to control reflux.
8. Wrong interval 6 months (RED X)
Re-scoping plain non-dysplastic metaplasia every 6 months is wrong/over-surveillance; the correct interval is 3-5 years. The 6-12 month interval applies only once low-grade dysplasia is found.
⚠️ 'surveillance every 6 months for non-dysplastic Barrett's' -> wrong; correct interval is 3-5 years.
9. High-grade dysplasia -> RFA/EMR/ESD (gold star)
High-grade dysplasia (and intramucosal cancer) is treated with endoscopic eradication therapy: EMR/ESD to resect visible/nodular lesions plus radiofrequency ablation (RFA) of the remaining flat Barrett's segment. This has largely replaced prophylactic esophagectomy.
10. Esophagectomy (historical)
Esophagectomy was historically used for high-grade dysplasia but carries high morbidity/mortality; endoscopic eradication (RFA + EMR/ESD) is now preferred for HGD and intramucosal cancer. Reserve surgery for invasive disease beyond the mucosa.
11. PPI does not remove cancer risk (RED X)
PPIs control reflux symptoms and may slow progression but do NOT eliminate the cancer risk of established Barrett's; surveillance must continue and dysplasia still needs ablation/resection. PPI is adjunct, not cure.
⚠️ 'PPI eliminates the cancer risk in Barrett's' -> wrong; it controls reflux but doesn't remove risk; surveillance continues.
12. PPI does not remove cancer risk (RED X, lower)
Reinforced near the high-grade/cancer end: even with high-grade dysplasia, PPI alone is insufficient; definitive endoscopic eradication therapy is required. Acid suppression does not reverse dysplasia.
⚠️ 'PPI alone manages high-grade dysplasia' -> wrong; HGD needs endoscopic eradication (RFA + EMR/ESD), not just PPI.
