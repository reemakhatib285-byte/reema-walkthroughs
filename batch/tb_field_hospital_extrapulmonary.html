📖 Read-through — all 15 symbols
1. Pleural TB — fluid vs biopsy
TB pleuritis gives an exudative, lymphocyte-predominant effusion with HIGH adenosine deaminase (ADA) and low glucose. Pleural fluid AFB/culture yield is low (~30–50%); pleural BIOPSY yields ~80%.
⚠️ Diagnose pleural TB by biopsy of the pleura, NOT by fluid culture — fluid smear is usually negative.
2. Pleural fluid: exudate, low glucose
Lymphocytic exudate with elevated ADA (>40) and low glucose is the classic TB pleural profile; mesothelial cells are characteristically scarce.
⚠️ Absence of mesothelial cells supports TB; eosinophilic effusion argues AGAINST TB.
3. Miliary TB — disseminated
Miliary TB = hematogenous dissemination with countless 1–2 mm nodules across organs. High mortality; can cause ARDS and marrow involvement. Common in HIV and young children.
⚠️ In a CSF/effusion, a very low ADA argues against TB; miliary disease itself needs urgent multi-drug therapy + biopsy of an accessible site.
4. Lymph node TB — scrofula
Scrofula = TB cervical lymphadenitis, classically matted, rubbery posterior cervical/supraclavicular nodes. Most common extrapulmonary site overall.
⚠️ Confirm by EXCISIONAL/aspiration biopsy and culture; FNA can miss it. In children, nontuberculous mycobacteria can mimic scrofula.
5. TB meningitis — basilar
TB meningitis is subacute (1–2 weeks) with high CSF protein, LOW glucose, lymphocytic pleocytosis, and basilar meningitis affecting cranial nerves. Xpert/CSF AFB low sensitivity.
⚠️ Add CORTICOSTEROIDS to TB meningitis and pericarditis to reduce mortality/complications — a high-yield exception to 'no steroids in infection'.
6. Xpert CSF ~80% / culture gold
GeneXpert MTB/RIF is rapid (<2 h) and also detects rifampin resistance; sensitivity ~40–65% on paucibacillary samples. Culture (MGIT) remains the gold standard and allows full DST.
⚠️ A negative Xpert does NOT rule out paucibacillary TB (meningitis, pleura) — culture and biopsy still needed.
7. Pott disease — spinal TB
Spinal TB (Pott disease) favors the lower thoracic spine causing anterior vertebral collapse → gibbus kyphosis, and cold paraspinal/psoas abscesses. Treat with HRZE; surgery for cord compression.
⚠️ A 'cold' (non-tender, afebrile) psoas abscess tracking from the spine = TB until proven otherwise; pyogenic abscesses are hot/painful.
8. Chemo yes, steroids no (Pott)
Standard anti-TB chemotherapy (HRZE) treats Pott disease; steroids are NOT routinely indicated for skeletal TB (unlike CNS/pericardial TB).
⚠️ Steroids help TB meningitis/pericarditis but NOT spinal/pleural TB — match the steroid indication to the site.
9. Xpert <2 h, RIF resistance
GeneXpert gives a result in under 2 hours and simultaneously screens for rifampin resistance, a surrogate for MDR-TB.
⚠️ Rifampin resistance on Xpert flags possible MDR-TB — escalate to second-line regimen and full DST.
10. AFB smear sensitivity 40–65%
Sputum AFB smear is fast and cheap but only 40–65% sensitive and cannot distinguish M. tuberculosis from nontuberculous mycobacteria.
⚠️ A positive smear ≠ confirmed TB (could be NTM); a negative smear does not exclude TB — culture/NAAT confirm.
11. Culture gold standard, slow
Liquid MGIT culture is the gold standard, turning positive in ~1–2 weeks (solid Löwenstein–Jensen up to 8 weeks) and enabling drug-susceptibility testing.
⚠️ Culture confirms diagnosis and resistance but is too slow to guide initial therapy — start empiric RIPE on suspicion.
12. TPT eligibility — treat LTBI
High-risk LTBI (HIV, transplant, anti-TNF candidates, recent converters) should receive TB preventive therapy after active TB is excluded.
⚠️ Always exclude active disease before LTBI monotherapy to avoid generating resistance.
13. HIV: start ART by CD4 timing
In HIV/TB co-infection, start TB therapy first, then ART: within 2 weeks if CD4 <50, by 8 weeks if CD4 ≥50 — to limit mortality while reducing IRIS.
⚠️ TB MENINGITIS is the exception — DELAY ART ~8 weeks regardless of CD4 because early ART worsens CNS IRIS.
14. Delay ART 8 wks in CNS TB
Immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome (IRIS) after ART is dangerous in CNS TB; delaying ART to ~8 weeks reduces fatal CNS IRIS.
⚠️ Don't rush ART in TB meningitis even with very low CD4 — opposite of the usual 'low CD4 = start ART early' rule.
15. HIV TB: paucibacillary, no granulomas
Advanced HIV impairs granuloma formation, giving disseminated/miliary disease with higher organism load but frequently anergic (false-negative) TST.
⚠️ A negative TST does NOT exclude TB in advanced HIV — anergy is common; rely on IGRA, NAAT, culture, and clinical suspicion.
