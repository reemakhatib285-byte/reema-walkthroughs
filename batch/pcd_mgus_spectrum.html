📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. MGUS criteria
MGUS (monoclonal gammopathy of undetermined significance) requires ALL three: serum M-protein <3 g/dL (<30 g/L) AND clonal marrow plasma cells <10% AND NO CRAB/SLiM end-organ damage. It is a premalignant clone present in ~3% of people over 50 and ~5% over 70. Management is observation, not treatment — recheck SPEP/CBC/calcium/creatinine periodically.
⚠️ WRONG answer: starting myeloma therapy (e.g., lenalidomide/bortezomib) for MGUS. The discriminator is that MGUS has no end-organ damage by definition — ANY CRAB feature or SLiM biomarker reclassifies the patient as multiple myeloma, which is what gets treated; MGUS is only monitored.
2. MGUS plasma cells less than 10%
MGUS marrow clonal plasma cells must be <10% (often a marrow isn't even needed if M-protein is low and labs are clean). The clone progresses to myeloma or a related malignancy at a steady ~1% per year. Mnemonic: 'MGUS marches at 1% a year' — risk is lifelong, so surveillance never fully stops.
⚠️ WRONG answer: reassuring the patient that MGUS is benign and discharging them with no follow-up. The discriminator is the cumulative ~1%/year progression risk — MGUS needs ongoing surveillance (interval guided by risk stratification), not one-and-done reassurance.
3. MGUS all-green (no damage)
MGUS lifetime progression is roughly 1%/year (~25% over a lifetime), and most patients never progress and die of other causes. Risk stratifies on three factors: non-IgG isotype, M-protein ≥1.5 g/dL, and abnormal serum free light chain ratio (0 vs 1–3 adverse factors). Low-risk IgG MGUS <1.5 g/dL with normal FLC ratio can have minimal follow-up.
⚠️ WRONG answer: treating all MGUS as equally low risk. The discriminator is the Mayo risk model — IgA/IgM isotype, M-protein ≥1.5 g/dL, and abnormal FLC ratio define high-risk MGUS warranting closer monitoring, sometimes with a baseline marrow and imaging.
4. Smoldering MM (SMM)
Smoldering multiple myeloma is the intermediate stage — greater tumor burden than MGUS but still NO CRAB and no SLiM biomarkers (so no end-organ damage). It is asymptomatic by definition and historically observed, though high-risk SMM is increasingly offered early lenalidomide on trials. Mnemonic: 'smoldering = fire is lit but the building isn't burning (no CRAB).'
⚠️ WRONG answer: treating all smoldering myeloma like active myeloma. The discriminator is the absence of CRAB/SLiM — standard care is close observation; only HIGH-RISK SMM (or development of a myeloma-defining event) prompts therapy, and early lenalidomide is largely trial/guideline-evolving.
5. SMM M-protein greater than or equal to 30 g/L
SMM is defined by serum M-protein ≥3 g/dL (≥30 g/L) OR urine M-protein ≥500 mg/24h, OR marrow clonal plasma cells 10–60%, in the absence of CRAB and SLiM events. So it sits between MGUS (<3 g/dL and <10% cells) and myeloma (any defining event). Numbers separate the stages — burden up, but organs still intact.
⚠️ WRONG answer: assuming an M-protein ≥3 g/dL automatically means symptomatic myeloma needing chemo. The discriminator is end-organ status — without CRAB or a SLiM biomarker, a ≥3 g/dL spike is smoldering myeloma, which is observed, not immediately treated.
6. SMM plasma cells 10-60%
SMM marrow clonal plasma cells fall in the 10–60% range. The upper bound matters: reaching ≥60% is a SLiM myeloma-defining event that diagnoses myeloma even without CRAB. Mnemonic for the 'Sixty' in SLiM: 60% plasma cells = the building is full, call it myeloma.
⚠️ WRONG answer: waiting for CRAB to appear before treating a patient whose marrow shows 65% clonal plasma cells. The discriminator is the SLiM '60%' rule — ≥60% marrow plasma cells defines myeloma now and is an indication to treat, regardless of intact end-organs.
7. SMM risk factors
High-risk SMM is captured by the '20-2-20' model: M-protein >2 g/dL, marrow plasma cells >20%, and FLC ratio >20 — any 2 of 3 marks high risk for rapid progression (~50% at 2 years). These patients are candidates for early intervention/trials. Mnemonic: '20-2-20' = the three twenties of dangerous smoldering disease.
⚠️ WRONG answer: using only M-protein level to gauge SMM risk. The discriminator is the multi-factor 20-2-20 stratification (M-protein, marrow plasma cell %, and FLC ratio together) — high-risk SMM behaves almost like myeloma and warrants closer follow-up or clinical-trial therapy.
8. Symptomatic MM
Active multiple myeloma = clonal marrow plasma cells (usually ≥10%) PLUS at least one myeloma-defining event: any CRAB feature OR any SLiM biomarker. This is the stage that gets treated — typical induction is a triplet/quadruplet such as VRd (bortezomib + lenalidomide + dexamethasone) ± daratumumab, then autologous stem cell transplant in eligible patients. Mnemonic: 'a defining event turns the clone into a disease.'
⚠️ WRONG answer: requiring symptoms (bone pain, fatigue) before diagnosing myeloma. The discriminator is the 2014 IMWG update — SLiM biomarkers (60% plasma cells, FLC ratio ≥100, >1 focal MRI lesion) define myeloma in still-asymptomatic patients so therapy can start before CRAB damage occurs.
9. MM workforce greater than or equal to 60%
Clonal marrow plasma cells ≥60% is the 'S' of SLiM and independently defines multiple myeloma even with no CRAB, because progression to organ damage is nearly inevitable. It removes the old requirement to wait for kidney/bone/calcium injury. This is the 'Sixty percent' criterion.
⚠️ WRONG answer: classifying a patient with 62% marrow plasma cells but normal calcium/creatinine/Hgb as smoldering myeloma. The discriminator is the SLiM ≥60% rule — that alone upgrades them to active myeloma requiring treatment.
10. SLiM biomarkers
SLiM myeloma-defining biomarkers: Sixty percent clonal marrow plasma cells, Light-chain (involved:uninvolved free) ratio ≥100 (with involved FLC ≥100 mg/L), and MRI with >1 focal lesion (each ≥5 mm). Any one defines multiple myeloma WITHOUT CRAB. Mnemonic: SLiM = Sixty / Light chains 100 / MRI lesions.
⚠️ WRONG answer: telling a patient with an FLC ratio of 120 and no CRAB that they only have smoldering disease. The discriminator is that any single SLiM biomarker (here, FLC ratio ≥100) is myeloma-defining and an indication to treat, even with normal calcium, kidneys, hemoglobin, and bones.
11. Progression rate 1%/year
MGUS progresses to malignancy at ~1%/year (constant lifelong). Smoldering myeloma progresses much faster — about 10%/year in the first 5 years, then slowing — which is why SMM is watched more closely than MGUS. The steepening curve up the 'building' mirrors rising tumor burden and risk.
⚠️ WRONG answer: applying the MGUS 1%/year rate to smoldering myeloma surveillance intervals. The discriminator is that SMM's early progression (~10%/year) demands tighter monitoring (e.g., every 2–3 months initially) than MGUS.
12. Solitary plasmacytoma
Solitary plasmacytoma is a single clonal plasma cell mass in bone or soft tissue (extramedullary) with a NORMAL marrow (<10% clonal cells) and no CRAB. Treatment of choice is definitive local radiotherapy (~40–50 Gy), which can be curative. Bone plasmacytomas progress to overt myeloma more often than extramedullary ones — follow with serial SPEP/FLC.
⚠️ WRONG answer: giving systemic myeloma chemotherapy for an isolated plasmacytoma. The discriminator is that it is localized with a clean marrow — first-line is radiation to the lesion, reserving systemic therapy for progression to multiple myeloma.
13. Nonsecretory myeloma
Nonsecretory myeloma (~1–2% of cases) has marrow plasmacytosis and CRAB end-organ damage but little or no detectable serum/urine M-protein on SPEP/UPEP. Most still have an abnormal serum free light chain ratio, so the FLC assay (plus marrow biopsy/imaging) makes the diagnosis. Mnemonic: 'silent spike, loud light chains.'
⚠️ WRONG answer: ruling out myeloma because SPEP/UPEP show no M-spike. The discriminator is nonsecretory/light-chain-only disease — order serum free light chains and a marrow biopsy; absence of an electrophoretic spike does NOT exclude myeloma.
14. All MM preceded by MGUS
Essentially all multiple myeloma is preceded by an asymptomatic MGUS phase (shown by stored-sample studies), often progressing through smoldering myeloma — it is a biologic continuum, not a sudden event. This underlies the rationale for surveillance and for emerging early-intervention trials in high-risk precursors. Mnemonic: MGUS → SMM → MM is one escalator, not three separate buildings.
⚠️ WRONG answer: assuming myeloma arises de novo and that a prior 'benign' MGUS is unrelated. The discriminator is the established precursor continuum — a known MGUS patient who develops CRAB has progressed along that same clone, which is why baseline characterization and monitoring matter.
