📖 Read-through — all 6 symbols
1. VT criteria scorecard
Features favoring VT in a wide-complex tachycardia: AV dissociation, cannon A waves, capture/fusion beats, very wide QRS (>140 ms RBBB / >160 LBBB), precordial concordance, northwest axis, and — most powerful — a history of structural heart disease/prior MI.
⚠️ WRONG: 'a regular wide-complex tachycardia in an older patient with prior MI is probably SVT with aberrancy.' Structural heart disease makes VT the default — treat as VT.
2. Stability does NOT rule out VT
Many VT patients are hemodynamically stable; tolerating the rhythm does not make it SVT. Diagnose by ECG criteria and history, not by how well the patient looks.
⚠️ WRONG: 'the patient is stable, so it must be SVT.' Hemodynamic stability does not distinguish VT from SVT.
3. VT champ vs SVT challenger
When in doubt about a wide-complex tachycardia, assume VT (it's more common and more dangerous, especially with structural heart disease). Misdiagnosing VT as SVT and giving AV-nodal blockers can be fatal.
⚠️ WRONG: 'default to SVT with aberrancy when unsure.' Default to VT — it's safer and usually correct.
4. Unstable → cardioversion; never adenosine/verapamil
An unstable wide-complex tachycardia gets immediate synchronized cardioversion. Do NOT give verapamil/diltiazem (or rely on adenosine) for undiagnosed wide-complex tachycardia — verapamil can cause hemodynamic collapse in VT.
⚠️ WRONG: 'try verapamil first for a wide-complex tachycardia.' In VT it can precipitate shock — cardiovert if unstable; use procainamide/amiodarone if stable.
5. ARVC fighter
Arrhythmogenic RV cardiomyopathy causes VT with an LBBB morphology (RV origin) in a young patient; ECG shows epsilon waves and T-inversions in V1-V3, with fibrofatty RV replacement on imaging — a cause of exertional sudden death.
⚠️ WRONG: 'LBBB-morphology VT in a young athlete is benign RVOT VT.' Consider ARVC (structural, inherited, SCD risk) — not just idiopathic RVOT VT.
6. Stable monomorphic → procainamide > amiodarone > lidocaine
For stable monomorphic VT, procainamide was superior to amiodarone in the PROCAMIO trial; amiodarone is a common alternative, and lidocaine is reserved largely for ischemic VT. Always have cardioversion ready.
⚠️ WRONG: 'amiodarone is clearly first-line for stable monomorphic VT.' Procainamide outperformed amiodarone (PROCAMIO) for termination with fewer adverse events.
