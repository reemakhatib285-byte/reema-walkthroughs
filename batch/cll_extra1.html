📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Smudge cells
Smudge (basket) cells on the peripheral smear are characteristic of CLL: the fragile, mechanically weak CLL lymphocytes rupture during slide preparation, leaving smeared nuclear debris. Their abundance correlates with CLL (a high smudge-cell fraction favors CLL).
⚠️ Smudge cells are an artifact of fragile cells, not a distinct cell type — adding albumin to the smear reduces them, confirming they are processing artifacts.
2. Mature lymphocytes
CLL = monomorphic population of SMALL, MATURE-appearing B lymphocytes with clumped ("soccer-ball") chromatin, scant cytoplasm, and inconspicuous nucleoli — accumulation of long-lived non-dividing cells rather than proliferating blasts.
⚠️ NOT blasts — there are no Auer rods and no prominent nucleoli (which would suggest acute leukemia or prolymphocytic transformation if >55% prolymphocytes).
3. ≥5×10⁹/L B cells
CLL diagnosis requires ≥5×10⁹/L clonal B lymphocytes in the peripheral blood sustained for ≥3 months, with clonality confirmed by flow (light-chain restriction, kappa OR lambda). This blood threshold is what separates CLL from MBL and SLL.
⚠️ It is the clonal B-cell count, not the total lymphocyte count, that must be ≥5×10⁹/L.
4. CD5+
CLL B cells aberrantly co-express CD5 (normally a T-cell antigen) alongside B-cell markers. Full CLL immunophenotype: CD5+, CD19+, CD23+, CD20 dim, CD200+, and DIM surface immunoglobulin with light-chain restriction.
⚠️ CD5 is also positive in mantle cell lymphoma — CD5+ alone does not equal CLL; you must add CD23 and cyclin D1 to separate them.
5. CD23+
CLL is CD23 POSITIVE — a defining marker that, combined with CD5+ and dim surface Ig, supports CLL. The classic CLL panel is CD5+ / CD19+ / CD23+ / CD20-dim / FMC7-negative / dim sIg.
⚠️ CD23 NEGATIVE points to MANTLE CELL lymphoma, not CLL (the single most tested discriminator).
6. Cyclin D1 negative
CLL is cyclin D1 NEGATIVE and SOX11 negative — it lacks the t(11;14) translocation. This is the key confirmatory step when CD5+ B-cell disease is found.
⚠️ Cyclin D1 POSITIVE (with t(11;14) and SOX11+) = MANTLE CELL lymphoma, NOT CLL. CD5+/CD23−/cyclin D1+ = mantle cell; CD5+/CD23+/cyclin D1− = CLL.
7. Flow cytometry first
Peripheral-blood FLOW CYTOMETRY (immunophenotyping) is the first and definitive diagnostic step — it demonstrates the clonal CD5+CD19+CD23+ dim-sIg population, avoiding the need for tissue or marrow in most cases.
8. No marrow biopsy
Bone marrow biopsy is NOT required to diagnose CLL — peripheral blood flow suffices. Marrow is reserved for clarifying the cause of cytopenias (autoimmune vs marrow infiltration) or pre-treatment assessment, not for diagnosis.
⚠️ Don't order a marrow biopsy just to confirm CLL — the diagnosis is made on blood flow cytometry.
9. MBL <5×10⁹
Monoclonal B-cell lymphocytosis (MBL): clonal CLL-phenotype B cells <5×10⁹/L WITHOUT lymphadenopathy, organomegaly, cytopenias, or disease symptoms — a precursor state requiring only observation.
⚠️ MBL is defined by absence of disease features AND clonal B cells <5×10⁹/L; if any node/organomegaly/cytopenia is present it is CLL/SLL, not MBL.
10. SLL = tissue
Small lymphocytic lymphoma (SLL) is the SAME disease as CLL but presents in lymph nodes/tissue rather than blood: lymphadenopathy/organomegaly with <5×10⁹/L blood clonal B cells. Diagnosis requires lymph-node biopsy.
⚠️ CLL and SLL are one entity (CLL/SLL) differing only in compartment — same immunophenotype, staged/treated similarly.
11. 1-2%/yr progression
High-count MBL progresses to treatment-requiring CLL at roughly 1–2% per year, justifying periodic monitoring without therapy.
12. Mantle cell DDx
Mantle cell lymphoma is the chief mimic: CD5+ (like CLL) but CD23 NEGATIVE, cyclin D1 POSITIVE, SOX11+, with t(11;14)(IGH-CCND1). Distinguishing it from CLL is the highest-yield immunophenotyping point.
⚠️ A CD5+ B-cell lymphoproliferative disorder is NOT automatically CLL — check CD23 and cyclin D1 to exclude mantle cell.
13. Follicular DDx
Follicular lymphoma: CD5 NEGATIVE, CD10 POSITIVE (germinal-center marker), BCL2+ with t(14;18). Marginal zone lymphoma is CD5−/CD10−. These CD5-negative entities separate cleanly from CLL.
⚠️ CD10 positivity points to a germinal-center lymphoma (follicular/Burkitt/DLBCL-GCB), not CLL — CLL is CD10 negative.
14. Incidental lymphocytosis
Most CLL is discovered incidentally as asymptomatic absolute lymphocytosis on a routine CBC; the diagnostic algorithm is incidental lymphocytosis → flow cytometry → clonal B cells ≥5×10⁹/L + typical phenotype → CLL confirmed without marrow biopsy.
