📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. HPA axis = three floors
The HPA axis runs hypothalamus (CRH) → pituitary (ACTH) → adrenal (cortisol). The level of the lesion (which floor) defines primary, secondary, or tertiary insufficiency and the lab pattern.
⚠️ Localize by ACTH and aldosterone: only the adrenal (primary) level loses aldosterone and raises ACTH.
2. CRH from hypothalamus
CRH from the hypothalamus drives pituitary ACTH. Tertiary insufficiency = loss of CRH, most often from chronic exogenous steroids suppressing the hypothalamus.
⚠️ Tertiary AI is usually iatrogenic (chronic steroid use), not a hypothalamic tumor.
3. ACTH from pituitary
ACTH derives from POMC, which also yields melanocyte-stimulating hormone (MSH). In primary AI, high ACTH drives high MSH → hyperpigmentation. Low ACTH (secondary/tertiary) gives no pigment.
⚠️ Hyperpigmentation occurs ONLY in primary AI (high ACTH/MSH); pale skin points to secondary/tertiary.
4. Cortisol + aldosterone from adrenal
The adrenal cortex makes cortisol (ACTH-driven) and aldosterone (RAAS/renin-driven, independent of ACTH). This independence is why aldosterone is lost only in primary AI.
⚠️ Aldosterone is controlled by RAAS, not ACTH — so secondary/tertiary AI preserves aldosterone.
5. Money table — primary vs sec vs tert
Primary: cortisol low, ACTH HIGH, aldosterone low, Na low, K high, hyperpigment +. Secondary/tertiary: cortisol low, ACTH low/normal, aldosterone normal, Na low (from ADH), K normal, no pigment.
⚠️ Hyperkalemia + hyperpigmentation + high ACTH = primary (Addison); normal K + pale = central.
6. First zone: primary (Addison)
Primary AI (Addison) = destruction of the adrenal cortex (autoimmune in the West, TB worldwide). High ACTH, low cortisol AND low aldosterone → salt craving, hypotension, hyperkalemia, hyperpigmentation.
⚠️ Only primary AI loses aldosterone → hyperkalemia and salt craving; central forms do not.
7. Second zone: secondary
Secondary AI = pituitary failure of ACTH (tumor, surgery, Sheehan, apoplexy). Cortisol low, ACTH low, aldosterone NORMAL (RAAS intact). No hyperpigmentation; K normal; Na may be low from cortisol-driven ADH.
⚠️ Secondary AI does NOT cause hyperkalemia — aldosterone is preserved. Skin is pale, not pigmented.
8. Third zone: tertiary (chronic steroids)
Tertiary AI = suppressed hypothalamic CRH from prolonged exogenous glucocorticoids; abrupt steroid withdrawal precipitates crisis. Labs mirror secondary (low ACTH, preserved aldosterone).
⚠️ The single most common cause of AI overall is exogenous steroid withdrawal — taper, never stop abruptly.
9. Hyponatremia in BOTH
Hyponatremia occurs in all forms: in primary from aldosterone loss (Na wasting) AND cortisol loss raising ADH; in secondary/tertiary purely from cortisol-deficiency-driven ADH excess. Potassium separates them.
⚠️ Low Na is common to all AI; use POTASSIUM (high in primary, normal centrally) to localize, not sodium.
10. Hyperpigmentation sites
Primary AI hyperpigmentation is most visible at palmar creases, knuckles, scars, and buccal mucosa due to MSH from elevated POMC/ACTH. Its presence essentially rules out central AI.
⚠️ Buccal/palmar-crease pigmentation = primary AI; its absence with low cortisol suggests central cause.
11. ADH up in primary
In primary AI, cortisol deficiency removes tonic inhibition of ADH, so ADH rises and worsens hyponatremia on top of aldosterone-driven sodium loss.
⚠️ Cortisol loss itself raises ADH — explains hyponatremia even when aldosterone is intact (central AI).
12. Aldosterone preserved centrally
Because aldosterone answers to RAAS not ACTH, central AI keeps potassium and acid-base normal; only cortisol-dependent water handling is off → mild hyponatremia, normal K.
⚠️ Don't expect hyperkalemia or metabolic acidosis in secondary/tertiary AI — aldosterone protects them.
13. Tertiary cause = steroid history
A documented course of chronic glucocorticoids (oral, inhaled high-dose, intra-articular) with recent taper/stop is the cue for tertiary AI; cosyntropin testing may be falsely reassuring early.
⚠️ Recent steroid taper presenting with crisis = tertiary AI — give stress-dose hydrocortisone immediately.
14. Clinical pearl banner
Pearl: primary = adrenal destruction (high ACTH, hyperpigmentation, hyperkalemia, low aldosterone). Secondary/tertiary = low ACTH, no pigment, no hyperkalemia, aldosterone preserved.
⚠️ The whole scene reduces to: ACTH direction + potassium + skin pigment localize the lesion level.
