📖 Read-through — all 11 symbols
1. Erythema nodosum (X)
Erythema nodosum (tender red shin nodules) is NOT a stigma of cirrhosis — it points to IBD, sarcoidosis, infection (strep, TB), or drugs. Don't pick it as a liver sign. The true cirrhosis stigmata come from hyperestrogenism and portal hypertension.
⚠️ "Erythema nodosum is a stigma of chronic liver disease" → wrong; it signals IBD/sarcoidosis, not cirrhosis.
2. Jaundice
Jaundice (scleral icterus when bilirubin >2-3 mg/dL) reflects impaired bilirubin conjugation/excretion in advanced liver disease. A component of Child-Pugh scoring. Worsening jaundice signals decompensation.
3. Spider angiomata
Spider angiomata (central arteriole with radiating vessels, blanch with pressure) result from hyperestrogenemia; numerous/large lesions correlate with severity and variceal risk. Found in the SVC distribution (face, upper chest, arms).
4. Palmar erythema
Palmar erythema (red thenar/hypothenar eminences) is another hyperestrogenism sign. Non-specific (also pregnancy, hyperthyroidism, RA) but supports chronic liver disease in context.
5. Gynecomastia
Gynecomastia, testicular atrophy, and loss of body hair stem from altered estrogen/androgen balance (reduced hepatic clearance of estrogens). Spironolactone used for ascites can worsen gynecomastia.
6. Caput medusae
Caput medusae = dilated periumbilical veins from portal hypertension recanalizing the umbilical vein. A portosystemic collateral, like esophageal varices and rectal varices. Reflects elevated portal pressure.
7. Dupuytren contracture
Dupuytren contracture (palmar fascia thickening flexing the ring/little fingers) is associated with alcoholic liver disease (also diabetes, manual labor). Part of the stigmata cluster on exam.
8. HRS — stop diuretics + albumin
Hepatorenal syndrome is a DIAGNOSIS OF EXCLUSION: AKI with intense renal vasoconstriction, bland urine (no protein/casts), no improvement after stopping diuretics and a 48h albumin challenge (1 g/kg/day). Rule out hypovolemia, ATN, obstruction first. The reflex must be STOP diuretics + give albumin, never more diuretics.
⚠️ "Treat hepatorenal syndrome with more diuretics/furosemide" → wrong; STOP diuretics, give albumin challenge, then vasoconstrictors. Diuretics worsen the prerenal physiology.
9. Terlipressin + albumin
After albumin, treat HRS with a vasoconstrictor + albumin: terlipressin (preferred, watch for ischemia/respiratory failure) or noradrenaline (ICU) or midodrine + octreotide where terlipressin unavailable. These reverse splanchnic vasodilation and improve renal perfusion as a bridge.
10. Transplant best (gold star)
Liver transplantation is the DEFINITIVE and best treatment for hepatorenal syndrome (gold star) — pharmacotherapy is only a bridge. HRS portends very poor survival without transplant. Renal function often recovers post-transplant.
11. Hepatopulmonary — platypnea
Hepatopulmonary syndrome = triad of liver disease + hypoxemia + intrapulmonary vascular dilations. Hallmark is PLATYPNEA-ORTHODEOXIA: dyspnea/desaturation WORSE when UPRIGHT (dilations are basal, more shunting standing). Diagnose with contrast (bubble) echocardiography showing delayed left-heart opacification. Definitive treatment is transplant.
