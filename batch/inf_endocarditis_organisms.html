📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Valve-throne vegetation
The vegetation is a friable mass of platelets, fibrin, and microcolonies of organisms on a valve leaflet, sitting in an avascular niche where neutrophils and antibiotics penetrate poorly, so bacteria reach 10^9–10^11/gram. It forms on the atrial side of the mitral and ventricular side of the aortic valve (low-pressure side of the regurgitant jet) and drives both continuous bacteremia and embolization. Endothelial injury from turbulent jets first lays down a sterile thrombus that a transient bacteremia then colonizes.
2. ORAL door — viridans strep
The oral cavity is the #1 portal (~40%). Viridans group strep (S. sanguinis, S. mutans, S. mitis) are alpha-hemolytic, optochin-RESISTANT oral flora that produce dextran to adhere to valves and cause SUBACUTE IE on a previously ABNORMAL valve after dental work or poor dentition. Penicillin-susceptible strains: penicillin G or ceftriaxone x4 weeks (2 weeks if gentamicin synergy added).
3. Green viridans army
Viridans strep cause the classic indolent subacute course — low-grade fever, weight loss, night sweats — on rheumatic, myxomatous (MVP), bicuspid, or degenerative valves. They need a pre-roughened endothelium because, unlike S. aureus, they lack adhesins for intact endothelium. Optochin RESISTANCE separates viridans from S. pneumoniae.
4. SKIN door — S. aureus / IVDU
Skin/vascular access (~33%, second portal): IV drug use, central lines, hemodialysis catheters. S. aureus (GPC clusters, catalase+, coagulase+) is the #1 OVERALL cause and the leading healthcare-associated cause; its MSCRAMM adhesins (clumping factor, fibronectin-binding protein) let it invade NORMAL valves, producing ACUTE, rapidly destructive IE with abscess and embolism. Any S. aureus bacteremia mandates echo. MSSA → nafcillin/oxacillin/cefazolin; MRSA → vancomycin or daptomycin.
5. TRICUSPID lungs door
In people who inject drugs, S. aureus dominates and the TRICUSPID valve is hit first because venous blood reaches it first. Right-sided IE throws SEPTIC PULMONARY emboli — multiple peripheral cavitary nodules, pleuritic chest pain, cough — and is often murmurless. Right-sided S. aureus IE may respond to medical therapy alone unless vegetations are large or infection persists.
6. No systemic/brain emboli (red-X brain)
Right-sided (tricuspid) vegetations embolize to the LUNGS, not the systemic arteries, so brain/systemic emboli and a loud left-sided regurgitant murmur are NOT the IVDU picture. Chest CT with multiple cavitary nodules in an injector = septic pulmonary emboli.
⚠️ 'IVDU endocarditis causes brain emboli and a loud regurgitant murmur' -> wrong; right-sided tricuspid disease seeds the LUNGS (septic pulmonary emboli), is frequently murmurless, and spares the systemic/cerebral circulation — that is exactly why the brain is X'd out.
7. SEROLOGY alcove (culture-negative)
Culture-negative IE (~5–10%) comes from fastidious/intracellular/zoonotic bugs or prior antibiotics: Coxiella (Q fever — livestock), Bartonella (cats/homeless), Brucella (unpasteurized dairy), Tropheryma whipplei, fungi. Diagnosis is by SEROLOGY/PCR of valve tissue, not routine culture.
⚠️ 'Order more blood cultures to grow the organism' -> wrong for these bugs; Coxiella, Bartonella, and Brucella are culture-NEGATIVE on routine media and are diagnosed by serology/PCR — repeating cultures just delays care.
8. Coxiella — sheep / Q fever
Coxiella burnetii (Q fever) is an intracellular, culture-negative cause of chronic IE linked to cattle/sheep/goats (parturient animals) and prosthetic valves. Phase I IgG ≥1:800 is a STANDALONE major Duke criterion. Treat with doxycycline 100 mg BID + hydroxychloroquine (alkalinizes the phagolysosome so doxy becomes bactericidal) for 18–24 months with serial titers.
9. Bartonella — cat
Bartonella is a fastidious, culture-NEGATIVE gram-negative cause of IE: B. henselae from cats (scratch/flea), B. quintana from homelessness and the body louse. Diagnose by high IgG titer (major criterion) or valve PCR. Treat with doxycycline + gentamicin ± ceftriaxone; valve surgery is often required. Sort culture-negative IE by exposure: cats → henselae, homeless/lice → quintana, livestock → Coxiella.
10. Early PVE (<2 mo) — CoNS
Coagulase-NEGATIVE staph (chiefly S. epidermidis) is the #1 cause of EARLY prosthetic valve endocarditis (within ~2–12 months of surgery), seeded perioperatively and forming methicillin-resistant biofilm on the prosthetic material. Treat prosthetic staph IE with vancomycin + RIFAMPIN (biofilm penetration) + gentamicin for ≥6 weeks.
⚠️ 'A single CoNS-positive culture is always a contaminant' -> wrong in a prosthetic-valve patient; CoNS is the leading EARLY PVE pathogen and the prime suspect, whereas in a native-valve patient one positive set is usually contamination.
11. Late PVE = community organisms
LATE prosthetic valve endocarditis (>12 months postop) is caused by the SAME organisms as community native-valve IE — viridans strep, S. aureus, enterococci, HACEK — because the prosthesis is endothelialized and seeding is now hematogenous (dental, GU). Timing splits the differential: EARLY (<12 mo) = CoNS/S. aureus biofilm; LATE (>12 mo) = community flora.
⚠️ 'All prosthetic-valve IE is coagulase-negative staph' -> wrong; only EARLY PVE is CoNS/S. aureus — LATE PVE mirrors community native-valve organisms (strep, enterococcus, HACEK).
12. S. gallolyticus → COLON
Streptococcus gallolyticus subsp. gallolyticus (formerly S. bovis biotype I) is a GI-tract gram-positive coccus whose IE has a strong association with colorectal NEOPLASIA. Any S. gallolyticus/bovis bacteremia or IE MANDATES colonoscopy to exclude colorectal cancer. Mnemonic: 'bovis in the blood, cancer in the colon.'
⚠️ 'Treat S. bovis IE with antibiotics and discharge' -> wrong; S. gallolyticus (bovis) IE obligates COLONOSCOPY to find an underlying colorectal cancer — skipping the cancer workup is the classic miss.
13. Enterococcus — GU source (red-X knight)
Enterococcus (E. faecalis >> E. faecium) is a GI/GU gram-positive coccus causing IE in OLDER MEN after genitourinary instrumentation (and women after obstetric procedures). It is intrinsically antibiotic-tolerant, so cure needs SYNERGISTIC combination — ampicillin + ceftriaxone (preferred, renal-sparing) or ampicillin + gentamicin, never monotherapy.
⚠️ 'Young patient with enterococcal IE — look for colon cancer' -> wrong; the colon-cancer organism is S. gallolyticus/bovis. Enterococcus points to a GU/GI source in OLDER post-instrumentation patients, hence the red X.
14. HACEK — grim reaper / crabs
HACEK = Haemophilus/Aggregatibacter aphrophilus, Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans, Cardiobacterium hominis, Eikenella corrodens, Kingella kingae — fastidious gram-negative coccobacilli of oral/respiratory flora causing subacute IE, sometimes with large vegetations. First-line: ceftriaxone (they may make beta-lactamase). With modern automated culture systems they grow within the standard ~5-day incubation.
⚠️ 'HACEK is culture-negative and needs weeks of special incubation' -> wrong; modern blood-culture systems recover HACEK within ~5 days, prolonged incubation is no longer required, and ceftriaxone is the treatment of choice.
