📖 Read-through — all 17 symbols
1. Iodine deficiency worldwide
Iodine deficiency is the most common cause of hypothyroidism (and goiter) WORLDWIDE — iodine is the substrate for T3/T4, so deficiency limits synthesis, raises TSH, and causes goiter. In endemic deficiency it also causes endemic cretinism in newborns. Salt iodization is the public-health countermeasure.
⚠️ Calling Hashimoto's the #1 cause worldwide — wrong; Hashimoto's is #1 only in iodine-SUFFICIENT regions. Globally, iodine deficiency leads.
2. >95% autoimmune (Hashimoto)
In iodine-sufficient countries, the leading cause of hypothyroidism is Hashimoto's (chronic autoimmune lymphocytic) thyroiditis — T-cell-mediated destruction with lymphocytic infiltration and germinal centers (Hürthle/Askanazy cells on histology). It produces a firm, non-tender goiter and gradual gland failure; associated with HLA and other autoimmune diseases.
⚠️ Forgetting the geographic dependence — in iodine-sufficient areas autoimmune (Hashimoto's) dominates; worldwide it is iodine deficiency.
3. Anti-TPO antibody
Hashimoto's is marked by anti-thyroid peroxidase (anti-TPO) antibodies — the most sensitive marker — and anti-thyroglobulin antibodies. Anti-TPO positivity also predicts progression of subclinical hypothyroidism to overt disease and post-partum thyroiditis.
⚠️ Naming TRAb as the Hashimoto antibody — wrong; TRAb (TSI) is the Graves' stimulating antibody. Hashimoto's = anti-TPO / anti-thyroglobulin.
4. Hashimoto → lymphoma
Long-standing Hashimoto's is the substrate for primary thyroid lymphoma (typically MALT or diffuse large B-cell), presenting as a rapidly enlarging neck mass in an older patient. Diagnosis is by biopsy; treatment is chemotherapy/radiation, NOT surgery.
⚠️ Assuming a rapidly growing mass in a Hashimoto patient is anaplastic carcinoma and rushing to surgery — biopsy first; lymphoma is treated medically and is far more curable.
5. APS-2 association
Autoimmune polyglandular syndrome type 2 (Schmidt syndrome): autoimmune thyroid disease + Addison's disease ± type 1 diabetes, plus other organ-specific autoimmunity (pernicious anemia, vitiligo, celiac). Adult onset, polygenic/HLA-linked. Crucially, in a patient with both, treat Addison's (give glucocorticoid) BEFORE starting levothyroxine.
⚠️ Starting levothyroxine before recognizing/treating coexisting Addison's — wrong; accelerating metabolism with thyroid hormone in untreated adrenal insufficiency can precipitate adrenal crisis. Treat cortisol first.
6. BIOTIN TRAP
High-dose biotin (B7, common in hair/nail supplements) interferes with streptavidin-biotin immunoassays, producing a FALSE pattern of low TSH with high free T4/T3 — mimicking Graves' (and can also falsely lower TRAb). Stop biotin ~2–3 days (≥48 h) before testing and recheck.
⚠️ Treating a biotin-induced lab pattern as real hyperthyroidism — wrong and dangerous; the clue is a clinically euthyroid patient on supplements. Recheck off biotin before acting.
7. Delayed relaxation reflex
The 'hung-up' (delayed-relaxation) deep tendon reflex — slow return phase, classically at the ankle — is a hallmark physical sign of hypothyroidism caused by slowed muscle contraction/relaxation kinetics.
⚠️ Mistaking the slow relaxation phase for a normal or hyperreflexic response — in hyperthyroidism reflexes are brisk/quick, the opposite finding.
8. Myxedema / periorbital edema
Generalized myxedema: deposition of hydrophilic glycosaminoglycans (hyaluronic acid) in the dermis causes NON-pitting, doughy edema, periorbital puffiness, macroglossia, and a hoarse voice. Severe decompensation → myxedema coma (hypothermia, hypoventilation, hyponatremia, altered mental status) — treat with IV levothyroxine + stress-dose steroids.
⚠️ Confusing generalized myxedema of HYPOthyroidism with PRETIBIAL myxedema (a Graves'/HYPERthyroid dermopathy on the shins) — same word, opposite disease.
9. Menorrhagia
Hypothyroidism causes menstrual disturbance — classically menorrhagia (and oligo-/anovulation, infertility) — partly via altered GnRH/prolactin dynamics (TRH can raise prolactin). Untreated hypothyroidism is a reversible cause of subfertility.
⚠️ Working up heavy menstrual bleeding without checking TSH — hypothyroidism is a treatable, easily missed cause.
10. Carpal tunnel
Hypothyroidism is a classic REVERSIBLE cause of carpal tunnel syndrome — glycosaminoglycan deposition and soft-tissue swelling compress the median nerve in the carpal tunnel. Symptoms improve with thyroid replacement.
⚠️ Going straight to carpal tunnel surgery without checking for treatable systemic causes such as hypothyroidism (and pregnancy, diabetes, acromegaly).
11. ↑CPK & ↑lipids
Hypothyroid metabolic labs: elevated creatine kinase (myopathy from slowed muscle metabolism) and hyperlipidemia — high LDL and triglycerides from reduced LDL-receptor expression and clearance. Lipids improve once euthyroid.
⚠️ Attributing an elevated CK to a statin myopathy or rhabdomyolysis without checking TSH, or starting lifelong lipid therapy before correcting the reversible hypothyroid dyslipidemia.
12. Hyponatremia & anemia
Hypothyroidism causes hyponatremia (reduced free-water clearance, SIADH-like) and anemia that is typically normocytic, but can be macrocytic — and remember Hashimoto associates with pernicious anemia (B12 deficiency → true macrocytic) via APS. Microcytic anemia may coexist if menorrhagia causes iron deficiency.
⚠️ Chasing macrocytic anemia as purely B12/folate without considering hypothyroidism itself (and the Hashimoto–pernicious anemia association).
13. Drug causes (amiodarone, lithium)
Drug- and iatrogenic causes: amiodarone (iodine load → Wolff-Chaikoff-mediated hypothyroidism, type 1; can also cause thyrotoxicosis), lithium (blocks hormone release), iodine excess or deficiency, plus post-radioiodine, post-thyroidectomy, and external neck radiation. Also checkpoint inhibitors (immune thyroiditis).
⚠️ Forgetting amiodarone can cause EITHER hypo- OR hyperthyroidism — do not assume direction; the iodine load drives hypothyroidism in many patients but amiodarone-induced thyrotoxicosis also occurs.
14. Non-adherence detective
A treated patient with persistently HIGH TSH but NORMAL/high free T4 suggests erratic adherence — the patient takes several missed pills just before the blood draw, normalizing T4 while the chronically under-replaced state keeps TSH elevated (long TSH lag). Confirms a behavioral, not pharmacologic, problem.
⚠️ Escalating the levothyroxine dose for this 'high TSH, normal T4' pattern — wrong; it reflects non-adherence (and the long TSH half-life), so increasing dose risks toxicity once the patient takes it consistently. Address adherence first.
15. Constipation
Slowed gut motility from thyroid hormone deficiency causes constipation — part of the global hypometabolic slowing (bradycardia, cold intolerance, fatigue, weight gain). It is the mirror image of the hyperdefecation/diarrhea of thyrotoxicosis.
⚠️ Treating chronic constipation symptomatically without checking TSH in a patient with other hypometabolic features.
16. Dry skin / hair loss
Hypothyroidism produces dry, coarse, cool skin, brittle hair, and diffuse hair thinning including the lateral third of the eyebrows (Queen Anne's sign / madarosis) — from reduced skin metabolism, sweating, and sebum.
⚠️ Attributing the skin/hair changes to age or dermatologic disease alone and missing the reversible endocrine cause.
17. Pituitary hyperplasia mimic
Severe long-standing primary hypothyroidism causes thyrotroph (and lactotroph) HYPERPLASIA from loss of negative feedback (high TRH/TSH drive), which can enlarge the pituitary and mimic an adenoma on MRI — and raise prolactin. The 'mass' SHRINKS with thyroid hormone replacement; it is not a true tumor.
⚠️ Sending a child/young patient with an enlarged pituitary from untreated hypothyroidism to pituitary surgery — wrong; treat the hypothyroidism and the pituitary regresses. Confirm TSH before imaging-driven decisions.
