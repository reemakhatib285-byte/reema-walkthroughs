📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Alveolar gas equation
Alveolar gas equation: PAO2 = FiO2×(Patm−PH2O) − PaCO2/R ≈ 150 − PaCO2/0.8 at sea level on room air. It predicts alveolar O2, the reference for calculating the A-a gradient.
⚠️ Rising PaCO2 (hypoventilation) lowers PAO2 mathematically — so hypoventilation causes hypoxemia with a NORMAL A-a gradient.
2. A-a gradient gauge
A-a gradient = PAO2 (calculated) − PaO2 (measured). Normal ≈ 5-15 (rises with age: ~age/4 + 4). A normal gradient localizes the problem to low inspired O2 or hypoventilation; a widened gradient indicates lung pathology.
⚠️ A normal A-a gradient narrows the cause to hypoventilation or high altitude — the other three mechanisms all WIDEN the gradient.
3. Low inspired O2 / altitude
High altitude/low FiO2 lowers PiO2 and thus PAO2, causing hypoxemia with a NORMAL A-a gradient. Corrects readily with supplemental O2. Triggers hypoxic ventilatory drive → respiratory alkalosis.
⚠️ Altitude hypoxemia has a normal A-a gradient — same category as hypoventilation, distinct from V/Q, shunt, and diffusion.
4. Hypoventilation
Hypoventilation raises PaCO2, which lowers PAO2 and PaO2, giving hypoxemia with a NORMAL A-a gradient that corrects with O2. Causes: CNS depression (opioids), neuromuscular disease, obesity hypoventilation, chest wall disorders.
⚠️ Hypoventilation is the only hypoxemia mechanism that always RAISES PaCO2 — high CO2 with normal A-a gradient points straight to it.
5. V/Q mismatch (most common)
V/Q mismatch is the MOST COMMON cause of hypoxemia (COPD, asthma, PE, pneumonia, interstitial disease). Widened A-a gradient; hypoxemia IMPROVES with supplemental O2 because some perfused alveoli still ventilate.
⚠️ V/Q mismatch corrects with O2, distinguishing it from true shunt which does NOT correct — the oxygen-response test separates them.
6. CO2 rise in V/Q
Severe/widespread V/Q mismatch can eventually raise CO2 when ventilation cannot compensate, but early on CO2 is normal/low due to compensatory hyperventilation of healthy units.
⚠️ Early V/Q mismatch often shows LOW CO2 (hyperventilation) — a normal or high CO2 in a hypoxic COPD patient signals decompensation.
7. Shunt (refractory)
True shunt: blood bypasses ventilated alveoli (atelectasis, pneumonia consolidation, ARDS, pulmonary AVM, intracardiac R→L shunt). Wide A-a gradient and hypoxemia REFRACTORY to 100% O2 — the defining feature.
⚠️ Failure to correct with 100% O2 = shunt, not V/Q mismatch — this single test is the classic board discriminator.
8. Shunt does not correct with O2
In shunt, supplemental O2 cannot reach the bypassed blood, so PaO2 stays low despite high FiO2. Treat the underlying lung collapse/consolidation (PEEP, recruitment) rather than just increasing FiO2.
⚠️ A shunt fraction >30-50% makes oxygen therapy nearly futile — escalate to PEEP/mechanical recruitment, not just more O2.
9. Diffusion limitation
Diffusion impairment (interstitial lung disease, emphysema, pulmonary fibrosis) widens the A-a gradient; hypoxemia worsens with exercise and improves with supplemental O2. Reduced DLCO on PFTs.
⚠️ Diffusion-limited hypoxemia is often only apparent on EXERTION — resting ABG can be normal in early ILD; check exercise desaturation/DLCO.
10. O2 test (response to oxygen)
The 100% O2 challenge classifies hypoxemia: corrects in low-FiO2, hypoventilation, V/Q mismatch, and diffusion; does NOT fully correct in shunt. This is the key bedside discriminator.
⚠️ Only TRUE SHUNT fails to correct with 100% O2 — all four other mechanisms improve, making the O2 test diagnostic.
11. Dead space (jail cell)
Dead space = ventilation without perfusion (V/Q → infinity), classically pulmonary embolism. Wasted ventilation raises required minute ventilation; in pure dead space, gas exchange units are ventilated but not perfused.
⚠️ PE causes BOTH dead space (occluded segments) and hypoxemia via V/Q mismatch/shunt in surrounding lung — not pure dead space alone.
12. Shunt (jail cell)
Shunt = perfusion without ventilation (V/Q → 0), the opposite extreme of dead space. Blood passes unventilated alveoli (atelectasis, ARDS) and stays deoxygenated, producing refractory hypoxemia.
⚠️ Dead space (V/Q=∞) and shunt (V/Q=0) are the two V/Q EXTREMES — confusing which has perfusion vs ventilation is a common error.
13. V/Q extremes spectrum
The lineup represents the V/Q continuum: dead space (high V/Q, no perfusion) on one end, shunt (low V/Q, no ventilation) on the other, with normal matching in between. Most disease lies along this spectrum.
⚠️ Pure shunt and pure dead space are extremes; most real disease is intermediate V/Q mismatch — the commonest hypoxemia mechanism overall.
14. Hypoxemia vs hypoxia
Hypoxemia = low arterial PaO2/SaO2 (a blood measurement); hypoxia = inadequate tissue O2 delivery. Hypoxia can occur with normal PaO2 (anemia, CO poisoning, cyanide, shock).
⚠️ CO poisoning and anemia cause tissue HYPOXIA with a NORMAL PaO2 and normal pulse-ox — measure co-oximetry, not just SpO2.
