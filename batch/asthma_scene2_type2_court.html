📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. Th2 allergic pathway
In the ALLERGIC (adaptive) pathway, dendritic cells present allergen to naive T cells, which (under IL-4 and alarmins) differentiate into Th2 cells. Th2 cells secrete the type-2 signature cytokines IL-4, IL-5, and IL-13, orchestrating IgE production, eosinophilia, and mucus/AHR. This phenotype is typically early-onset and atopic.
2. IL-4 queen → IgE class switch
IL-4 drives naive T-cell differentiation toward Th2 and induces B-cell immunoglobulin class-switching to IgE. It signals through the type-I and type-II receptors that share the IL-4Rα chain (the target of dupilumab). IL-4 = the IgE/allergic-commitment cytokine.
⚠️ Cytokine-to-effect pairing is heavily tested: IL-4 → IgE class switch / Th2 differentiation. Don't confuse it with IL-5 (eosinophils) or IL-13 (mucus/FeNO).
3. IL-5 king → eosinophil survival
IL-5 is the master cytokine for eosinophils — it drives their maturation in the marrow, recruitment, activation, and survival (anti-apoptosis). Mepolizumab and reslizumab neutralize IL-5; benralizumab targets the IL-5 receptor alpha (IL-5Rα) and additionally depletes eosinophils via ADCC.
⚠️ IL-5 = eosinophils. Anti-IL-5 biologics only work in patients with ELEVATED blood eosinophils (e.g., ≥300/µL) — they are useless in non-eosinophilic asthma, a classic distractor.
4. IL-13 → mucus + goblet + AHR/FeNO
IL-13 (closely related to IL-4, also using IL-4Rα) drives goblet-cell metaplasia and mucus hypersecretion, airway hyperresponsiveness, subepithelial fibrosis, and — by inducing epithelial iNOS — raises FeNO. It also contributes to IgE switching.
⚠️ IL-13 = mucus + AHR + FeNO. FeNO tracks IL-13/type-2 activity and predicts response to ICS and to dupilumab (which blocks IL-4Rα and therefore both IL-4 and IL-13).
5. Omalizumab archer (anti-IgE)
Omalizumab is a humanized monoclonal antibody that binds free IgE at its Cε3 (FcεRI-binding) site, lowering free IgE and down-regulating high-affinity receptors on mast cells/basophils. Indicated for moderate-severe ALLERGIC asthma; dose is based on body weight and baseline total IgE level. Also used in chronic urticaria.
⚠️ Omalizumab requires documented allergen sensitization AND an IgE level within the dosing range — it is the anti-IgE choice for ALLERGIC asthma and does not fit non-allergic eosinophilic disease.
6. Mast cell sensitization
IgE produced after class switching binds high-affinity FcεRI receptors on mast cells and basophils ('sensitization'). On re-exposure, allergen cross-links surface IgE, triggering degranulation (histamine, tryptase, leukotrienes) and the early allergic response — the step omalizumab interrupts.
7. Dupilumab blocks both (IL-4Rα)
Dupilumab targets the shared IL-4 receptor alpha (IL-4Rα) subunit, thereby blocking BOTH IL-4 and IL-13 signaling at once. It is highly effective in type-2 asthma (especially high FeNO and/or eosinophils), nasal polyposis, atopic dermatitis, and EoE.
⚠️ Dupilumab can cause a transient rise in blood eosinophils after initiation (usually asymptomatic and self-limited); rarely transient hypereosinophilia/EGPA-like presentations occur — generally benign, but a recognized board point. It is the biologic of choice when both IL-4 and IL-13 effects (high FeNO + eos + comorbid atopy) dominate.
8. CRTh2 / amplifies type 2
CRTh2 is the prostaglandin D2 (PGD2) receptor on Th2 cells, eosinophils, and ILC2s; PGD2 released from mast cells signals through it to chemoattract and activate these cells, amplifying the type-2 response. CRTh2 antagonists were investigated but have limited clinical role.
9. ILC2 non-allergic pathway
Group-2 innate lymphoid cells (ILC2) are the INNATE counterpart of Th2 cells: activated directly by the epithelial alarmins TSLP/IL-25/IL-33 (no antigen/IgE needed), they pour out IL-5 and IL-13, producing eosinophilic, high-FeNO asthma that is often late-onset and non-atopic.
⚠️ Eosinophilic asthma can be NON-allergic (ILC2-driven) — anti-IL-5 biologics still work, but omalizumab (anti-IgE) may NOT, because there is no IgE-mediated allergy to target.
10. Tezepelumab (anti-TSLP) upstream
Tezepelumab blocks TSLP, the most upstream epithelial alarmin, shutting off both the allergic (Th2) and non-allergic (ILC2) branches before they diverge. This gives efficacy across the widest range of phenotypes, including patients with LOW eosinophils and low FeNO.
⚠️ Tezepelumab is the BROADEST biologic — uniquely effective even with low type-2 biomarkers, making it the answer for severe asthma that is uncontrolled but does not have high eos/IgE/FeNO.
11. Neutrophilic / non-type 2 (steroid-resistant)
Non-type-2 neutrophilic asthma is driven by the Th17 axis (IL-17, IL-6, IL-8/CXCL8) recruiting neutrophils. It is linked to obesity, smoking, and severe/late-onset disease and characteristically responds poorly to inhaled or systemic corticosteroids.
⚠️ Type-2-targeted biologics (anti-IgE, anti-IL-5, anti-IL-4Rα) do NOT help neutrophilic asthma, and it is steroid-resistant — a frequent exam trap when low eosinophils/FeNO are given.
12. Leukotriene bench (LTRA / 5-LOX)
Cysteinyl leukotrienes (LTC4/D4/E4), made from arachidonic acid via 5-lipoxygenase, cause potent bronchoconstriction, mucus secretion, vascular leak, and eosinophil recruitment. Montelukast/zafirlukast block the CysLT1 receptor; zileuton inhibits 5-LOX (and needs LFT monitoring).
⚠️ Leukotriene modifiers are especially useful in AERD (aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease) and in exercise-induced bronchoconstriction; montelukast carries an FDA neuropsychiatric boxed warning.
