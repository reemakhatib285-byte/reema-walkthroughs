📖 Read-through — all 12 symbols
1. Creatinine produced at constant rate
Creatinine is generated at a steady rate from muscle creatine breakdown and is freely filtered with minimal tubular handling, making it a usable filtration marker — but only at steady state.
⚠️ Trusting serum Cr in non-steady-state AKI — when Cr is rising daily, no equation gives a valid GFR.
2. Muscle mass drives production
Creatinine generation is proportional to muscle mass. High muscle mass raises serum Cr independent of GFR; low muscle mass lowers it. This is the key confounder of all creatinine-based estimates.
⚠️ Reading a muscular patient's higher Cr as kidney disease — their GFR may be normal; their muscle mass is the driver.
3. Sarcopenia → false-normal Cr
Low muscle mass (elderly, sarcopenia, cachexia, amputees) produces little creatinine, so a 'normal' Cr can hide a markedly reduced GFR. Use cystatin C or measured clearance, and adjust drug dosing carefully.
⚠️ Reassuring a frail elderly patient with 'normal' Cr — their true GFR may be severely reduced (false-low Cr scenario).
4. Filtered freely
Creatinine is filtered freely at the glomerulus but a small amount is also secreted by proximal tubules, causing creatinine clearance to slightly overestimate true GFR. Drugs like trimethoprim/cimetidine block secretion and raise Cr without lowering GFR.
⚠️ Interpreting a Cr bump on trimethoprim/cimetidine as true GFR decline — they block tubular secretion, not filtration.
5. True GFR <30 needs caution
At low GFR, tubular secretion contributes proportionally more, so eGFR/creatinine clearance can substantially overestimate true GFR (an 'eGFR 65' may mask true GFR <30). Confirm advanced CKD with cystatin C or measured clearance.
⚠️ Trusting eGFR near the low range as accurate — it overestimates true GFR most when function is poor.
6. Cockcroft-Gault: drug dosing
Cockcroft-Gault = [(140−age) x weight x 0.85 if female] / (72 x SCr); estimates creatinine clearance (mL/min). It is the equation most drug package inserts are validated against, so use it for renal drug dosing.
⚠️ Dosing nephrotoxic/renally-cleared drugs off CKD-EPI eGFR — package-insert thresholds are based on Cockcroft-Gault.
7. MDRD better below 60
The MDRD equation was derived in CKD populations and performs best when eGFR is <60. It is reported as mL/min/1.73m2 (indexed to BSA) and underestimates at higher GFRs.
⚠️ Using MDRD to label mild reductions — it is unreliable and biased above 60; it shines below 60.
8. CKD-EPI most accurate overall
CKD-EPI is more accurate than MDRD across the GFR range, especially above 60, and is the preferred staging equation. Cystatin C–based CKD-EPI further reduces muscle-mass bias.
⚠️ Defaulting to MDRD for staging — CKD-EPI is the recommended, more accurate equation across the spectrum.
9. Steady state required
All creatinine equations assume steady-state kinetics. They are invalid in rapidly changing function (AKI), pregnancy, extremes of body size, or unusual diets. Use only when Cr is stable.
⚠️ Plugging a rapidly rising Cr into an eGFR formula — the result is meaningless in non-steady-state AKI.
10. AKI — creatinine rising daily
In AKI the serum creatinine lags behind the true fall in GFR and changes daily, so any eGFR is unreliable. Manage by the creatinine trend and clinical context, not a calculated GFR.
⚠️ Quoting an 'eGFR' during active AKI — the number underestimates how low GFR truly is and shouldn't guide dosing.
11. Formulas invalid in non-steady state
When steady-state is violated (AKI, dialysis, acute illness, large fluid shifts) estimating equations and even drug-dosing formulas break down — discard them and rely on measured clearance or clinical judgment.
⚠️ Continuing to dose by formula during acute changes — switch to clinical judgment / measured clearance instead.
12. Not fooled by muscle (drug-dosing worker)
Cystatin C is produced by all nucleated cells and is largely independent of muscle mass, so cystatin C–based eGFR is less confounded by sarcopenia or high muscularity. Use it to confirm when creatinine is unreliable.
⚠️ Relying solely on creatinine in extremes of muscle mass — cystatin C cross-checks and removes the muscle bias.
