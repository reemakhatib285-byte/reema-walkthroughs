📖 Read-through — all 15 symbols
1. Step 0: exclude exogenous steroids
Before any testing, exclude iatrogenic Cushing from exogenous glucocorticoids — the most common cause overall. Exogenous steroids give a Cushingoid body with LOW ACTH and LOW endogenous cortisol.
⚠️ Most common Cushing cause is exogenous steroids; suppressed ACTH AND low cortisol distinguish it from disease.
2. Need 2 positive screening tests
Confirm hypercortisolism with at least two abnormal first-line tests before localizing: 24h urinary free cortisol, late-night salivary cortisol, and/or 1 mg overnight DST. One positive test alone is insufficient.
⚠️ Don't localize off a single abnormal test — require two concordant positives to confirm Cushing.
3. 24h urinary free cortisol
Elevated 24-hour urinary free cortisol (often >3–4x ULN) reflects integrated daily cortisol production and is a core screening test. Collection errors and high fluid intake can confound.
⚠️ A markedly high UFC (>3x ULN) is strong evidence; mild elevations need a second confirmatory test.
4. Late-night salivary cortisol
Cushing abolishes the normal nighttime cortisol nadir, so an elevated late-night salivary cortisol is sensitive. Two collections increase reliability.
⚠️ Loss of the late-night cortisol nadir is the hallmark; a normal late-night value argues against Cushing.
5. 1 mg overnight DST
After 1 mg dexamethasone at 11 pm, morning cortisol >1.8 µg/dL (50 nmol/L) fails to suppress and screens positive. False positives occur with estrogen, CYP inducers, and depression.
⚠️ Cortisol >1.8 after low-dose dex = fails to suppress = abnormal; estrogen/CBG can cause false positives.
6. Plasma ACTH sorts the cause
Once hypercortisolism is confirmed, measure plasma ACTH. LOW/suppressed ACTH (<5 pg/mL) = ACTH-independent (adrenal). HIGH/normal ACTH = ACTH-dependent (pituitary or ectopic).
⚠️ ACTH direction is the master sort: low = adrenal source, high = pituitary/ectopic.
7. ACTH < 5 → adrenal (independent)
Suppressed ACTH points to a primary adrenal source — adenoma, carcinoma, or bilateral hyperplasia. Proceed to adrenal CT, not pituitary MRI.
⚠️ Low ACTH means go to adrenal imaging; ordering a pituitary MRI here is the wrong next step.
8. ACTH > 15 → pituitary/ectopic
Elevated/normal ACTH means an ACTH-dependent cause. Next distinguish pituitary (Cushing disease, ~80%) from ectopic ACTH (small-cell lung, carcinoid) using high-dose dex, CRH, and imaging.
⚠️ High ACTH still requires separating pituitary from ectopic — don't stop at 'ACTH-dependent.'
9. High-dose dex test
High-dose dex suppresses cortisol in most pituitary tumors (retain feedback) but NOT in ectopic sources. CRH stimulation raises ACTH/cortisol in pituitary disease, not ectopic.
⚠️ Pituitary tumors suppress with high-dose dex and respond to CRH; ectopic tumors do neither.
10. Pituitary MRI
Pituitary MRI is obtained when ACTH is high and dynamic testing suggests Cushing disease. Microadenomas <6 mm may be invisible, so a negative MRI does not exclude pituitary source.
⚠️ A normal pituitary MRI does NOT rule out Cushing disease — proceed to IPSS when MRI is small/negative.
11. IPSS — gold standard
When MRI and dynamic tests disagree, inferior petrosal sinus sampling settles pituitary vs ectopic. A central-to-peripheral ACTH gradient (≥2 baseline, ≥3 after CRH) confirms pituitary source. IPSS wins ties.
⚠️ IPSS is the tiebreaker — a positive central gradient confirms pituitary even with a negative MRI.
12. Ectopic ACTH — find the tumor
Ectopic ACTH (small-cell lung cancer, bronchial carcinoid, MTC) gives very high ACTH/cortisol, severe hypokalemia, and rapid onset. Localize with chest/abdomen CT and somatostatin-receptor imaging.
⚠️ Severe hypokalemic alkalosis + rapid Cushing + smoker = ectopic ACTH (SCLC), not pituitary disease.
13. ACTH-independent → adrenal CT
With suppressed ACTH, image the adrenals: unilateral adenoma (lipid-rich, low HU) vs adrenocortical carcinoma (large, irregular, high HU) vs bilateral macronodular/PPNAD hyperplasia. Treatment is adrenalectomy.
⚠️ Low-ACTH Cushing goes to adrenal CT; size/HU separate benign adenoma from carcinoma.
14. Adrenalectomy → transient AI
After removing a cortisol-secreting adrenal tumor, the suppressed contralateral adrenal/HPA axis cannot respond, so post-operative adrenal insufficiency is expected — give stress-dose glucocorticoid replacement and taper.
⚠️ Expect post-adrenalectomy adrenal insufficiency from a chronically suppressed axis — cover with steroids, don't withhold.
15. Clinical pearl banner
Pearl: first PROVE hypercortisolism (two tests), then ACTH-SORT. Low ACTH = adrenal; high ACTH = pituitary vs ectopic, resolved by high-dose dex/CRH and ultimately IPSS.
⚠️ The whole algorithm: confirm cortisol first, measure ACTH second; never localize before confirming and ACTH-sorting.
