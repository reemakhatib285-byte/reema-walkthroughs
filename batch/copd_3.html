📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Tiotropium donkey (LAMA, first-line)
Tiotropium is a long-acting muscarinic antagonist (LAMA) and a foundational COPD maintenance therapy: it blocks M3 receptors → bronchodilation, reduces exacerbations and symptoms, and improves quality of life. LAMA/LABA long-acting bronchodilators are the backbone of treatment across groups.
⚠️ Maintenance bronchodilators relieve symptoms and reduce exacerbations but do NOT reverse fixed airflow obstruction or reduce mortality. Only smoking cessation and long-term oxygen (in hypoxemia) have proven mortality benefit.
2. No evidence dust mask
Generic mucolytics and dust masks are not proven disease-modifying therapy in COPD and are common distractor 'wrong answers.' Effective interventions are smoking cessation, vaccination, bronchodilators, pulmonary rehab, and LTOT when indicated.
⚠️ Choosing mucolytics or a 'dust mask' as a core COPD therapy is the wrong board answer.
3. Group A — bronchodilator alone
GOLD Group A (low symptoms: mMRC 0-1 / CAT <10, AND 0-1 moderate exacerbations with no hospitalization) starts with a SINGLE long-acting bronchodilator (LAMA or LABA). The ABE grouping — driven by symptom burden and exacerbation history — selects initial inhaler therapy, not the spirometric grade.
⚠️ Initial inhaler choice is by ABE group (symptoms + exacerbations), not by GOLD 1-4 FEV1 grade.
4. Group B — LAMA+LABA
GOLD Group B (more symptoms: mMRC >=2 / CAT >=10, but still low exacerbation risk) is treated with DUAL bronchodilation: LABA + LAMA combination, which outperforms monotherapy on dyspnea and lung function.
⚠️ Do NOT add an inhaled corticosteroid first in Group B — dual LABA+LAMA bronchodilation is preferred; ICS is reserved for exacerbators/high eosinophils.
5. Group E — LAMA+LABA, add ICS if eos >=300
GOLD Group E (frequent/severe Exacerbations: >=2 moderate or >=1 hospitalization in a year) starts with LABA + LAMA; ADD an inhaled corticosteroid (triple therapy) when blood eosinophils are >= 300 cells/μL. ICS strongly favored if eos >=300 or concomitant asthma.
⚠️ Add ICS based on high eosinophils and exacerbation history — NOT as routine therapy and never as monotherapy. ICS increases PNEUMONIA risk, so it is targeted, not universal.
6. ICS never alone
Inhaled corticosteroids in COPD are ALWAYS paired with a long-acting bronchodilator (LABA+ICS or triple LABA+LAMA+ICS) — never used as monotherapy, unlike in asthma. The benefit of ICS is reducing exacerbations in the right phenotype.
⚠️ ICS monotherapy is NEVER appropriate in COPD. Also weigh the pneumonia risk of ICS against exacerbation reduction.
7. Eosinophil counting station
Blood eosinophil count is a biomarker guiding ICS use: benefit is greatest at >= 300 cells/μL, intermediate 100-300, and little benefit <100 cells/μL. It helps predict who will respond to inhaled steroids and who is at higher pneumonia risk for marginal gain.
⚠️ Low eosinophils (<100) predict POOR ICS benefit and favor de-escalation — do not add ICS reflexively in low-eosinophil patients.
8. Beta-blocker contraindication crossed
Active smoking blunts the anti-inflammatory response to inhaled corticosteroids. Smoking cessation is THE key disease-modifying step — it slows the accelerated FEV1 decline and is one of only two interventions (with LTOT in hypoxemia) shown to reduce mortality.
⚠️ ICS are less effective in current smokers; the highest-yield intervention is smoking cessation, not escalating inhalers. (Note: cardioselective beta-blockers are NOT contraindicated in COPD when otherwise indicated.)
9. Panlobular emphysema lungs (lower lobe)
Alpha-1 antitrypsin (AAT) deficiency causes PANACINAR (panlobular), LOWER-lobe predominant emphysema, classically in YOUNG (<45) non-smokers or those with disproportionate disease. AAT normally inhibits neutrophil elastase; its deficiency lets elastase destroy alveolar walls. Screen with a serum AAT level.
⚠️ Lower-lobe panacinar emphysema in a young/non-smoking patient = alpha-1, NOT typical smoking COPD (which is upper-lobe centriacinar/centrilobular). Failing to check an AAT level in early-onset emphysema is the miss.
10. Liver knight (alpha-1 liver disease)
Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency also causes LIVER disease — misfolded mutant Z protein accumulates in hepatocytes (PAS-positive, diastase-resistant globules) causing neonatal cholestasis, cirrhosis, and hepatocellular carcinoma. Lung disease comes from too little circulating AAT; liver disease from accumulation of the abnormal protein.
⚠️ Suspect alpha-1 when emphysema coexists with unexplained liver disease in a young patient. The liver damage is from protein ACCUMULATION (gain of toxicity), distinct from the lung's loss-of-function mechanism.
11. Cor pulmonale / clubbing think cancer
Severe hypoxemic COPD (FEV1 <25%, chronic low PaO2) drives pulmonary hypertension and cor pulmonale (right heart failure). Digital CLUBBING is NOT a feature of COPD itself and should prompt a search for another cause.
⚠️ Clubbing in a COPD patient is a RED FLAG for lung cancer (or bronchiectasis/ILD) — do not attribute it to COPD; image the chest.
12. Roflumilast PDE4 inhibitor
Roflumilast is an oral PDE4 inhibitor that reduces exacerbations in the CHRONIC BRONCHITIS phenotype with FEV1 < 50% and frequent exacerbations despite inhaled therapy. It raises intracellular cAMP, reducing airway inflammation.
⚠️ Roflumilast is reserved for the chronic-bronchitis/exacerbator phenotype, not all COPD. Key side effects: weight loss, nausea/diarrhea, and psychiatric effects (mood changes) — avoid in underweight or depressed patients.
13. Azithromycin triple therapy syringe
Chronic low-dose azithromycin (immunomodulatory/anti-inflammatory plus antimicrobial effect) reduces exacerbation frequency in patients STILL exacerbating on maximal inhaled (triple) therapy, especially former smokers.
⚠️ Add-on chronic azithromycin is for REFRACTORY exacerbators, not routine maintenance. Watch for QT prolongation, hearing loss, and macrolide resistance; screen for/exclude active NTM infection first.
14. Lung volume reduction surgery (LVRS)
Lung volume reduction surgery removes the most destroyed (hyperinflated) tissue to improve elastic recoil and diaphragm mechanics. Best candidates have UPPER-lobe predominant emphysema AND low baseline exercise capacity — this subgroup gets a survival and functional benefit.
⚠️ LVRS helps upper-lobe disease with poor exercise tolerance. It is HARMFUL (increased mortality) in patients with FEV1 <20% plus either homogeneous emphysema or very low DLCO — these are contraindications, not candidates.
