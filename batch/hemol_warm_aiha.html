📖 Read-through — all 14 symbols
1. Warm AIHA — 37°C
Warm autoimmune hemolytic anemia is the most common AIHA; the autoantibody is IgG binding maximally at body temperature (37°C).
⚠️ Calling it IgM/cold — warm AIHA is IgG at 37°C.
2. IgG antibodies
IgG-coated red cells are partially phagocytosed by splenic macrophages, producing spherocytes; the antibody is often anti-Rh (anti-e).
⚠️ Expecting complement-mediated intravascular lysis — warm AIHA is mainly extravascular/splenic.
3. Spleen clearance
The spleen is the site of clearance in warm AIHA and splenomegaly is common.
⚠️ Liver clearance — that is cold agglutinin disease, not warm.
4. Spherocytes
Membrane nibbling by splenic macrophages produces spherocytes; warm AIHA is spherocytic like HS but the DAT is positive.
⚠️ Reading spherocytes as hereditary spherocytosis — the DAT separates them.
5. Coombs IgG ±C3
A DAT positive for IgG (with or without C3) defines warm AIHA.
⚠️ A C3-only DAT — that is COLD agglutinin disease, not warm.
6. Steroids — first line
First-line therapy for warm AIHA is corticosteroids (prednisone ~1 mg/kg), plus folate supplementation.
⚠️ Using steroids first-line in COLD AIHA — they do not work in cold disease.
7. Rituximab
Rituximab is second-line for steroid-refractory or relapsed warm AIHA; splenectomy is another option.
8. CLL / SLE (secondary)
Secondary warm AIHA is driven by CLL, lymphoma, SLE or drugs — evaluate for an underlying cause.
9. Evans syndrome
Evans syndrome = warm AIHA combined with immune thrombocytopenia (and sometimes immune neutropenia).
10. Cold AIHA — IgM, 4°C
Cold agglutinin disease is an IgM autoantibody binding best in the cold (0–4°C), agglutinating red cells in cool peripheral blood and causing acrocyanosis.
⚠️ Calling it IgG — cold disease is IgM.
11. I antigen (anti-I)
Cold agglutinins target the I antigen (anti-I).
⚠️ Anti-i is associated with infectious mononucleosis (EBV), whereas anti-I is Mycoplasma/idiopathic — do not swap them.
12. C3 only — liver clearance
IgM fixes complement then falls off, so red cells are coated only with C3 and are cleared by the LIVER (Kupffer cells); the DAT is C3-only (IgG negative).
⚠️ Expecting splenic clearance or an IgG DAT — cold disease is hepatic clearance with a C3-only DAT.
13. Mycoplasma / EBV / Waldenström
Secondary cold disease: Mycoplasma pneumoniae (anti-I) and EBV (anti-i) post-infectious; chronic disease with Waldenström macroglobulinemia / lymphoma (monoclonal IgM).
14. Cold treatment — keep warm, rituximab, plasma exchange
Manage cold AIHA by keeping the patient WARM, treating the underlying cause (azithromycin for Mycoplasma), and giving rituximab for primary disease; plasma exchange helps acute severe cases because IgM is intravascular.
⚠️ Treating cold AIHA with steroids or splenectomy — both are ineffective because clearance is hepatic/complement-mediated; use rituximab (± the complement inhibitor sutimlimab).
