📖 Read-through — all 13 symbols
1. Resp ACIDOSIS = CO2 retention
Hypoventilation → ↑PCO2 → ↓pH. The kidney compensates by retaining HCO3.
⚠️ Respiratory acidosis is a VENTILATION problem — fix ventilation, not just oxygen.
2. Acute resp acidosis: HCO3 +1 / 10
Acute respiratory acidosis: HCO3 rises ~1 mEq/L for every 10 mmHg rise in PCO2.
⚠️ If HCO3 has risen much more than +1/10, it's chronic (or a mixed metabolic alkalosis).
3. Chronic resp acidosis: HCO3 +4 / 10
Chronic respiratory acidosis: HCO3 rises ~3.5–4 mEq/L per 10 mmHg PCO2 (full renal compensation over days).
⚠️ Chronic compensators (COPD) tolerate high PCO2 — don't over-ventilate to a ‘normal’ PCO2.
4. Max renal compensation (HCO3 ≤ 38)
Renal compensation has a ceiling — HCO3 doesn't rise indefinitely.
⚠️ An HCO3 well above the expected range signals a SECOND, metabolic alkalosis.
5. Cause — airway / ventilation failure
Upper-airway obstruction, severe asthma/COPD, or inadequate ventilation cause CO2 retention.
⚠️ Rising PCO2 in a tiring asthmatic is an ominous sign — support ventilation.
6. Cause — opioid/sedative hypoventilation
CNS depressants (opioids, benzodiazepines) blunt respiratory drive → respiratory acidosis. Reverse opioids with naloxone.
⚠️ Sleepy + slow breathing + high PCO2 → think drug-induced hypoventilation.
7. Cause — obesity hypoventilation
Obesity hypoventilation syndrome → chronic CO2 retention and compensated respiratory acidosis.
⚠️ Daytime hypercapnia in an obese patient = OHS, not just OSA.
8. Cause — COPD / OSA (chronic)
COPD and obstructive sleep apnea are classic chronic CO2 retainers with elevated baseline HCO3.
⚠️ Their baseline high HCO3 reflects chronic compensation — don't mistake it for primary alkalosis.
9. Resp ALKALOSIS = hyperventilation
Hyperventilation → ↓PCO2 → ↑pH. The kidney compensates by excreting HCO3.
⚠️ Tachypnea isn't always anxiety — rule out hypoxia, PE, and sepsis first.
10. Acute resp alkalosis: HCO3 −2 / 10
Acute respiratory alkalosis: HCO3 falls ~2 mEq/L per 10 mmHg drop in PCO2.
⚠️ A bigger HCO3 fall than −2/10 means chronicity or a coexisting metabolic acidosis.
11. Chronic resp alkalosis: HCO3 −4 to −5 / 10
Chronic respiratory alkalosis: HCO3 falls ~4–5 mEq/L per 10 mmHg PCO2 (e.g. high altitude, pregnancy).
⚠️ Pregnancy is a normal CHRONIC respiratory alkalosis — don't over-investigate it.
12. Causes — anxiety, pain, fever, pregnancy
Anxiety/panic, pain, fever, early sepsis, hypoxia/PE, pregnancy and liver failure all drive hyperventilation.
⚠️ Hyperventilation + hypoxia = consider PE before settling on anxiety.
13. Salicylate toxicity (mixed)
Salicylates cause an EARLY respiratory alkalosis PLUS a high anion-gap metabolic acidosis — a classic mixed disorder. Treat with urine alkalinization ± dialysis.
⚠️ Don't call salicylate poisoning a pure respiratory alkalosis — look for the coexisting high-AG acidosis.
